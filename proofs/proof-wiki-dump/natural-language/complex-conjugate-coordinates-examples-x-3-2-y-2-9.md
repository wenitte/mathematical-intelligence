Example of Complex Conjugate Coordinates
The equation of the circle:

$\paren {x - 3}^2 + y^2 = 9$
can be expressed in complex conjugate coordinates as:

$\paren {z - 3} \paren {\overline z - 3} = 9$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {x - 3}^2 + y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {\paren {x - 3} + i y} \paren {\paren {x - 3} - i y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {\paren {x + i y} - 3} \paren {\paren {x - i y} - 3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {z - 3} \paren {\overline z - 3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)





as $z = x + i y$, $\overline z = x - i y$



$\blacksquare$
