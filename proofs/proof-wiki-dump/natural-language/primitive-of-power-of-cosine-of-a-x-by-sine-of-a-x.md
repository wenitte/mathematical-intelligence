Theorem
$\ds \int \cos^n a x \sin a x \rd x = \frac {-\cos^{n + 1} a x} {\paren {n + 1} a} + C$
for $n \ne -1$.


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos a x\)





Werner Formula for Sine by Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a \sin a x\)





Primitive of $\cos a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \cos^n a x \sin a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {-z^n \rd x} a\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-z^{n + 1} } {\paren {n + 1} a} + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos^{n + 1} a x} {\paren {n + 1} a} + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$


Also see
Primitive of $\sin^n a x \cos a x$
For $n = -1$, use Primitive of $\dfrac {\sin a x} {\cos a x}$.
