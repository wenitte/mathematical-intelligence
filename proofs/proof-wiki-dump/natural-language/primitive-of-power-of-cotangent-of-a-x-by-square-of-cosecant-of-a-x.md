Theorem
$\ds \int \cot^n a x \csc^2 a x \rd x = \frac {-\cot^{n + 1} a x} {\paren {n + 1} a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cot a x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a \csc^2 a x\)





Derivative of $\cot a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \cot^n a x \csc^2 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {-1} a z^n \rd z\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} a \frac {z^{n + 1} } {n + 1}\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cot^{n + 1} a x} {\paren {n + 1} a} + C\)





substituting for $z$ and simplifying



$\blacksquare$
