Example of Area of Triangle in Determinant Form
Let $T$ be a triangle embedded in the complex plane with vertices at $\paren {-4 - i}, \paren {1 + 2 i}, \paren {4 - 3 i}$.
The area of $T$ is given by:

$\map \Area T = 17$


Proof
From Area of Triangle in Determinant Form:
$\map \Area T = \dfrac 1 2 \size {\paren {\begin{vmatrix}
-4 & -1 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & 1 \\
4 & -3 & 1 \\
\end{vmatrix} } }$















\(\ds \map \Area T\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 \size {\paren {\begin{vmatrix}
-4 & -1 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & 1 \\
4 & -3 & 1 \\
\end{vmatrix} } }\)





















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 \size {\paren {\paren {-4} \times 2 - \paren {-1} \times 1} - \paren {\paren {-4} \times \paren {-3} - 4 \times \paren {-1} } + \paren {1 \times \paren {-3} - 2 \times 4} }\)





Definition of Determinant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 \size {\paren {-8 - \paren {-1} } - \paren {12 - \paren {-4} } + \paren {-3 - 8} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 \size {\paren {-7} - \paren {16} + \paren {-11} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 \size {-34}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 17\)









$\blacksquare$
