Theorem
$\cos 30 \degrees = \cos \dfrac \pi 6 = \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2$
where $\cos$ denotes the cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \paren {\cos 30 \degrees}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \paren {\sin 30 \degrees}^2\)





Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \paren {\frac 1 2}^2\)





Sine of $30 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 3 4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cos 30 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\frac 3 4}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2\)





positive because $\cos 30 \degrees$ is in Quadrant I



$\blacksquare$
