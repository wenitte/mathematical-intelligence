Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a ring with unity.
Let $J$ be an ideal of $R$.
If $J$ contains a unit of $R$, then $J = R$.


Corollary
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a ring with unity.

Let $J$ be an ideal of $R$.
If $J$ contains the unity of $R$, then $J = R$.


Proof
Let $u \in J$, where $u \in U_R$.
Also by definition, we have $u^{-1} \in U_R$.
Let $x \in R$.














\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds x \in R\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds x \circ u^{-1} \in R\)





as $R$ is closed














\(\ds \)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds \paren {x \circ u^{-1} } \circ u \in J\)





Definition of Ideal of Ring














\(\ds \)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds x \in J\)





Ring properties: $u \circ u^{-1} = 1_R$




Thus $R \subseteq J$.
As $J \subseteq R$ by definition, it follows that $J = R$.
$\blacksquare$
