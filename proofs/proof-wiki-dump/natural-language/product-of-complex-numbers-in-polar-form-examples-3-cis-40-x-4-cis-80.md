Example of Use of Product of Complex Numbers in Polar Form
$3 \cis 40 \degrees \times 4 \cis 80 \degrees = -6 + 6 \sqrt 3 i$


Proof













\(\ds 3 \cis 40 \degrees \times 4 \cis 80 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {3 \times 4} \map \cis {40 \degrees + 80 \degrees}\)





Product of Complex Numbers in Polar Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \cis 120 \degrees\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \paren {\cos 120 \degrees + i \sin 120 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \times \paren {-\dfrac 1 2} + 12 i \paren {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2}\)





Cosine of $120 \degrees$, Sine of $120 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -6 + 6 \sqrt 3 i\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
