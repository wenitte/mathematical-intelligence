Examples of Use of Laplace Transform of Integral
Let $\laptrans f$ denote the Laplace transform of the real function $f$.


$\ds \laptrans {\int_0^1 \sin 2 u \rd u} = \dfrac 2 {s \paren {s^2 + 4} }$


Proof













\(\ds \laptrans {\int_0^1 \sin 2 u \rd u}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 s \laptrans {\sin 2 t}\)





Laplace Transform of Integral














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {s \paren {s^2 + 2^2} }\)





Laplace Transform of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {s \paren {s^2 + 4} }\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
