Examples of Use of Product Rule for Derivatives
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {x \sin x} = x \cos x + \sin x$


Proof
Let $u = x$.
Let $v = \sin x$.
Thus we have:

$y = u v$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds v \dfrac {\d u} {\d x} + u \dfrac {\d v} {\d x}\)





Product Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin x \cdot 1 + x \cdot \paren {\cos x}\)





Power Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Sine Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \cos x + \sin x\)





simplification and rearrangement



$\blacksquare$
