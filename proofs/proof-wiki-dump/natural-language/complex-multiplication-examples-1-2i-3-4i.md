Example of Complex Multiplication
$\paren {1 + 2 i} \paren {3 + 4 i} = -5 + 10 i$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {1 + 2 i} \paren {3 + 4 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {1 \times 3 - 2 \times 4} + \paren {1 \times 4 + 2 \times 3} i\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {3 - 8} + \paren {4 + 6} i\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -5 + 10 i\)









$\blacksquare$
