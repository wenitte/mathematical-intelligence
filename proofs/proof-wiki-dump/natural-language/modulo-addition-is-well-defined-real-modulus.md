Theorem
Let $z \in \R$ be a real number.
Let:

$a \equiv b \pmod z$
and:

$x \equiv y \pmod z$
where $a, b, x, y \in \R$.

Then:

$a + x \equiv b + y \pmod z$


Proof













\(\ds a\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds b\)

\(\ds \pmod z\)


















\(\ds c\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds d\)

\(\ds \pmod z\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a \bmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b \bmod z\)





Definition of Congruence














\(\ds x \bmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y \bmod z\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists k_1 \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds a - b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k_1 z\)
















\(\ds \exists k_2 \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds x - y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k_2 z\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {a + x} - \paren {b + y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {k_1 + k_2} z\)





Definition of Integer Addition








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a + x\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds b + y\)

\(\ds \pmod z\)



Definition of Congruence



$\blacksquare$
