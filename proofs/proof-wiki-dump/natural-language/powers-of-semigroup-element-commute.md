Theorem
Let $\struct {S, \odot}$ be a semigroup.
Let $a \in S$.
Let $m, n \in \Z_{>0}$.
Then:

$\forall m, n \in \Z_{>0}: a^n \odot a^m = a^m \odot a^n$


Proof













\(\ds a^n \odot a^m\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{n + m}\)





Index Laws for Semigroup: Sum of Indices














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{m + n}\)





Integer Addition is Commutative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^m \odot a^n\)





Index Laws for Semigroup: Sum of Indices



$\blacksquare$
