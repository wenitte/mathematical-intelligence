Theorem
Let $\cosh: \C \to \C$ be the hyperbolic cosine function on the set of complex numbers.

Then $\cosh$ is even:

$\map \cosh {-x} = \cosh x$


Proof 1













\(\ds \map \cosh {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{-x} + e^{-\paren {-x} } } 2\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{-x} + e^x} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^x + e^{-x} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh x\)









$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \map \cosh {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {-i x}\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {i x}\)





Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh x\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
