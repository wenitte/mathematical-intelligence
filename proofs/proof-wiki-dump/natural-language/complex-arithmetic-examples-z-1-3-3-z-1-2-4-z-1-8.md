Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 2 + i$.
Then:

$z_1^3 - 3 z_1^2 + 4 z_1 - 8 = -7 + 3 i$


Proof













\(\ds z_1^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + i} \paren {2 + i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 + 4 i + i^2\)





Definition of Complex Multiplication














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 + 4 i\)
























\(\ds z_1^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z_1^2 \times z_1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {3 + 4 i} \paren {2 + i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 + 8 i + 3 i + 4 i^2\)





Definition of Complex Multiplication














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + 11 i\)










So:














\(\ds z_1^3 - 3 z_1^2 + 4 z_1 - 8\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + 11 i} - 3 \paren {3 + 4 i} + 4 \paren {2 + i} - 8\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + 11 i} - \paren {9 + 12 i} + \paren {8 + 4 4} - 8\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 - 9 + 8 - 8} + \paren {11 - 12 + 4} i\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -7 + 3 i\)









$\blacksquare$
