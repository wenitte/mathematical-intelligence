Theorem
$\ds \int_0^\infty \frac {\cos p x - \cos q x} {x^2} \rd x = \frac {\pi \paren {\size q - \size p} } 2$
where $p, q$ are real numbers.


Proof













\(\ds \int_0^\infty \frac {\cos p x - \cos q x} {x^2} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^\infty \frac {1 - \cos q x - \paren {1 - \cos p x} } {x^2} \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^\infty \frac {1 - \cos q x} {x^2} \rd x - \int_0^\infty \frac {1 - \cos p x} {x^2} \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac \pi 2 \size q - \frac \pi 2 \size p\)





Integral to Infinity of $\dfrac {1 - \cos p x} {x^2}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\pi \paren {\size q - \size p} } 2\)









$\blacksquare$
