Theorem
Let $\struct {X, \norm {\, \cdot \,} }$ be a normed vector space.
Let $d$ be the metric induced by $\norm {\, \cdot \,}$.

Then $d$ is invariant.


Proof
Let $x, y, z \in X$. 
Then, we have: 














\(\ds \map d {x + z, y + z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {\paren {x + z} - \paren {y + z} }\)





Definition of Metric Induced by Norm














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {x - y + z - z}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {x - y}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map d {x, y}\)





Definition of Metric Induced by Norm



$\blacksquare$
