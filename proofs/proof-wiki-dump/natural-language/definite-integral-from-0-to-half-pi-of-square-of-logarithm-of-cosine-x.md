Theorem
$\ds \int_0^{\pi/2} \paren {\map \ln {\cos x} }^2 \rd x = \frac \pi 2 \paren {\ln 2}^2 + \frac {\pi^3} {24}$


Proof













\(\ds \int_0^{\pi/2} \paren {\map \ln {\cos x} }^2 \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^{\pi/2} \paren {\map \ln {\map \cos {\frac \pi 2 - x} } }^2 \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^{\pi/2} \paren {\map \ln {\sin x} }^2 \rd x\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac \pi 2 \paren {\ln 2}^2 + \frac {\pi^3} {24}\)





Definite Integral from $0$ to $\dfrac \pi 2$ of $\paren {\map \ln {\sin x} }^2$



$\blacksquare$
