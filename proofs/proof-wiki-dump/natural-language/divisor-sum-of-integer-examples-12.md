Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {12} = 28$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$12 = 2^2 \times 3$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {12}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2^3 - 1} {2 - 1} \times \paren {3 + 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 7 1 \times 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7 \times 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 28\)









$\blacksquare$
