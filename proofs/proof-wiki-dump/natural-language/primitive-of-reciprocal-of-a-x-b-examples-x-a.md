Example of Use of Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {a x + b}$
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {x - a} = \ln \size {x - a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {a x + b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {a x + b} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {a x + b}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {x - a} + C\)





substituting $a \gets 1$, $b \gets -a$



$\blacksquare$
