Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a ring with unity.
Let $a \in R$.

Let $\ideal a$ be the principal ideal of $R$ generated by $a$.

Then $\ideal a$ is an ideal of $R$.


Proof
First we establish that $\ideal a$ is an ideal of $R$, by verifying the conditions of Test for Ideal.

$\ideal a \ne \O$, as $1_R \circ a = a \in \ideal a$.

Let $x, y \in \ideal a$.
Then:










\(\ds \exists r, s \in R: \, \)



\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r \circ a, y = s \circ a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x + \paren {-y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r \circ a + \paren {-s \circ a}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r + \paren {-s} } \circ a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x + \paren {-y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \ideal a\)










Let $s \in \ideal a, x \in R$.














\(\ds s\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \ideal a, x \in R\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists r \in R: \, \)



\(\ds s\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r \circ a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x \circ s\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \circ r \circ a\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \ideal a\)










and similarly $s \circ x \in \ideal a$.

Thus by Test for Ideal, $\ideal a$ is an ideal of $R$.
$\blacksquare$
