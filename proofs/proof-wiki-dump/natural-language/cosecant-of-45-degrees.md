Theorem
$\csc 45^\circ = \csc \dfrac \pi 4 = \sqrt 2$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.


Proof













\(\ds \csc 45^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\sin 45^\circ}\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac {\sqrt 2} 2}\)





Sine of 45 Degrees














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 2\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 \sqrt 2$



$\blacksquare$
