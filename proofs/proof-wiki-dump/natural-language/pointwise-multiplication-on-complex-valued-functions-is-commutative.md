Definition
Let $S$ be a non-empty set.
Let $f, g: S \to \C$ be complex-valued functions.
Let $f \times g: S \to \C$ denote the pointwise product of $f$ and $g$.

Then:

$f \times g = g \times f$

That is, pointwise multiplication of complex-valued functions is commutative.


Proof









\(\ds \forall x \in S: \, \)



\(\ds \map {\paren {f \times g} } x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map f x \times \map g x\)





Definition of Pointwise Multiplication of Complex-Valued Functions














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map g x \times \map f x\)





Complex Multiplication is Commutative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\paren {g \times f} } x\)





Definition of Pointwise Multiplication of Complex-Valued Functions



$\blacksquare$
