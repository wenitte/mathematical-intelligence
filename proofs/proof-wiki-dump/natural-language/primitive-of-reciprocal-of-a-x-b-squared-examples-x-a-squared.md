Example of Use of Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {\paren {a x + b}^2}$
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\paren {x - a}^2} = -\frac 1 {\paren {x - a} } + C$


Proof













\(\ds \ds \int \frac {\d x} {\paren {a x + b}^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 {a \paren {a x + b} } + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {\paren {a x + b}^2}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 {\paren {x - a} } + C\)





substituting $a \gets 1$, $b \gets -a$



$\blacksquare$
