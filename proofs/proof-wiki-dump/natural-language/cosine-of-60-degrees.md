Theorem
$\cos 60 \degrees = \cos \dfrac \pi 3 = \dfrac 1 2$
where $\cos$ denotes the cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 60 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {90 \degrees - 30 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin 30 \degrees\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 2\)





Sine of $30 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
