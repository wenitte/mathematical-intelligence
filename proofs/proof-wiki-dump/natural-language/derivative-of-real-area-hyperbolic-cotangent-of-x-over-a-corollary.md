Theorem
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {x + a} {x - a} } } = \dfrac 1 {a^2 - x^2}$
where $\size x > a$.


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \map \arcoth {\frac x a}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \cdot \dfrac 1 2 \map \ln {\dfrac {\frac x a + 1} {\frac x a - 1} }\)





Definition of Real Area Hyperbolic Cotangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {x + a} {x - a} }\)





multiplying top and bottom of argument by $a$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {x + a} {x - a} } }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 a \map \arcoth {\frac x a} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \cdot \dfrac {-a} {x^2 - a^2}\)





Derivative of Real Area Hyperbolic Cotangent of x over a














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {a^2 - x^2}\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
