Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 1 - i$ and $z_2 = -2 + 4 i$.
Then:

$\cmod {z_1 \overline {z_2} + z_2 \overline {z_1} } = 12$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {z_1 \overline {z_2} + z_2 \overline {z_1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\paren {1 - i} \paren {-2 - 4 i} + \paren {-2 + 4 i} \paren {1 + i} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\paren {-2 - 4 i + 2 i + 4 i^2} + \paren {-2 - 2 i + 4 i + 4 i^2} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\paren {-6 - 2 i} + \paren {-6 + 2 i} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {12}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12\)









$\blacksquare$
