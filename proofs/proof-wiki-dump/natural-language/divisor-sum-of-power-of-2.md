Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{>0}$ be a power of $2$.
Then:

$\map {\sigma_1} n = 2 n - 1$


Proof
Let $n = 2^k$.
Then:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {2^{k + 1} - 1} {2 - 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Power of Prime














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 2^k - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 n - 1\)









$\blacksquare$
