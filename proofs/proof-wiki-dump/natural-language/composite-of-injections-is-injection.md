Theorem
A composite of injections is an injection.

That is:

If $f$ and $g$ are injections, then so is $f \circ g$.


Proof
Let $f$ and $g$ be injections.
Then:














\(\ds \map {f \circ g} x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {f \circ g} y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map f {\map g x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map f {\map g y}\)





Definition of Composition of Mappings








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map g x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map g y\)





as $f$ is injective








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)





as $g$ is injective



$\blacksquare$
