Theorem
Let $j, k \in \Z$ be integers such that $k > 0$.
Then there exist $q \in \Z$ such that $j - q k > 0$.


Proof
Let $q = -\size j - 1$.
Then:














\(\ds j - q k\)

\(=\)







\(\ds j - \paren {-\size j - 1} k\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds j + \size j + k\)









We have that:

$\forall j \le 0: j + \size j = 0$
and:

$\forall j > 0: j + \size j = 2 j$
So:

$j - q k \ge k$
and as $k > 0$ the result follows.
$\blacksquare$
