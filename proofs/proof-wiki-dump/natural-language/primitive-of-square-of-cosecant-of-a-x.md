Theorem
$\ds \int \csc^2 a x \rd x = -\frac {\cot a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \csc^2 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot x + C\)





Primitive of $\csc^2 x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \csc^2 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {-\cot a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\cot a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
