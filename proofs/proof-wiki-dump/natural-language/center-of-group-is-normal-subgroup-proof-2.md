Theorem
Let $G$ be a group
The center $\map Z G$ of $G$ is a normal subgroup of $G$.


Proof
We have:

$\forall a \in G: x \in \map Z G^a \iff a x a^{-1} = x a a^{-1} = x \in \map Z G$
Therefore:

$\forall a \in G: \map Z G^a = \map Z G$
and $\map Z G$ is a normal subgroup of $G$.
$\blacksquare$
