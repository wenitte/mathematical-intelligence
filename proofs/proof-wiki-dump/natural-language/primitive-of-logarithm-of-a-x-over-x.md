Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\ln a x} x \rd x = \frac {\paren {\ln a x}^2} 2 + C$


Proof
Let $z = a x$.














\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \d z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \rd x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\ln a x} x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\ln z} {z / a} \dfrac {\rd z} a\)





Integration by Substitution: $z = a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\ln z \rd z} z\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {\ln z}^2} 2 + C\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {\ln a x}^2} 2 + C\)









$\blacksquare$
