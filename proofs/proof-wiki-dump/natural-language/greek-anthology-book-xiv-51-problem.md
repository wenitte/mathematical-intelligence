Problem
$A$. I have what the second has and the third of what the third has.
$B$. I have what the third has and the third of what the first has.
$C$. And I have ten minae and the third of what the second has.


Solution
Let $a$, $b$ and $c$ (respectively) be what $A$, $B$ and $C$ have in minae.
We have:














\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b + \frac c 3\)




















\(\ds b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds c + \frac a 3\)




















\(\ds c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 + \frac b 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3 a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 b + c\)





multiplying through by $3$














\(\ds 3 b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 c + a\)




















\(\ds 3 c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 30 + b\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3 b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 30 + b + a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 b - 30\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3 \paren {2 b - 30}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 b + 10 + \frac b 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 18 b - 270\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9 b + 30 + b\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 8 b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 300\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {300} 8\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 37 \frac 1 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \paren {37 \frac 1 2} - 30\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 75 - 30\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 45\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 + \frac 1 3 \times {37 \frac 1 2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 + \frac 1 2 \frac {75} 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 + \frac {25} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 22 \frac 1 2\)









So:

$A$ has $45$ minae
$B$ has $37 \frac 1 2$ minae
$C$ has $22 \frac 1 2$ minae.
$\blacksquare$
