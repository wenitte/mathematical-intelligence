Theorem
Let $\sech: \C \to \C$ be the hyperbolic secant function on the set of complex numbers.

Then $\sech$ is even:

$\map \sech {-x} = \sech x$


Proof 1













\(\ds \map \sech {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\map \cosh {-x} }\)





Definition 2 of Hyperbolic Secant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cosh x}\)





Hyperbolic Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sech x\)









$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \sech \paren {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cosh \paren {-x} }\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Secant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos \paren {-i x} }\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos \paren {i x} }\)





Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cosh x}\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sech x\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Secant



$\blacksquare$
