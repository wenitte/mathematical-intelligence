Theorem
Let $c \in \Z$ be an integer.
Let $f_c: \Z \to \Z$ be the mapping defined as:

$\forall n \in \Z: \map {f_c} n = n^c$
Then $f_c$ is completely multiplicative.


Proof
Let $r, s \in \Z$.
Then:














\(\ds \map {f_c} {r s}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r s}^c\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^c s^c\)





Product of Powers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {f_c} r \map {f_c} s\)









$\blacksquare$
