Example of Cyclic Permutation of Kaprekar Number
$2727$ is a cyclic permutation of the $4$-digit Kaprekar number $7272$.
Thus we have:














\(\ds 2727^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7 \, 436 \, 529\)




















\(\ds 743 + 6529\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7272\)









and it is seen that $7272$ is a (trivial) cyclic permutation of $7272$.
$\blacksquare$
