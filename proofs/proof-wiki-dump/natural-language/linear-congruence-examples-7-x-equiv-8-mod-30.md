Example of Linear Congruence
The linear congruence:

$7 x \equiv 8 \pmod {30}$
has the unique solution:

$x = 14$


Proof
We have that:

$\gcd \set {7, 30} = 1$
Hence by Solution of Linear Congruence, $7 x \equiv 8 \pmod {30}$ has $1$ solution in the integers, given by:
which, after working, gives us:

$x = 14$
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
