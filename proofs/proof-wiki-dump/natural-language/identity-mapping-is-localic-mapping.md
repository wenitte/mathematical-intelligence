Theorem
Let $L = \struct {S, \preceq}$ be a locale.
Let $\operatorname{id}_S : S \to S$ be the identity mapping on $S$.

Then:

$\operatorname{id}_S : L \to L$ is a localic mapping.


Proof
By definition of localic mapping, we need to show that $\operatorname{id}_S$ is the upper adjoint of a Galois connection where  the lower adjoint is a frame homomorphism of $L$ to $L$.

From Identity Mapping forms Galois Connection:

$\tuple{\operatorname{id}_S, \operatorname{id}_S}$ is a Galois connection

From Identity Mapping is Frame Homomorphism

$\operatorname{id}_S$ is a frame homomorphism of $L$ to $L$

The result follows.
$\blacksquare$
