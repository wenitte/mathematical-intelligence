Theorem
Let $a, b \in \R_{>0}$ be (strictly) positive real numbers.
Let $n \in \Z_{>0}$ be a strictly positive integer

Then:

$\sqrt [n] {\dfrac a b} = \dfrac {\sqrt [n] a} {\sqrt [n] b}$

where $\sqrt [n] {}$ denotes the $n$th root.


Proof













\(\ds \sqrt [n] {\dfrac a b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\dfrac a b}^{1 / n}\)





Definition of $n$th Root














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a \times \dfrac 1 b}^{1 / n}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{1 / n} \times \paren {\dfrac 1 b}^{1 / n}\)





Power of Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{1 / n} \times b^{-1 / n}\)





Exponent Combination Laws/Negative Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{1 / n} \times \dfrac 1 {b^{1 / n} }\)





Exponent Combination Laws/Negative Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt [n] a \times \dfrac 1 {\sqrt [n] b}\)





Definition of $n$th Root














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt [n] a} {\sqrt [n] b}\)









$\blacksquare$
