Theorem
$\csc 60^\circ = \csc \dfrac \pi 3 = \dfrac {2 \sqrt 3} 3$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.


Proof













\(\ds \csc 60^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\sin 60^\circ}\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac {\sqrt 3} 2}\)





Sine of 60 Degrees














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 \sqrt 3} 3\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 \sqrt 3$



$\blacksquare$
