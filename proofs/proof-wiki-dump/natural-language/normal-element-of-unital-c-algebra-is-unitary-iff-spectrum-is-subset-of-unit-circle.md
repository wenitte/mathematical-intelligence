Theorem
Let $\struct {A, \ast, \norm {\, \cdot \,} }$ be a unital $\text C^\ast$-algebra.
Let $x \in A$ be normal. 
Let $\map {\sigma_A} x$ denote the spectrum of $x$ in $A$.

Then $x$ is unitary if and only if:

$\map {\sigma_A} x \subseteq \set {z \in \C : \cmod z = 1}$


Proof
Necessary Condition
Let $A' \subseteq A$ be the $\text C^\ast$-algebra generated by $\set { {\mathbf 1}_A, x}$. 
By C*-Algebra Generated by Commutative Self-Adjoint Set is Commutative, $A'$ is commutative.
Let $\Phi_{A'}$ be the spectrum of $A'$.
From Spectrum of Element of Unital C*-Subalgebra of Unital C*-Algebra, we have:

$\map {\sigma_A} x = \map {\sigma_{A'} } x$
From Spectrum of Element of Unital Commutative Banach Algebra, we have $\map {\sigma_A} x = \map {\sigma_{A'} } x = \set {\map \phi x : \phi \in \Phi_{A'} }$. 
From Character on Unital C*-Algebra has Modulus One at Unitary Elements, we have $\cmod {\map \phi x} = 1$ for each $\phi \in \Phi_{A'}$.
Hence $\map {\sigma_A} x \subseteq \set {z \in \C : \cmod z = 1}$. 
$\Box$


Sufficient Condition
Suppose that $x \in A$ has:

$\map {\sigma_A} x \subseteq \set {z \in \C : \cmod z = 1}$
Let $\Theta_x$ be the continuous functional calculus for $x$.
Let $\iota : \map {\sigma_A} x \to \C$ be the inclusion mapping.
Since $\map {\sigma_A} x \subseteq \set {z \in \C : \cmod z = 1}$, we have:

$z \overline z = \cmod z^2 = 1$ for each $z \in \map {\sigma_A} x$
from Product of Complex Number with Conjugate. 
Hence we have $\iota \overline \iota = 1$. 
We therefore have: 














\(\ds x^\ast x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\map {\Theta_x} \iota}^\ast \map {\Theta_x} \iota\)





Definition of Continuous Functional Calculus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\Theta_x} {\overline \iota} \map {\Theta_x} \iota\)





$\Theta_x$ is a $\ast$-algebra homomorphism














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\Theta_x} {\overline \iota \iota}\)





$\Theta_x$ is an algebra homomorphism














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\Theta_x} 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds {\mathbf 1}_A\)





$\Theta_x$ is unital



Since $x$ is normal, we also have:

$x x^\ast = {\mathbf 1}_A$
Hence $x$ is unitary.
$\blacksquare$
