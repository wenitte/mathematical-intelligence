Theorem
Let $\mathbf a$, $\mathbf b$ and $\mathbf c$ be differentiable vector-valued functions in Cartesian $3$-space.

The derivative of their vector triple product is given by:

$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\mathbf a \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} } = \dfrac {\d \mathbf a} {\d x} \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \paren {\dfrac {\d \mathbf b} {\d x} \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \dfrac {\d \mathbf c} {\d x} }$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\mathbf a \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d \mathbf a} {\d x} \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c}\)





Derivative of Vector Cross Product of Vector-Valued Functions














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d \mathbf a} {\d x} \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \paren {\dfrac {\d \mathbf b} {\d x} \times \mathbf c + \mathbf b \times \dfrac {\d \mathbf c} {\d x} }\)





Derivative of Vector Cross Product of Vector-Valued Functions














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d \mathbf a} {\d x} \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \paren {\dfrac {\d \mathbf b} {\d x} \times \mathbf c} + \mathbf a \times \paren {\mathbf b \times \dfrac {\d \mathbf c} {\d x} }\)





Vector Cross Product Distributes over Addition



$\blacksquare$
