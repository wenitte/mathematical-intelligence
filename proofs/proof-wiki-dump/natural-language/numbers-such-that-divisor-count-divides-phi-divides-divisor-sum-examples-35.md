Examples of Numbers such that Divisor Count divides Phi divides Divisor Sum
The number $35$ has the property that:

$\map {\sigma_0} {35} \divides \map \phi {35} \divides \map {\sigma_1} {35}$
where:

$\divides$ denotes divisibility
$\sigma_0$ denotes the divisor count function
$\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ (phi) function
$\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {35}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 4 \, \)





\(\ds \)





$\sigma_0$ of $35$














\(\ds \map \phi {35}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 24 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 6 \times 4\)





$\phi$ of $35$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {35}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 48 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 2 \times 24\)





$\sigma_1$ of $35$



$\blacksquare$
