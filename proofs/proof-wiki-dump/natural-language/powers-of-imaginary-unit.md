Theorem
The (integer) powers of the imaginary unit $i$ are:














\(\ds i^0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)




















\(\ds i^1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i\)




















\(\ds i^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1\)




















\(\ds i^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -i\)




















\(\ds i^4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









$\blacksquare$


Proof
By definition:

$i^2 = -1$

Then we have:














\(\ds i^0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{0 \ln i}\)





Definition of Power to Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^0\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)










Then:














\(\ds i^1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{1 \ln i}\)





Definition of Power to Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{\ln i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds i\)





Definition of Exponential Function




Finally:














\(\ds i^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i^2 \times i\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {-1} \times i\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -i\)





Definition of Negative of Complex Number



$\blacksquare$
