Theorem
$\ds \int \csc^2 x \rd x = -\cot x + C$
where $C$ is an arbitrary constant.


Proof
From Derivative of Cotangent Function:

$\dfrac \d {\d x} \cot x = -\csc^2 x$
The result follows from the definition of primitive.
$\blacksquare$
