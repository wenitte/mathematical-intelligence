Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {679} = 784$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$679 = 7 \times 97$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {679}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {7 + 1} \paren {97 + 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Square-Free Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 8 \times 98\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^3 \times \paren {2 \times 7^2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^4 \times 7^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2^2 \times 7}^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 784\)









$\blacksquare$
