Theorem
Proportion is a symmetric relation.
That is:

$\forall x, y \in \R: x \propto y \implies y \propto x$


Proof
Let $x, y$ be arbitrary.
Let $x$ be proportional to $y$:

$x \propto y$
Then by definition:










\(\ds \exists k \ne 0: \, \)



\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k \times y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k^{-1} \times x\)









The result follows from the definition of symmetry and proportion.
$\blacksquare$
