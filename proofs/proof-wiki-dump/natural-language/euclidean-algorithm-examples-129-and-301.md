Examples of Use of Euclidean Algorithm
The GCD of $129$ and $301$ is found to be:

$\gcd \set {129, 301} = 43$
Hence $43$ can be expressed as an integer combination of $129$ and $301$:

$43 = 1 \times 301 - 2 \times 129$


Proof



\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 301\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 129 + 43\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 129\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 43\)









Thus:

$\gcd \set {129, 301} = 43$

Then we have:














\(\ds 43\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 301 - 2 \times 129\)





from $(1)$



$\blacksquare$
