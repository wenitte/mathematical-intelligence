Theorem
$\ds \int \dfrac {\cos a x} {\sin a x} \rd x = \dfrac 1 a \ln \size {\sin a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \dfrac {\cos a x} {\sin a x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \cot a x \rd x\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \ln \size {\sin a x} + C\)





Primitive of $\cot a x$



$\blacksquare$
