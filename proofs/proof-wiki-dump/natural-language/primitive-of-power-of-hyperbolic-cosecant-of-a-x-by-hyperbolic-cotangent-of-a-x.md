Theorem
$\ds \int \csch^n a x \coth a x \rd x = \frac {-\csch^n a x} {n a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \csch a x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a \csch a x \coth a x\)





Derivative of $\csch a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \csch^n a x \coth a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {-z^{n - 1} \rd z} a\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} a \int z^{n - 1} \rd z\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} a \frac {z^n} n\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\csch^n a x} {n a} + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
