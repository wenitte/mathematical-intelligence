Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin a x \cos a x} = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin x \cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\tan x} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {\sin x \cos x}$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin a x \cos a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
