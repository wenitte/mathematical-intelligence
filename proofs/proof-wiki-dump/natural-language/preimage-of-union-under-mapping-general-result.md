Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets.
Let $f: S \to T$ be a mapping.
Let $\powerset T$ be the power set of $T$.
Let $\mathbb T \subseteq \powerset T$.

Then:

$\ds f^{-1} \sqbrk {\bigcup \mathbb T} = \bigcup_{X \mathop \in \mathbb T} f^{-1} \sqbrk X$


Proof
As $f$, being a mapping, is also a relation, we can apply Preimage of Union under Relation: General Result:

$\ds \RR^{-1} \sqbrk {\bigcup \mathbb T} = \bigcup_{X \mathop \in \mathbb T} \RR^{-1} \sqbrk X$
$\blacksquare$
