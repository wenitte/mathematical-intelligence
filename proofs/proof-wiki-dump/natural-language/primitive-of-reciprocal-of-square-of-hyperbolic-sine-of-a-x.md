Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sinh^2 a x} = -\frac {\coth a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sinh^2 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \csch^2 a x \rd x\)





Definition 2 of Hyperbolic Cosecant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\coth a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\csch^2 a x$



$\blacksquare$
