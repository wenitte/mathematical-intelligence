Corollary to Limit at Infinity of Sine Integral Function
$\ds \lim_{x \mathop \to -\infty} \map \Si x = -\frac \pi 2$

where $\Si$ denotes the sine integral function.


Proof













\(\ds \map \Si {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map \Si x\)





Sine Integral Function is Odd














\(\ds \lim_{x \mathop \to -\infty} \map \Si x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\lim_{x \mathop \to +\infty} \map \Si x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac \pi 2\)





Limit at Infinity of Sine Integral Function



$\blacksquare$
