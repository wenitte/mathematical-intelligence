Theorem
The perimeter $C$ of a circle with radius $r$ is given by:

$C = 2 \pi r$


Proof
By definition, the perimeter is the length of the circumference of the circle.
Let $C$ be the perimeter.

Then:














\(\ds \pi\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac C {2 r}\)





Definition of $\pi$ (pi)








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds C\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \pi r\)









$\blacksquare$
