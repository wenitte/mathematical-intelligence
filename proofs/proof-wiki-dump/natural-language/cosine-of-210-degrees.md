Theorem
$\cos 210 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {7 \pi} 6 = -\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 210 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {360 \degrees - 150 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos 150 \degrees\)





Cosine of Conjugate Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\sqrt 3} 2\)





Cosine of $150 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
