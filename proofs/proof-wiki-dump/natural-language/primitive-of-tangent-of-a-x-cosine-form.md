Theorem
$\ds \int \tan a x \rd x = \frac {-\ln \size {\cos a x} } a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \tan x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\ln \size {\cos x}\)





Primitive of $\tan x$: Cosine Form








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \tan a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {-\ln \size {\cos a x} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\ln \size {\cos a x} } a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
