Theorem
Let $a \in \R_{>0}$ be a strictly positive real number.
Let $x \in \R$ be a real number.
Let $a^x$ be defined as $a$ to the power of $x$.

Then:

$a^{-x} = \dfrac 1 {a^x}$


Proof













\(\ds a^{-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp {-x \ln a}\)





Definition of Power to Real Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\map \exp {x \ln a} }^{-1}\)





Exponential of Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\map \exp {x \ln a} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a^x}\)





Definition of Power to Real Number



$\blacksquare$
