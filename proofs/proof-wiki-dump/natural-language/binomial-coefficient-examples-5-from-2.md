Example of Binomial Coefficient
$\dbinom 2 5 = 0$


Proof
From the definition of binomial coefficient:

$\dbinom n k = \begin{cases}
\dfrac {n!} {k! \paren {n - k}!} & : 0 \le k \le n \\
& \\
0 & : \text { otherwise } \end{cases}$
Here we have $k = 5$ and $n = 2$.
As $k > n$:

$\dbinom 2 5 = 0$
$\blacksquare$
