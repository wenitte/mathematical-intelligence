Theorem
Let $x \in \R$ be a real number.

$x^{\underline 1} = x$
where $x^{\underline 1}$ denotes the falling factorial.


Proof













\(\ds x^{\underline 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\Gamma \left({x + 1}\right)} {\Gamma \left({x - 1 + 1}\right)}\)





Falling Factorial as Quotient of Factorials














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\Gamma \left({x + 1}\right)} {\Gamma \left({x}\right)}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Gamma Difference Equation



$\blacksquare$
