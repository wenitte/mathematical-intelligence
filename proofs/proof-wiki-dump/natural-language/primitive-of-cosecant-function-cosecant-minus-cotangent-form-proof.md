Theorem
$\ds \int \csc x \rd x = \ln \size {\csc x - \cot x} + C$
where $\csc x - \cot x \ne 0$.


Proof













\(\ds \int \csc x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\tan \frac x 2} + C\)





Primitive of $\csc x$: Tangent Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\frac {1 - \cos x} {\sin x} } + C\)





Half Angle Formula for Tangent: Corollary $2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\frac 1 {\sin x} - \frac {\cos x} {\sin x} } + C\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\csc x - \cot x} + C\)





Definition of Cosecant and Definition of Cotangent



$\blacksquare$
