Theorem
Let $r, s \in \R_{> 0}$ be (strictly) positive real numbers.
Let $x \in \Q$ be a rational number.
Let $r^x$ be defined as $r$ to the power of $x$.
Then:

$\paren {r s}^x = r^x s^x$


Proof
Let $x = \dfrac p q$ where $p, q \in \Z$ and $q > 0$.
We have:














\(\ds r^x s^x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r^p}^{1 / q} \paren {s^p}^{1 / q}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r^p s^p}^{1 / q}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {r s}^p}^{1 / q}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r s}^{p / q}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r s}^x\)









$\blacksquare$
