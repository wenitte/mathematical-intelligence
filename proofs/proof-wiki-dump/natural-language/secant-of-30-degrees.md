Theorem
$\sec 30 \degrees = \sec \dfrac \pi 6 = \dfrac {2 \sqrt 3} 3$
where $\sec$ denotes secant.


Proof













\(\ds \sec 30 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos 30 \degrees}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac {\sqrt 3} 2}\)





Cosine of $30 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 \sqrt 3} 3\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 \sqrt 3$



$\blacksquare$
