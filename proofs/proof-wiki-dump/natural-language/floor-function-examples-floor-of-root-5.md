Theorem
$\floor {\sqrt 5} = 2$
where $\floor x$ denotes the floor of $x$.


Proof
The decimal expansion of $\sqrt 5$ is:

$\sqrt 5 \approx 2 \cdotp 23606 \, 79774 \, 99789 \, 6964 \ldots$
Thus:

$2 \le \sqrt 5 < 3$
Hence $2$ is the floor of $\sqrt 5$ by definition.
$\blacksquare$
