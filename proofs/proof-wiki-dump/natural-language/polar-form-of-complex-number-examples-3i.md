Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $-3 i$ can be expressed as a complex number in polar form as $\polar {3, \dfrac {3 \pi} 2}$.


Proof
  













\(\ds \cmod {-3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\paren {-3}^2 + 0^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)










Then:














\(\ds \map \cos {\map \arg {-3 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 0 3\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\pi} 2 \text { or } \dfrac {3 \pi} 2\)





Cosine of Half-Integer Multiple of Pi


















\(\ds \map \sin {\map \arg {-3 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-3} 3\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3 \pi} 2\)





Sine of Half-Integer Multiple of Pi




Hence:

$\map \arg {-3 i} = \dfrac {3 \pi} 2$
and hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
