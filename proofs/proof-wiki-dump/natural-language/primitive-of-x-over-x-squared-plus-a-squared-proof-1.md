Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {x^2 + a^2} = \frac 1 2 \map \ln {x^2 + a^2} + C$


Proof













\(\ds u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^2 + a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x\)





Power Rule for Derivatives and Derivative of Constant








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {x^2 + a^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \ln \size {x^2 + a^2} + C\)

\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \, \map \ln {x^2 + a^2} + C\)
