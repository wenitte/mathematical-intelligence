Theorem













\(\ds \sin \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds - \sin \theta\)




















\(\ds \cos \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \theta\)




















\(\ds \tan \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds - \tan \theta\)











Proof













\(\ds \sin \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds - \sin \theta\)





Sine Function is Odd














\(\ds \cos \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \theta\)





Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \tan \left({- \theta}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds - \tan \theta\)





Tangent Function is Odd



$\blacksquare$


Comment
The concept of bagging up these identities into one, and calling it the Opposites Theorem, seems to be a modern innovation designed to ease the learning of trigonometry in schools.
