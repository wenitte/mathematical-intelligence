Example of Use of Integration by Substitution
$\ds \int \sqrt {1 - x} \rd x = -\dfrac 2 3 \paren {1 - x}^{3 / 2} + C$


Proof













\(\ds u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {1 - x}\)




















\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - u^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d x} {\d u}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2 u\)





Power Rule for Derivatives








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \sqrt {1 - x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int -2 u^2 \rd u\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 2 3 u^3 + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 2 3 \paren {1 - x}^{3 / 2} + C\)





substituting for $u$



$\blacksquare$
