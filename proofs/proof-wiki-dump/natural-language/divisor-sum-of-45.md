Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {45} = 78$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$45 = 3^2 \times 5$
Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {45}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3^3 - 1} {3 - 1} \times \frac {5^2 - 1} {5 - 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {27 - 1} 2 \times \frac {25 - 1} 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 13 \times 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 78\)









$\blacksquare$
