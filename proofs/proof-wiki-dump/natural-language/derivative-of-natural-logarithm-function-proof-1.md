Theorem
Let $\ln x$ be the natural logarithm function.
Then:

$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\ln x} = \dfrac 1 x$


Proof













\(\ds \ln x\)

\(:=\)







\(\ds \int_1^x \dfrac 1 t \rd t\)





Definition 1 of Natural Logarithm














\(\ds \frac \d {\d x} \ln x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac \d {\d x} \int_1^x \dfrac 1 t \rd t\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 x\)





Fundamental Theorem of Calculus



$\blacksquare$
