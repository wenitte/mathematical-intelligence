Theorem
$\sec 45 \degrees = \sec \dfrac \pi 4 = \sqrt 2$
where $\sec$ denotes secant.


Proof













\(\ds \sec 45 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos 45 \degrees}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac {\sqrt 2} 2}\)





Cosine of $45 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 2\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 \sqrt 2$



$\blacksquare$
