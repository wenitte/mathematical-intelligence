Theorem
Let $n$ be a $2$-digit positive integer.
Then:

$n$ equals the sum added to the product of its digits
if and only if:

the last digit of $n$ is $9$.


Proof
Let $n = 10 x + y$ where $0 < x \le 9, 0 \le y \le 9$.
Then:














\(\ds \paren {x + y} + \paren {x y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 x + y\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x y - 9 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x \paren {y - 9}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)





as $x \ne 0$



$\blacksquare$
