Theorem
$\map \cos {\dfrac {3 \pi} 2 - \theta} = -\sin \theta$
where $\cos$ and $\sin$ are cosine and sine respectively.


Proof













\(\ds \map \cos {\frac {3 \pi} 2 - \theta}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \frac {3 \pi} 2 \cos \theta + \sin \frac {3 \pi} 2 \sin \theta\)





Cosine of Difference














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 \times \cos \theta + \paren {-1} \times \sin \theta\)





Cosine of Three Right Angles and Sine of Three Right Angles














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sin \theta\)









$\blacksquare$
