Theorem
The divisibility relation is a transitive relation on $\Z$, the set of integers.
That is:

$\forall x, y, z \in \Z: x \divides y \land y \divides z \implies x \divides z$


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(\divides\)







\(\ds y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists q_1 \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds q_1 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)





Definition of Divisor of Integer














\(\ds y\)

\(\divides\)







\(\ds z\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists q_2 \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds q_2 y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z\)





Definition of Divisor of Integer








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds q_2 \paren {q_1 x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z\)





substituting for $y$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {q_2 q_1} x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z\)





Integer Multiplication is Associative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists q \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds q x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z\)





where $q = q_1 q_2$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(\divides\)







\(\ds z\)





Definition of Divisor of Integer



$\blacksquare$
