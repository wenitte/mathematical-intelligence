Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 - \cos a x} = -\frac 1 a \cot \frac {a x} 2 + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 - \cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot \frac x 2 + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {1 - \cos x}$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 - \cos a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} a \cot \frac {a x} 2 + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
