Example of Use of Quotient Rule for Derivatives
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac {\sin x} x} = \dfrac {x \cos x - \sin x} {x^2}$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac {\sin x} x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x \map {\frac \d {\d x} } {\sin x} - \sin x \map {\frac \d {\d x} } x} {x^2}\)





Quotient Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x \cdot \cos x - \sin x \cdot 1} {x^2}\)





Derivative of Sine Function, Power Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x \cos x - \sin x} {x^2}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
