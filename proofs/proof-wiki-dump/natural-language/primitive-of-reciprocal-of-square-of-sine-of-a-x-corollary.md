Corollary to Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {\sin^2 a x}$
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 x} = -\cot x + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cot a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {\sin^2 a x}$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\map \cot {1 \times x} } 1 + C\)





setting $a \gets 1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot x + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
