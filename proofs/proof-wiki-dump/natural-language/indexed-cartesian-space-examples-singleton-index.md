Example of Indexed Cartesian Space
Let $X$ be a set.
Then the indexed Cartesian space $X^{\set 1}$ is defined as:

$X^{\set 1} = \set 1 \times X$


Proof













\(\ds X^{\set 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {f: \paren {f: \set 1 \to X} \land \paren {\forall i \in \set 1: \paren {\map f i \in X} } }\)





Definition 2 of Indexed Cartesian Space














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\tuple {i, s}: i \in \set 1, s \in X}\)





Definition of Mapping














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\tuple {1, s}: s \in X}\)





Definition of Singleton














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set 1 \times X\)





Definition of Cartesian Product



$\blacksquare$
