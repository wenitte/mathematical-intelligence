Theorem
Let $S$ be a set.
Let $\RR$ be an algebra of sets on $S$.
Then:

$\forall A, B \in S: A \cap B \in \RR$


Proof
By definition $2$ of Algebra of Sets:

$\RR$ is a ring of sets with a unit.
By definition $1$ of Ring of Sets:




\((\text {RS} 2_1)\)  

$:$  



Closure under Intersection:   

  \(\ds \forall A, B \in \RR:\)

\(\ds A \cap B \in \RR \)   







  

$\blacksquare$
