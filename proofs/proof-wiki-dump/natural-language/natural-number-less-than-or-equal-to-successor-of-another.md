Theorem
Let $\N$ be the natural numbers.
Let $m, n \in \N$ such that $m \le n^+$.

Then either:

$(1): \quad m \le n$
or:

$(2): \quad m = n^+$


Proof
Let $m \le n^+$.
Suppose $m \le n$ is false.
Then:

$n^+ \le m$
and because $m \le n^+$:

$m = n^+$
$\blacksquare$
