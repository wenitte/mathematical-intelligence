Theorem
$\cosh^3 x = \dfrac {\cosh 3 x + 3 \cosh x} 4$
where $\cosh$ denotes hyperbolic cosine.


Proof 1













\(\ds \cosh 3 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \cosh^3 x - 3 \cosh x\)





Triple Angle Formula for Hyperbolic Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 4 \cosh^3 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh 3 x + 3 \cosh x\)





rearranging








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cosh^3 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\cosh 3 x + 3 \cosh x} 4\)





dividing both sides by $4$



$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \cosh^3 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {2^3} \paren {e^x + e^{-x} }^3\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 8 \paren {e^{3x} + e^{-3x} + 3e^{x} + 3e^{-x} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 4 \paren {\frac{ e^{3x} + e^{-3x} } 2} + \frac 3 4 \paren {\frac{e^{x} + e^{-x} } 2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 3x} 4 + \frac {3 \cosh x} 4\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine



$\blacksquare$


Proof 3













\(\ds \cosh^3 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos^3 i x\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\map \cos {3 i x} + 3 \cos i x} 4\)





Cube of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 3 x + 3 \cosh x} 4\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
