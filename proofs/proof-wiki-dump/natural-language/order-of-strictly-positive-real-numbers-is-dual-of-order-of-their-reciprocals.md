Theorem
$\forall x, y \in \R: x > y > 0 \implies \dfrac 1 x < \dfrac 1 y$


Proof
From Reciprocal of Strictly Positive Real Number is Strictly Positive:

$(1): \quad x > 0 \implies \dfrac 1 x > 0$
$(2): \quad y > 0 \implies \dfrac 1 y > 0$

Then:














\(\ds x\)

\(>\)







\(\ds y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x \times \frac 1 x\)

\(>\)







\(\ds y \times \frac 1 x\)





Real Number Axiom $\R \text O2$: Usual Ordering is Compatible with Multiplication and from $(1)$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x \times \frac 1 x \times \frac 1 y\)

\(>\)







\(\ds y \times \frac 1 x \times \frac 1 y\)





Real Number Axiom $\R \text O2$: Usual Ordering is Compatible with Multiplication and from $(2)$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x \times \frac 1 x} \times \frac 1 y\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \paren {y \times \frac 1 y} \times \frac 1 x\)





Real Number Axiom $\R \text M1$: Associativity of Multiplication and Real Number Axiom $\R \text M2$: Commutativity of Multiplication








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 1 \times \frac 1 y\)

\(>\)







\(\ds 1 \times \frac 1 x\)





Real Number Axiom $\R \text A4$: Inverses for Addition








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac 1 y\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \frac 1 x\)





Real Number Axiom $\R \text M3$: Identity Element for Multiplication








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac 1 x\)

\(<\)







\(\ds \frac 1 y\)





Definition of Dual Ordering



$\blacksquare$
