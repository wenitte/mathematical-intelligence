Theorem
$\ds \int \cos^2 a x \rd x = \frac x 2 + \frac {\sin 2 a x} {4 a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \cos^2 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac x 2 + \frac {\sin 2 x} 4 + C\)





Primitive of $\cos^2 x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \cos^2 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {\frac {a x} 2 + \frac {\sin 2 a x} 4} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac x 2 + \frac {\sin 2 a x} {4 a} + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
