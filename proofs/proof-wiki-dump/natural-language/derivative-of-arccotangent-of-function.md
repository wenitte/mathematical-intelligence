Theorem
Let $u$ be a differentiable real function of $x$.
Then:

$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\arccot u} = -\dfrac 1 {1 + u^2} \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}$
where $\arccot$ denotes the arccotangent of $x$.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\frac \d {\d x} } {\arccot u}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\frac \d {\d u} } {\arccot u} \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Chain Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 1 {1 + u^2} \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Arccotangent Function



$\blacksquare$
