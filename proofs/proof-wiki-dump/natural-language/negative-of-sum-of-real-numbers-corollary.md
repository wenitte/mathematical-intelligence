Theorem
$\forall x, y \in \R: -\paren {x - y} = -x + y$


Proof













\(\ds -\paren {x - y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {x + \paren {-y} }\)





Definition of Real Subtraction














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -x - \paren {-y}\)





Negative of Sum of Real Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -x + \paren {-\paren {-y} }\)





Definition of Real Subtraction














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -x + y\)





Negative of Negative Real Number



$\blacksquare$
