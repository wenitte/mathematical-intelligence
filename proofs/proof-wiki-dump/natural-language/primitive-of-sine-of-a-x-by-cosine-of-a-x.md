Theorem
$\ds \int \sin a x \cos a x \rd x = \frac {\sin^2 a x} {2 a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \sin a x \cos a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sin 2 a x} 2 \rd x\)





Double Angle Formula for Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos 2 a x} {4 a} + C\)





Primitive of $\cos a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\paren {1 - 2 \sin^2 a x} } {4 a} + C\)





Double Angle Formula for Cosine: Corollary $2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} {4 a} + \frac {\sin^2 a x} {2 a} + C\)





separating fraction














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin^2 a x} {2 a} + C\)





subsuming $\dfrac {-1} {4 a}$ into arbitrary constant



$\blacksquare$
