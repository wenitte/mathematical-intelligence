Theorem
$\csc 30^\circ = \csc \dfrac \pi 6 = 2$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.


Proof













\(\ds \csc 30^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\sin 30^\circ}\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac 1 2}\)





Sine of $30^\circ$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2$



$\blacksquare$
