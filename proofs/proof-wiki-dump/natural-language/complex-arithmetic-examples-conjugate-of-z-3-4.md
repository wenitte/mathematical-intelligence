Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z^3 = -\dfrac 1 2 + \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i$.
Then:

$\paren {\overline {z_3} }^4 = -\dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {\overline {z_3} }^4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {-\dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i}^4\)





Definition of Complex Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {-\dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i}^3 \times \paren {-\dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i}\)










But from Cube Roots of Unity:

$\paren {-\dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i}^3 = 1$

Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
