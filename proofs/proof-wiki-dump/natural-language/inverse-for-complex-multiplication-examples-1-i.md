Example of Inverse for Complex Multiplication
$\dfrac 1 {1 + i} = \dfrac {1 - i} 2$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {1 + i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {1 - i} {\paren {1 + i} \paren {1 - i} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $1 - i$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {1 - i} {1^2 + 1^2}\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {1 - i} 2\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
