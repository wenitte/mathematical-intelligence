Example of Use of General Binomial Theorem
$\paren {1 + 2 x}^{-\frac 3 2} = 1 - 3 x + \dfrac {15} 2 x^2 - \dfrac {35} 2 x^3 + \cdots$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {1 + 2 x}^{-\frac 3 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{n \mathop = 0}^\infty \frac {\paren {-\frac 3 2}^{\underline n} } {n!} \paren {2 x}^n\)





General Binomial Theorem














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + \paren {-\frac 3 2} \paren {2 x} + \dfrac {\paren {-\frac 3 2} \paren {-\frac 5 2} } {2!} \paren {2 x}^2 + \dfrac {\paren {-\frac 3 2} \paren {-\frac 5 2} \paren {-\frac 7 2} } {3!} \paren {2 x}^3 + \cdots\)





expanding term by term














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - 3 x + \dfrac {\paren {-3} \paren {-5} } {2^2 \times 2!} \paren {2 x}^2 + \dfrac {\paren {-3} \paren {-5} \paren {-7} } {2^3 \times 3!}  \paren {2 x}^3 + \cdots\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - 3 x + \dfrac {15} 2 x^2 - \dfrac {35} 2 x^3 + \cdots\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
