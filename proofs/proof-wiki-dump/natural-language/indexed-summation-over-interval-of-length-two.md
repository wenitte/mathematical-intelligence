Theorem
Let $\mathbb A$ be one of the standard number systems $\N, \Z, \Q, \R, \C$.
Let $a \in \Z$ be an integer.
Let $f: \set {a, a + 1} \to \mathbb A$ be a real-valued function.

Then the indexed summation:

$\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^{a + 1} \map f i = \map f a + \map f {a + 1}$


Proof
We have:














\(\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^{a + 1} \map f i\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^a \map f i + \map f {a + 1}\)





Definition of Indexed Summation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map f a + \map f {a + 1}\)





Indexed Summation over Interval of Length One



$\blacksquare$
