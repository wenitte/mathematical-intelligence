Theorem
Let $\struct {F, +, \times}$ be a field.
Let $a \in F$.

Then the negative $-a$ of $a$ is unique.


Proof
By definition, a field is a ring whose ring product less zero is an abelian group.
The result follows from Ring Negative is Unique.
$\blacksquare$
