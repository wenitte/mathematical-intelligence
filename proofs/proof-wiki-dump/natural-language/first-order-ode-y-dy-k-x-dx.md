Theorem
Let $k \in \R$ be a real number.
The first order ODE:

$y \rd y = k x \rd x$
has the general solution:

$y^2 = k x^2 + C$


Proof













\(\ds y \rd y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k x \rd x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int y \rd y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int k x \rd x\)





Solution to Separable Differential Equation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {y^2} 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {k x^2} 2 + C_1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k x^2 + C\)





reassigning the arbitrary constant.



$\blacksquare$
