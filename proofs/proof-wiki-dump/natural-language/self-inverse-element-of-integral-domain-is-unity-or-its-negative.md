Theorem
Let $\struct {D, +, \circ}$ be an integral domain whose zero is $0_D$ and whose unity is $1_D$.
Let $x \in D$ such that $x^2 = 1_D$.

Then either $x = 1_D$ or $x = -1_D$.


Proof













\(\ds x^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1_D\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x + 1_D} \paren {x + \paren {-1_D} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0_D\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x + 1_D\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0_D\)


















\(\, \ds \lor \, \)

\(\ds x + \paren {-1_D}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0_D\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1_D\)


















\(\, \ds \lor \, \)

\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1_D\)









$\blacksquare$
