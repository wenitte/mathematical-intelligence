Corollary to Laplace Transform of Bessel Function of the First Kind of Order Zero
Let $J_0$ denote the Bessel function of the first kind of order $0$.

Then the Laplace transform of $\map {J_0} {a t}$ is given as:

$\laptrans {\map {J_0} {a t} } = \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {s^2 + a^2} }$


Proof













\(\ds \laptrans {\map {J_0} t}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {s^2 + 1} }\)





Laplace Transform of Bessel Function of the First Kind of Order Zero








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \laptrans {\map {J_0} {a t} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {\paren {s / a}^2 + 1} }\)





Laplace Transform of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {s^2 + a^2} }\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
