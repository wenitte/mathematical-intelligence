Example of Complex Conjugate Coordinates
The equation of the straight line in the plane:

$2 x + y = 5$
can be expressed in complex conjugate coordinates as:

$\paren {2 i + 1} z + \paren {2 i - 1} \overline z = 10 i$


Proof
We have that:














\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x + i y\)




















\(\ds \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x - i y\)









and so:














\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {z + \overline z} 2\)




















\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {z - \overline z} {2 i}\)










Hence:














\(\ds 2 x + y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 \paren {\frac {z + \overline z} 2} + \paren {\frac {z - \overline z} {2 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {z + \overline z} 2 i + \paren {z - \overline z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 i z + 2 i \overline z + z - \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {2 i + 1} z + \paren {2 i - 1} \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 i\)









$\blacksquare$
