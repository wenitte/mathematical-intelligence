Example of Complex Dot Product
Let:

$z_1 = 2 + 5 i$
$z_2 = 3 - i$
Then:

$z_1 \circ z_2 = 1$
where $\circ$ denotes (complex) dot product.


Proof













\(\ds z_1 \circ z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + 5 i} \circ \paren {3 - i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 3 + 5 \times \paren {-1}\)





Definition 1 of Dot Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 - 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









$\blacksquare$
