Theorem
Let $r \in \R_{> 0}$ be a (strictly) positive real number.
Let $x, y \in \Q$ be rational numbers.
Let $r^x$ be defined as $r$ to the power of $x$.

Then:

$\paren {r^x}^y = r^{x y}$


Proof
Let $x = \dfrac p q, y = \dfrac u v$.
Consider $\paren {\paren {r^x}^y}^{q v}$.
Then:














\(\ds \paren {\paren {r^x}^y}^{q v}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^\paren {u / v} }^{q v}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {\paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^\paren {u / v} }^v}^q\)





Product of Indices of Real Number: Integers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^u}^q\)





Definition of Rational Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^{q u}\)





Product of Indices of Real Number: Integers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^q}^u\)





Product of Indices of Real Number: Integers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {r^p}^u\)





Definition of Rational Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^{p u}\)





Product of Indices of Real Number: Integers








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {\paren {r^\paren {p / q} }^\paren {u / v} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^{\paren {p u} / \paren {q v} }\)





taking $q v$th root of both sides








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {r^x}^y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^{x y}\)









$\blacksquare$
