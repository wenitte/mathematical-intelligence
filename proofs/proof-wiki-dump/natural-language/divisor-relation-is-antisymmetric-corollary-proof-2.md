Corollary to Divisor Relation is Antisymmetric
Let $a, b \in \Z$.
If $a \divides b$ and $b \divides a$ then $a = b$ or $a = -b$.


Proof
Let $a \divides b$ and $b \divides a$.
Then by definition of divisor:

$\exists c, d \in \Z: a c = b, b d = a$
Thus:














\(\ds a c d\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds c d\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds d\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm 1\)





Divisors of One








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm b\)





as $a = b d$



$\blacksquare$
