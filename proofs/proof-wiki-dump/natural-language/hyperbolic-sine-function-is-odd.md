Theorem
Let $\sinh: \C \to \C$ be the hyperbolic sine function on the set of complex numbers.

Then $\sinh$ is odd:

$\map \sinh {-x} = -\sinh x$


Proof 1













\(\ds \map \sinh {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{-x} - e^{-\paren {-x} } } 2\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{-x} - e^x} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {e^x - e^{-x} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sinh x\)









$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \map \sinh {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -i \, \map \sin {-i x}\)





Hyperbolic Sine in terms of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \, \map \sin {i x}\)





Sine Function is Odd














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sinh x\)





Hyperbolic Sine in terms of Sine



$\blacksquare$
