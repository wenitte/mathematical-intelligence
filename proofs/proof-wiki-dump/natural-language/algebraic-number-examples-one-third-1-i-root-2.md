Example of Algebraic Number
$\dfrac 1 3 \paren {1 + i \sqrt 2}$ is an algebraic number.


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 3 \paren {1 + i \sqrt 2}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3 x - 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \sqrt 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {3 x - 1}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 i^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 9 x^2 - 6 x + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3 x^2 - 2 x + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









Hence the result by definition of algebraic number.
$\blacksquare$
