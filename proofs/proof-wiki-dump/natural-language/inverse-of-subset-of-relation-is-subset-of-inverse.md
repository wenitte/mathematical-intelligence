Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets
Let $\RR_1 = S \times T$ be a relation on $S \times T$.
Let $\RR_2 \subseteq \RR_1$.

Then:

$\RR_2^{-1} \subseteq \RR_1^{-1}$
where $\RR_1^{-1}$ denotes the inverse of $\RR_1$.


Proof













\(\ds \tuple {t, s}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2^{-1}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {s, t}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2\)





Definition of Inverse Relation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {s, t}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1\)





Definition of Subset








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {t, s}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1^{-1}\)





Definition of Inverse Relation



The result follows by definition of subset.
$\blacksquare$
