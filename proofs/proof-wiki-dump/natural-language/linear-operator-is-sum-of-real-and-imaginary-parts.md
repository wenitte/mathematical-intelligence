Theorem
Let $\HH$ be a Hilbert space over $\C$.
Let $A \in \map B \HH$ be a bounded linear operator.

Let $B$ and $C$ be the real and imaginary parts of $A$, respectively.

Then $A = B + i C$.


Proof













\(\ds B + i C\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \paren {A + A^*} + i \frac 1 {2i} \paren {A - A^*}\)





Definitions of $B, C$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 A + \frac 1 2 A^* + \frac 1 2 A - \frac 1 2 A^*\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds A\)









$\blacksquare$
