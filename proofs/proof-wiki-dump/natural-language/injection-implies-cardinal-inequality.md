Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets.
Let $f: S \to T$ be an injection.
Let $\card T$ denote the cardinal number of $T$.
Let:

$T \sim \card T$
where $\sim$ denotes set equivalence

Then:

$\card S \le \card T$


Proof
Let $f \sqbrk S$ denote the image of $S$ under $f$.















\(\ds S\)

\(\sim\)







\(\ds f \sqbrk S\)





Set is Equivalent to Image under Injection














\(\ds \)

\(\subseteq\)







\(\ds T\)





Image Preserves Subsets








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \card S\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \card {f \sqbrk S}\)





Equivalent Sets have Equal Cardinal Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(\le\)







\(\ds \card T\)





Subset implies Cardinal Inequality



$\blacksquare$
