Theorem
Let $\tuple {\floor {k \phi}, \floor {k \phi^2} }$ be a Wythoff pair.
The difference between the coordinates of this Wythoff pair is $k$.

That is:

$\floor {k \phi^2} - \floor {k \phi} = k$


Proof













\(\ds \floor {k \phi^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \floor {k \paren {1 + \phi} }\)





Square of Golden Mean equals One plus Golden Mean














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \floor {k + k \phi}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds k + \floor {k \phi}\)









$\blacksquare$
