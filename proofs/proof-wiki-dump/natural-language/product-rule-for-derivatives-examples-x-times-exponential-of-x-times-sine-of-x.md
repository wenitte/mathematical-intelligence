Examples of Use of Product Rule for Derivatives
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {x e^x \sin x} = e^x \paren {\paren {1 + x} \sin x + x \cos x}$


Proof
Let $u = x$.
Let $v = e^x$.
Let $w = \sin x$.
Thus we have:

$y = u v w$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds u v \dfrac {\d w} {\d x} + u w \dfrac {\d v} {\d x} + v w \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Product Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x e^x \cos x + x \sin x e^x + e^x \sin x \cdot 1\)





Power Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Sine Function, Derivative of Exponential Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^x \paren {\paren {1 + x} \sin x + x \cos x}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
