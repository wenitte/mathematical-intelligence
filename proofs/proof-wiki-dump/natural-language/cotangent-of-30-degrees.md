Theorem
$\cot 30 \degrees = \cot \dfrac \pi 6 = \sqrt 3$
where $\cot$ denotes cotangent.


Proof













\(\ds \cot 30 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cos 30 \degrees} {\sin 30 \degrees}\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac {\sqrt 3} 2} {\frac 1 2}\)





Cosine of $30 \degrees$ and Sine of $30 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2$



$\blacksquare$
