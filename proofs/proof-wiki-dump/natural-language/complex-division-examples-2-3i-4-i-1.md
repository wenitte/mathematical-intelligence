Example of Complex Division
$\dfrac {2 - 3 i} {4 - i} = \dfrac {11} {17} - \dfrac {10} {17} i$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {2 - 3 i} {4 - i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\paren {2 - 3 i} \paren {4 + i} } {\paren {4 - i} \paren {4 + i} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $4 + i$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {8 - 12 i + 2 i - 3 i^2} {4^2 + 1^2}\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {11 - 10 i} {17}\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
