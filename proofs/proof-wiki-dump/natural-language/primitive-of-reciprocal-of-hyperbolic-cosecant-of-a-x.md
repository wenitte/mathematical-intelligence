Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\csch a x} = \frac {\cosh a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\csch a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sinh a x \rd x\)





Definition 2 of Hyperbolic Cosecant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\sinh a x$



$\blacksquare$
