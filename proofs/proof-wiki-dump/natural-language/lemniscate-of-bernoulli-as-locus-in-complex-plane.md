Theorem
The locus of $z$ on the complex plane such that:

$\cmod {z - a} \cmod {z + a} = a^2$
is a lemniscate of Bernoulli.


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {z - a} \cmod {z + a}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {\paren {x - a}^2 + y^2} \paren {\paren {x + a}^2 + y^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4\)





Definition of Complex Modulus








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 - 2 a x + a^2 + y^2} \paren {x^2 + 2 a x + a^2 + y^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {\paren {x^2 + a^2 + y^2} - 2 a x} \paren {\paren {x^2 + a^2 + y^2} + 2 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 + a^2 + y^2}^2 - 4 a^2 x^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4\)





Difference of Two Squares








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 + y^2}^2 + a^4 + 2 \paren {x^2 + y^2} a^2 - 4 a^2 x^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 + y^2}^2 + 2 a^2 \paren {x^2 - 2 x^2 + y^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 + y^2}^2 + 2 a^2 \paren {y^2 - x^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 + y^2}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 a^2 \paren {x^2 - y^2}\)









which is the equation defining a lemniscate of Bernoulli with major semiaxis $a \sqrt 2$.
$\blacksquare$
