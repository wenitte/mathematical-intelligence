Theorem
Let $z_1 := r_1 e^{i \theta_1}, z_2 := r_2 e^{i \theta_2} \in \C$ be complex numbers expressed in exponential form.

Then:

$z_1 \circ z_2 = r_1 r_2 \, \map \cos {\theta_2 - \theta_1}$
where $z_1 \circ z_2$ denotes the dot product of $z_1$ and $z_2$.


Proof













\(\ds z_1 \circ z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {\overline {z_1} z_2}\)





Definition 3 of Dot Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {r_1 e^{-i \theta_1} r_2 e^{i \theta_2} }\)





Exponential Form of Complex Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {r_1 r_2 e^{i \paren {\theta_2 - \theta_1} } }\)





Product of Complex Numbers in Exponential Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {r_1 r_2 \paren {\map \cos {\theta_2 - \theta_1} + i \, \map \sin {\theta_2 - \theta_1} } }\)





Definition of Polar Form of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds r_1 r_2 \map \cos {\theta_2 - \theta_1}\)





Definition of Real Part



$\blacksquare$
