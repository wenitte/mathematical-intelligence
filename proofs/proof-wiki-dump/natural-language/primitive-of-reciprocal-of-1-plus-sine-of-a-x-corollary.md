Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 + \sin x} = \map \tan {\frac x 2 - \frac \pi 4} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 + \sin a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 a \map \tan {\frac \pi 4 - \frac {a x} 2} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {1 + \sin a x}$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 + \sin x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 - \frac x 2} + C\)





setting $a \gets 1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \tan {\frac x 2 - \frac \pi 4} + C\)





Tangent Function is Odd



$\blacksquare$
