Theorem
Let $x$ be an ordinal.
Let $x^+$ denote the successor of $x$.
Let $1$ denote (ordinal) one, the successor of the zero ordinal $\O$.

Then:

$x + 1 = x^+$
where $+$ denotes ordinal addition.


Proof













\(\ds x + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x + \O^+\)





Definition of (ordinal) $1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x + \O}^+\)





Definition of Ordinal Addition














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^+\)





Ordinal Addition by Zero



$\blacksquare$
