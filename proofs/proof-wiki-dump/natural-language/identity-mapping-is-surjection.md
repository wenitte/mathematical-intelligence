Theorem
On any set $S$, the identity mapping $I_S: S \to S$ is a surjection.


Proof
The identity mapping is defined as:

$\forall y \in S: \map {I_S} y = y$
Then we have:










\(\ds \forall y \in S: \exists x \in S: \, \)



\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)





that is, $y$ itself








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall y \in S: \exists x \in S: \, \)



\(\ds \map {I_S} x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)





Definition of $I_S$



Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
