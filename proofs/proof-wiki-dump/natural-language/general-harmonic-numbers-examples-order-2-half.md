Example of General Harmonic Number
$\harm 2 {\dfrac 1 2} = 4 - 2 \map \zeta 2$
where $\harm 2 {\dfrac 1 2}$ denotes the general harmonic number of order $2$ evaluated at $\dfrac 1 2$.


Proof













\(\ds \harm r x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \harm r {x - 1} + \dfrac 1 {x^r}\)





Recurrence Relation for General Harmonic Numbers








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \harm 2 {\dfrac 1 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \harm 2 {-\dfrac 1 2} + \frac 1 {\paren {\frac 1 2}^2}\)





setting $x := \dfrac 1 2$ and $r := 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds - 2 \map \zeta 2 + 4\)





General Harmonic Number of Order $2$ at $-\dfrac 1 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 - 2 \map \zeta 2\)





rearranging



$\blacksquare$
