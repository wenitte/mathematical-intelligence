Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a ring with unity.
Let $z \in U_R$, where $U_R$ is the set of units.

Then:

$\paren {-z}^{-1} = -\paren {z^{-1} }$
where $z^{-1}$ is the ring product inverse of $z$.


Proof
Let the unity of $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be $1_R$.














\(\ds \paren {-\paren {z^{-1} } } \circ \paren {-z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z^{-1} \circ z\)





Product of Ring Negatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1_R\)





Inverse under $\circ$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds z \circ z^{-1}\)





Inverse under $\circ$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {-z} \circ \paren {-\paren {z^{-1} } }\)





Product of Ring Negatives



Thus:

$\paren {-z}^{-1} = -\paren {z^{-1} }$
$\blacksquare$
