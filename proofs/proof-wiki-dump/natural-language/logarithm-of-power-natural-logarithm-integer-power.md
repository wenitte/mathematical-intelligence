Theorem
Let $x \in \R$ be a strictly positive real number.
Let $n \in \R$ be any integer.
Let $\ln x$ be the natural logarithm of $x$.

Then:

$\map \ln {x^n} = n \ln x$


Proof
From Logarithm of Power/Natural Logarithm/Natural Power, the theorem is already proven for positive integers.
Let $j \in \Z_{<0}$.
Let $-j = k \in Z_{>0}$.
Then:














\(\ds 0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln 1\)





Logarithm of 1 is 0














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \ln {x^k x^{-k} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \ln {x^k} + \map \ln {x^{-k} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds k \ln x + \map \ln {x^{-k} }\)





Logarithm of Power/Natural Logarithm/Natural Power








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \ln {x^{-k} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -k \ln x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \ln {x^j}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds j \ln x\)









$\blacksquare$
