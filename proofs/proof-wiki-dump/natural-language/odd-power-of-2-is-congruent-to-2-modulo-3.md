Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{\ge 0}$ be an odd positive integer.
Then:

$2^n \equiv 2 \pmod 3$


Proof
From Congruence Modulo 3 of Power of 2:

$2^n \equiv \paren {-1}^n \pmod 3$
We have that $n$ is odd.
Hence:














\(\ds 2^n\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds -1\)

\(\ds \pmod 3\)


















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 3 - 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 3\)


















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 2\)

\(\ds \pmod 3\)







$\blacksquare$
