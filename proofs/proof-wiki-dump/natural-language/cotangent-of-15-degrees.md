Theorem
$\cot 15 \degrees = \cot \dfrac {\pi} {12} = 2 + \sqrt 3$
where $\cot$ denotes cotangent.


Proof













\(\ds \cot 15^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cos 15 \degrees} {\sin 15 \degrees}\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} 4} {\frac {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} 4}\)





Cosine of $15 \degrees$ and Sine of $15 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2}\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2}^2} {\paren {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} \paren {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {6 + 2 \sqrt 6 \sqrt 2 + 2 } {6 - 2}\)





multiplying out, and Difference of Two Squares














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {8 + 4 \sqrt 3} 4\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + \sqrt 3\)





dividing top and bottom by $4$



$\blacksquare$
