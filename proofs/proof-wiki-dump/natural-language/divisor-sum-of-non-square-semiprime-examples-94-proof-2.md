Example of Divisor Sum of Non-Square Semiprime
$\map {\sigma_1} {94} = 144$


Proof
We have that:

$94 = 2 \times 47$
and so by definition is a semiprime whose prime factors are distinct.

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {94}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + 1} \paren {47 + 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Non-Square Semiprime














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 48\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times \paren {2^4 \times 3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^4 \times 3^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2^2 \times 3}^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 144\)









$\blacksquare$
