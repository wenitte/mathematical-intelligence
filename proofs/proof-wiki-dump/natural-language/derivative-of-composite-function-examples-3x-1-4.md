Example of Derivative of Composite Function
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\paren {3 x + 1}^4} = 12 \paren {3 x + 1}^3$


Proof
Let $u = 3 x + 1$.
Let $y = u^4$.
Then we have:

$y = \paren {3 x + 1}^4$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d u} \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \cdot 4 \paren {3 x + 1}^3\)





Power Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Identity Function: Corollary














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \paren {3 x + 1}^3\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
