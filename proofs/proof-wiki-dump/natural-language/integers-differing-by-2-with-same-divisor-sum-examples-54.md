Example of Integers Differing by 2 with Same Divisor Sum
Let $\sigma_1: \Z_{>0} \to \Z_{>0}$ denote the divisor sum function.
Then:

$\map {\sigma_1} {54} = \map {\sigma_1} {56} = 120$


Proof
From $\sigma_1$ of $54$:

$\map {\sigma_1} {54} = 120$
From $\sigma_1$ of $56$:

$\map {\sigma_1} {56} = 120$
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
