Theorem
The intersection of two symmetric relations is also a symmetric relation.


Proof
Let $\RR_1$ and $\RR_2$ be symmetric relations on a set $S$.
Let $\RR_3 = \RR_1 \cap \RR_2$.
Then:














\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1\)





Definition of Set Intersection












\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {y, x}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1\)





Definition of Symmetric Relation












\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds \tuple {y, x}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {y, x}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_3\)





Definition of Set Intersection



$\blacksquare$
