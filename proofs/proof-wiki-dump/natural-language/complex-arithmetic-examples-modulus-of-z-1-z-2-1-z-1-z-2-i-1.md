Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 1 - i$ and $z_2 = -2 + 4 i$.
Then:

$\cmod {\dfrac {z_1 + z_2 + 1} {z_1 - z_2 + i} } = \dfrac 3 5$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {\dfrac {z_1 + z_2 + 1} {z_1 - z_2 + i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\dfrac {\paren {1 - i} + \paren {-2 + 4 i} + 1} {\paren {1 - i} - \paren {-2 + 4 i} + i} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\dfrac {\paren {3 i} } {\paren {3 + 4 i} } }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\cmod {3 i} } {\cmod {3 + 4 i} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 3 {\sqrt {3^2 + 4^2} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 3 5\)









$\blacksquare$
