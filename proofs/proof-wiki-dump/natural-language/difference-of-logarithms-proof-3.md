Theorem
$\log_b x - \log_b y = \map {\log_b} {\dfrac x y}$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\log_b} {\frac x y} + \log_b y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\log_b} {\frac x y \times y}\)





Sum of Logarithms














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \log_b x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map {\log_b} {\frac x y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \log_b x - \log_b y\)





subtracting $\log_b y$ from both sides



$\blacksquare$
