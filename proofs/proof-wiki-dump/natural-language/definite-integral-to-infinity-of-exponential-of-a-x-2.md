Theorem
$\ds \int_0^\infty e^{-a x^2} \rd x = \frac 1 2 \sqrt {\frac \pi a}$
where $a$ is a strictly positive real number.


Proof













\(\ds \int_0^\infty e^{-a x^2} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^\infty e^{-\paren {\sqrt a x}^2} \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\sqrt a} \int_0^\infty e^{-t^2} \rd t\)





substituting $t = \sqrt a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \sqrt {\frac \pi a}\)





Integral to Infinity of $e^{-t^2}$



$\blacksquare$
