Examples of Use of Euclidean Algorithm
The GCD of $1769$ and $2378$ is found to be:

$\gcd \set {1769, 2378} = 29$


Proof



\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 2378\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 1769 + 609\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 1769\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 609 + 551\)










\(\text {(3)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 609\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 551 + 58\)










\(\text {(4)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 551\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9 \times 58 + 29\)










\(\text {(5)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 58\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 29\)










Thus:

$\gcd \set {1769, 2378} = 29$
$\blacksquare$
