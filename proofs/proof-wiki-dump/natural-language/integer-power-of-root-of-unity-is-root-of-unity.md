Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{> 0}$ be a strictly positive integer.
Let $F$ be a field.
Let $\alpha$ be an $n$-th root of unity.
Let $k \in \Z$.

Then:

$\alpha^k$ is an $n$-th root of unity.
Proof
We have:














\(\ds \alpha^n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





By Assumption














\(\ds \paren{\alpha^n}^k\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1^k\)




















\(\ds \paren{\alpha^k}^n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Product of Indices Law for Field



$\blacksquare$
