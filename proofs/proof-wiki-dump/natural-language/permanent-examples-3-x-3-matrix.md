Example of Permanent
The square matrix:

$\begin {pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end {pmatrix}$
has a permanent of $450$.


Proof
Let $P$ denote the permanent of $\begin {pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end {pmatrix}$.
We have:














\(\ds P\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 5 \times 9 + 1 \times 6 \times 8 + 2 \times 4 \times 9 + 2 \times 6 \times 7 + 3 \times 4 \times 8 + 3 \times 5 \times 7\)





Definition of Permanent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 450\)





after calculation



$\blacksquare$
