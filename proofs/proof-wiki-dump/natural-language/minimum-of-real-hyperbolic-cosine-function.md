Theorem
Let $x$ be a real number.
Then:

$\cosh x \ge 1$
where $\cosh$ denotes the hyperbolic cosine function.


Proof













\(\ds \cosh^2 x - \sinh^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Difference of Squares of Hyperbolic Cosine and Sine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cosh^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + \sinh^2 x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\ge\)







\(\ds 1\)





Square of Real Number is Non-Negative



Furthermore, $\cosh x = 1$ when $x = 0$, satisfying the equality case.
$\blacksquare$
