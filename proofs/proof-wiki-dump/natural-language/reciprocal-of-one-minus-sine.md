Theorem
$\dfrac 1 {1 - \sin x} = \dfrac 1 2 \map {\sec^2} {\dfrac \pi 4 + \dfrac x 2}$


Proof













\(\ds 1 - \sin x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin \frac \pi 2 - \sin x\)





Sine of Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \map \cos {\frac 1 2 \paren {\frac \pi 2 + x} } \map \sin {\frac 1 2 \paren {\frac \pi 2 - x} }\)





Sine minus Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \map \cos {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2} \map \sin {\frac \pi 4 - \frac x 2}\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \map \cos {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2} \map \cos {\frac \pi 2 - \paren {\frac \pi 4 - \frac x 2} }\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \map \cos {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2} \map \cos {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2}\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \map {\cos^2} {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2}\)





simplifying








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac 1 {1 - \sin x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \map {\sec^2} {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2}\)





Definition of Secant Function



$\blacksquare$
