Theorem
Let $x, b \in \R$ be strictly positive real numbers such that $b > 1$.

Then:

$\map {\log_b} {\dfrac 1 x} = -\log_b x$
where $\log_b$ denotes the logarithm to base $b$.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\log_b} {\dfrac 1 x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \log_b 1 - \log_b x\)





Difference of Logarithms














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 - \log_b x\)





Logarithm of 1 is 0



Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
