Theorem
Let $m \in \Z_{> 0}$.
Let $x, y, c \in \Z$.
Let $x \equiv y \pmod m$.

Then:

$c x \equiv c y \pmod m$


Proof
Let $x \equiv y \pmod m$.
Then by definition of congruence:

$\exists k \in Z: x - y = k m$
Hence:

$c x - c y = c k m$
and so by definition of congruence:

$c x \equiv c y \pmod m$
$\blacksquare$
