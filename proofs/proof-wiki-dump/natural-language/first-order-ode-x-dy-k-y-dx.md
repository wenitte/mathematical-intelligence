Theorem
The first order ODE:

$(1): \quad x \rd y = k y \rd x$
has the general solution:

$y = C x^k$


Proof
$(1)$ can be expressed as:














\(\ds x \rd y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k y \rd x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \dfrac {\d y} y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k \int \dfrac {\d x} x\)





Solutions to Separable Differential Equation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \ln y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k \ln x + \ln C\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \ln {C x^k}\)




















\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds C x^k\)









$\blacksquare$
