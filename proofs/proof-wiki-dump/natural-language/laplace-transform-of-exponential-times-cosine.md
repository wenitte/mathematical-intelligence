Theorem
$\map {\laptrans {e^{b t} \cos a t} } s = \dfrac {s - b} {\paren {s - b}^2 + a^2}$
where:

$a$ and $b$ are real numbers
$s$ is a complex number with $\map \Re s > a + b$
$\laptrans f$ denotes the Laplace transform of $f$ evaluated at $s$.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\laptrans {e^{b t} \cos a t} } s\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\laptrans {\cos a t} } {s - b}\)





Laplace Transform of Exponential times Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {s - b} {\paren {s - b}^2 + a^2}\)





Laplace Transform of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
