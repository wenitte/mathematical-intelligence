Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {x \sqrt {x^2 + a^2} } = -\frac 1 a \sinh^{-1} {\frac a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {x \sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 a \csch^{-1} {\frac x a} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {x \sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }$: Inverse Hyperbolic Cosecant Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 a \sinh^{-1} {\frac a x} + C\)





Real Area Hyperbolic Sine of Reciprocal equals Real Area Hyperbolic Cosecant



$\blacksquare$
