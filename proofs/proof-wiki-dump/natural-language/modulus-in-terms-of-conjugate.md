Theorem
Let $z = a + i b$ be a complex number.
Let $\cmod z$ be the modulus of $z$.
Let $\overline z$ be the conjugate of $z$.

Then:

$\cmod z^2 = z \overline z$


Proof
Let $z = a + i b$.
Then:














\(\ds z \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2 + b^2\)





Product of Complex Number with Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod z^2\)





Definition of Complex Modulus



$\blacksquare$
