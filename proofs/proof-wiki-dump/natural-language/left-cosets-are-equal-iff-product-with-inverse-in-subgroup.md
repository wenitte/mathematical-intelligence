Theorem
Let $G$ be a group.
Let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$.
Let $x, y \in G$.
Let $x H$ denote the left coset of $H$ by $x$.

Then:

$x H = y H \iff x^{-1} y \in H$


Proof













\(\ds x H\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y H\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(\equiv^l\)







\(\ds y \pmod H\)





Left Coset Space forms Partition








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x^{-1} y\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds H\)





Equivalent Statements for Congruence Modulo Subgroup



$\blacksquare$
