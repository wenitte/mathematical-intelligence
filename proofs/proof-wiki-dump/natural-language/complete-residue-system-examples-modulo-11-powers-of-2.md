Examples of Complete Residue Systems
The set of integers:

$\set {0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512}$
forms a complete residue system modulo $11$.


Proof
We have:














\(\ds 16\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 11 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 5\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)


















\(\ds 32\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 11 + 10\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 10\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)


















\(\ds 64\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5 \times 11 + 9\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 9\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)


















\(\ds 128\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 11 \times 11 + 7\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 7\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)


















\(\ds 256\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 23 \times 11 + 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 3\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)


















\(\ds 512\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 46 \times 11 + 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 6\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)







Thus we see that:

$\set {0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512}$
is equivalent to:

$\set {0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 5, 10, 9, 7, 3, 6}$
The result follows from Initial Segment of Natural Numbers forms Complete Residue System.
$\blacksquare$
