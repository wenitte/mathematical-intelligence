Theorem
Let $z = a + i b$ be a complex number.
Then its fifth power is given by:

$z^5 = a^5 - 10 a^3 b^2 + 5 a b^4 + i \paren {5 a^4 b - 10 a^2 b^3 + b^5}$


Proof













\(\ds z^5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a + i b}^5\)





by hypothesis














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^5 + 5 a^4 \paren {i b} + 10 a^3 \paren {i b}^2 + 10 a^2 \paren {i b}^3 + 5 a \paren {i b}^4 + \paren {i b}^5\)





Fifth Power of Sum














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^5 + 5 i a^4 b + 10 i^2 a^3 b^2 + 10 i^3 a^2 b^3 + 5 i^4 a b^4 + i^5 b^5\)





Complex Multiplication is Commutative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^5 + 5 i a^4 b - 10 a^3 b^2 - 10 i a^2 b^3 + 5 a b^4 + i b^5\)





Definition of Imaginary Unit














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^5 - 10 a^3 b^2 + 5 a b^4 + i\paren {5 a^4 b - 10 a^2 b^3 + b^5}\)





gathering like terms



$\blacksquare$
