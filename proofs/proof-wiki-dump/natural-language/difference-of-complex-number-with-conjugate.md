Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Let $\overline z$ be the complex conjugate of $z$.
Let $\map \Im z$ be the imaginary part of $z$.

Then

$z - \overline z = 2 i \, \map \Im z$


Proof
Let $z = x + i y$.
Then:














\(\ds z - \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x + i y} - \paren {x - i y}\)





Definition of Complex Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x + i y - x + i y\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 i y\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 i \, \map \Im z\)





Definition of Imaginary Part



$\blacksquare$


Also defined as
This result is also reported as:

$\map \Im z = \dfrac {z - \overline z} {2 i}$
