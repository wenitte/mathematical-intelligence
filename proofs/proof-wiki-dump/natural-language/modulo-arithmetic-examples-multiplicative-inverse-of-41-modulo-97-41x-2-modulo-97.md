Example of Modulo Arithmetic
The solution of the integer congruence:

$41 x \equiv 2 \pmod {97}$
is:

$x = 45$


Proof
From Multiplicative Inverse of $41 \pmod {97}$ we have:

$41 \times 71 = 1 \pmod {97}$

Thus:














\(\ds 41 x\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 2\)

\(\ds \pmod {97}\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 71 \times 41 x\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 71 \times 2\)

\(\ds \pmod {97}\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 142\)

\(\ds \pmod {97}\)


















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 45\)

\(\ds \pmod {97}\)








Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
