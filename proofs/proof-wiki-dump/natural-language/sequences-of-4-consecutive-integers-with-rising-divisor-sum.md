Theorem
The following ordered quadruples of consecutive integers have divisor sum values which are strictly increasing:

$61, 62, 63, 64$
$73, 74, 75, 76$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {61}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 62\)





Divisor Sum of Prime Number: $61$ is prime














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {62}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 96\)





$\sigma_1$ of $62$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {63}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 104\)





$\sigma_1$ of $63$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {64}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 127\)





$\sigma_1$ of $64$


















\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {73}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 74\)





Divisor Sum of Prime Number: $73$ is prime














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {74}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 114\)





$\sigma_1$ of $74$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {75}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 124\)





$\sigma_1$ of $75$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {76}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 140\)





$\sigma_1$ of $76$



$\blacksquare$
