Example of Modulo Polynomial Division
Let:














\(\ds \map f x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^3 - x^2 - 1\)




















\(\ds \map g x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x - 5\)










Then $\map f x$ divided by $\map g x$ modulo $11$ is:

$x^2 + 4 x - 2$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {x - 5} \paren {x^2 + 4 x - 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^3 - x^2 - 22 x + 10\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds x^3 - x^2 - 1\)

\(\ds \pmod {11}\)







$\blacksquare$
