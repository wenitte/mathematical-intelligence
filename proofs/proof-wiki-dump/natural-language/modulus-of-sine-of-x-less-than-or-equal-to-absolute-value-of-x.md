Theorem
Let $x$ be a real number.
Then:

$\size {\sin x} \le \size x$


Proof
Clearly the inequality holds if $x = 0$.
Take $x \ne 0$. 
From the Mean Value Theorem and Derivative of Sine Function, there exists $c \in \R$ such that: 

$\ds \frac {\sin x - \sin 0} {x - 0} = \cos c$
so:

$\sin x = x \cos c$
Then we have: 














\(\ds \size {\sin x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \size {x \cos c}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\le\)







\(\ds \size x\)









as required.
$\blacksquare$
