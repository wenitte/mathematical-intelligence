Theorem
Let $b \in \R_{>0}$ be a strictly positive real number such that $b \ne 1$.
Let $\log_b$ denote the logarithm to base $b$.

Then:

$\log_b b = 1$


Proof
By definition of logarithm:














\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \log_b b\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds b^y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b\)





Definition of Real General Logarithm








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Definition of Power to Real Number



$\blacksquare$
