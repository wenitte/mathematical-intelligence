Corollary to Primitive of Sine Function
$\ds \int \sin a x \rd x = - \frac {\cos a x} a + C$
where $a$ is a non-zero constant.


Proof













\(\ds \int \sin x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cos x + C\)





Primitive of $\sin x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \sin a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {-\cos a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\cos a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
