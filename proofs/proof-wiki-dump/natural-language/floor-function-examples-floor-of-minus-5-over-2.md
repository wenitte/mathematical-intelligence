Theorem
$\floor {-\dfrac 5 2} = -3$
where $\floor x$ denotes the floor of $x$.


Proof
We have that:














\(\ds -\dfrac 5 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -3 + \dfrac 1 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds -3\)

\(\le\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 5 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(<\)







\(\ds -2\)










Hence $-3$ is the floor of $-\dfrac 5 2$ by definition.
$\blacksquare$
