Theorem
$\map \cos {2 \pi - \theta} = \cos \theta$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.

That is, the cosine of an angle equals its conjugate.


Proof













\(\ds \map \cos {2 \pi - \theta}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {2 \pi} \cos \theta + \map \sin {2 \pi} \sin \theta\)





Cosine of Difference














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times \cos \theta + 0 \times \sin \theta\)





Cosine of Full Angle and Sine of Full Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \theta\)









$\blacksquare$
