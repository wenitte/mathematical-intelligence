Example of Algebraic Number
$\sqrt 3 + \sqrt 2$ is an algebraic number.


Proof
Let $x = \sqrt 3 + \sqrt 2$.
We have that:














\(\ds x - \sqrt 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x - \sqrt 2}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^2 - 2 \sqrt 2 x + 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^2 - 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \sqrt 2 x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 - 1}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 8 x^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^4 - 2 x^2 + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 8 x^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^4 - 10 x^2 + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









Thus $\sqrt 3 + \sqrt 2$ is a root of $x^4 - 10 x^2 + 1 = 0$.
Hence the result by definition of algebraic number.
$\blacksquare$
