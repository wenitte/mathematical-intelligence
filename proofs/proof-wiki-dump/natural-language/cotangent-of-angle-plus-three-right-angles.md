Theorem
$\map \cot {x + \dfrac {3 \pi} 2} = -\tan x$


Proof













\(\ds \map \cot {x + \frac {3 \pi} 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\map \cos {x + \frac {3 \pi} 2} } {\map \sin {x + \frac {3 \pi} 2} }\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin x} {-\cos x}\)





Cosine of Angle plus Right Angle and Sine of Angle plus Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\tan x\)





Tangent is Sine divided by Cosine



$\blacksquare$
