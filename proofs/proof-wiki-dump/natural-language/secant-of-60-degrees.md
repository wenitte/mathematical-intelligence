Theorem
$\sec 60 \degrees = \sec \dfrac \pi 3 = 2$
where $\sec$ denotes secant.


Proof













\(\ds \sec 60 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos 60 \degrees}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac 1 2}\)





Cosine of $60 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2$



$\blacksquare$
