Theorem













\(\ds \sin \frac \theta 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds +\sqrt {\frac {1 - \cos \theta} 2}\)





for $\dfrac \theta 2$ in quadrant $\text I$ or quadrant $\text {II}$














\(\ds \sin \frac \theta 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sqrt {\dfrac {1 - \cos \theta} 2}\)





for $\dfrac \theta 2$ in quadrant $\text {III}$ or quadrant $\text {IV}$



where $\sin$ denotes sine and $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof 1













\(\ds \cos \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - 2 \ \sin^2 \frac \theta 2\)





Double Angle Formula for Cosine: Corollary $2$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 \ \sin^2 \frac \theta 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \cos \theta\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sin \frac \theta 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm \sqrt {\frac {1 - \cos \theta} 2}\)










We also have that:

In quadrant $\text I$ and quadrant $\text {II}$, $\sin \theta > 0$
In quadrant $\text {III}$ and quadrant $\text {IV}$, $\sin \theta < 0$.
$\blacksquare$


Proof 2
Define:

$u = \dfrac \theta 2$

Then:














\(\ds \sin^2 u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {1 - \cos 2 u} 2\)





Power Reduction Formulas








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sin \frac \theta 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm \sqrt {\frac {1 - \cos \theta} 2}\)










We also have that:

In quadrant $\text I$, and quadrant $\text {II}$, $\sin \theta > 0$
In quadrant $\text {III}$, and quadrant $\text {IV}$, $\sin \theta < 0$.
$\blacksquare$
