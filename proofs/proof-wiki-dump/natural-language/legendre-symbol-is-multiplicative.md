Theorem
Let $p$ be a odd prime.
Let $a, b \in \Z$.

Then:

$\paren {\dfrac {a b} p} = \paren {\dfrac a p} \paren {\dfrac b p}$
where $\paren {\dfrac a p}$ is the Legendre symbol.


Proof
We have: 














\(\ds \paren {\frac {a b} p}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a b}^{\frac {p - 1} 2} \bmod p\)





Definition 2 of Legendre Symbol














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{\frac {p - 1} 2} b^{\frac {p - 1} 2} \bmod p\)





Power of Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\frac a p} \paren {\frac b p}\)





Definition 2 of Legendre Symbol, Congruence of Product



$\blacksquare$
