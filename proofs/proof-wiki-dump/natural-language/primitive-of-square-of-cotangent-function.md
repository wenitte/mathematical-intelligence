Theorem
$\ds \int \cot^2 x \rd x = -\cot x - x + C$
where $C$ is an arbitrary constant.


Proof













\(\ds \int \cot^2 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \paren {\csc^2 x - 1} \rd x\)





Difference of Squares of Cosecant and Cotangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \csc^2 x \rd x + \int \paren {-1} \rd x\)





Linear Combination of Primitives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot x + C + \int \paren {-1} \rd x\)





Primitive of Square of Cosecant Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot x - x + C\)





Primitive of Constant



$\blacksquare$
