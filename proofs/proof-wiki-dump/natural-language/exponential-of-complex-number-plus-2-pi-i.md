Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.

Then:

$\map \exp {z + 2 \pi i} = \map \exp z$


Proof













\(\ds \map \exp {z + 2 \pi i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z \, \map \exp {2 \pi i}\)





Exponential of Sum: Complex Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z \times 1\)





Euler's Formula Example: $e^{2 i \pi}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z\)









$\blacksquare$
