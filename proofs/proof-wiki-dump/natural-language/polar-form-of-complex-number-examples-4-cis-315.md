Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $\polar {4, 315 \degrees}$ can be expressed in Cartesian form as:

$4 \cis 315 \degrees = 2 \sqrt 2 - 2 \sqrt 2 i$
and depicted in the complex plane as:





Proof













\(\ds 4 \cis 315 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \paren {\cos 315 \degrees + i \sin 315 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \times \paren {\dfrac {\sqrt 2} 2 + \dfrac {-\sqrt 2} 2 i}\)





Cosine of $315 \degrees$ and Sine of $315 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \sqrt 2 - 2 \sqrt 2 i\)









$\blacksquare$
