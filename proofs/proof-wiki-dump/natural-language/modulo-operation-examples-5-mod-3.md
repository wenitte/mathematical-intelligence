Theorem
$5 \bmod 3 = 2$
where $\bmod$ denotes the modulo operation.


Proof
By definition of modulo operation:

$x \bmod y := x - y \floor {\dfrac x y}$
for $y \ne 0$.

We have:

$\dfrac 5 3 = 1 + \dfrac 2 3$
and so:

$\floor {\dfrac 5 3} = 1$
Thus:

$5 \bmod 3 = 5 - 3 \times \floor {\dfrac 5 3} = 5 - 3 \times 1 = 2$
$\blacksquare$
