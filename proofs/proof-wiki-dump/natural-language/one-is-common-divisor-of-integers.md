Theorem
Let $a, b \in \Z$ be integers.

Then $1$ is a common divisor of $a$ and $b$.


Proof
From One Divides all Integers:

$1 \divides a$
and:

$1 \divides b$
where $\divides$ denotes divisibility.
The result follows by definition of common divisor.
$\blacksquare$
