Theorem
$\ds \int \cot a x \rd x = \frac {\ln \size {\sin a x} } a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \cot x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\sin x}\)





Primitive of $\cot x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \cot a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {\ln \size {\sin a x} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\ln \size {\sin a x} } a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$


Examples
Cotangent of $x / 2$
$\ds \int \cot \dfrac x 2 \rd x = 2 \ln \size {\sin \dfrac 1 2} + C$
