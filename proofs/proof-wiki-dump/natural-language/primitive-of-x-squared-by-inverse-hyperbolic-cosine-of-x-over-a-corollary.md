Theorem
$\ds \int x^2 \paren {-\cosh^{-1} \dfrac x a} \rd x = \dfrac {x^3} 3 \paren {-\cosh^{-1} \dfrac x a} - \dfrac {\paren {x^2 + 2 a^2} \sqrt {x^2 - a^2} } 9 + C$
where $-\cosh^{-1}$ denotes the negative branch of the real inverse hyperbolic cosine multifunction.


Proof













\(\ds -\cosh^{-1} \frac x a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\arcosh \frac x a\)





Definition of Real Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int x^2 \paren {-\cosh^{-1} \dfrac x a} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\int x^2 \arcosh \frac x a \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {\dfrac {x^3} 3 \arcosh \dfrac x a - \dfrac {\paren {x^2 + 2 a^2} \sqrt {x^2 - a^2} } 9 + C}\)





Primitive of $x^2 \arcosh \dfrac x a$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x^3} 3 \paren {-\cosh^{-1} \dfrac x a} - \dfrac {\paren {x^2 + 2 a^2} \sqrt {x^2 - a^2} } 9 + C\)





Definition of Real Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine



$\blacksquare$
