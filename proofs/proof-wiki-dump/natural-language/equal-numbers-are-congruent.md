Theorem
$\forall x, y, z \in \R: x = y \implies x \equiv y \pmod z$
where $x \equiv y \pmod z$ denotes congruence modulo $z$.


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x - y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x - y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 \cdot z\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds y\)

\(\ds \pmod z\)



Definition of Congruence Modulo $z$



$\blacksquare$
