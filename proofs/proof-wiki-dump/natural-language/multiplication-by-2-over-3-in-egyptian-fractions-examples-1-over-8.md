Example of Use of Multiplication by 2 over 3 in Egyptian Fractions
$\dfrac 2 3 \times \dfrac 1 8 = \dfrac 1 {16} + \dfrac 1 {48}$
despite the fact that it is apparent that:

$\dfrac 2 3 \times \dfrac 1 8 = \dfrac 1 {12}$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {16} + \dfrac 1 {48}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {2 \times 8} + \dfrac 1 {6 \times 8}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 3 {6 \times 8} + \dfrac 1 {6 \times 8}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 4 {6 \times 8}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {3 \times 8}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 3 \times \dfrac 1 8\)









$\blacksquare$
