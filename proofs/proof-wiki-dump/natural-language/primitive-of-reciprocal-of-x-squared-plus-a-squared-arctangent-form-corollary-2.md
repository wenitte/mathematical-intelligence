Corollary to Primitive of Reciprocal of x squared plus a squared
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {a^2 + b^2 x^2} = \frac 1 {a b} \arctan \frac {b x} a + C$
where $a$ and $b$ are non-zero constants.

Proof
Let $z = b x$.
Then:

$\dfrac {\d x} {\d z} = \dfrac 1 b$
Hence:














\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {a^2 + b^2 x^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \dfrac 1 b \frac {\d z} {a^2 + z^2}\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 b \cdot \frac 1 a \arctan \frac z a + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {a^2 + z^2}$: Arctangent Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a b} \arctan \frac {b x} a + C\)





subtituting for $z$ and simplifying



$\blacksquare$
