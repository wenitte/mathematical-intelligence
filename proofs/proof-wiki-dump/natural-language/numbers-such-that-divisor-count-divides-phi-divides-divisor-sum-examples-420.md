Examples of Numbers such that Divisor Count divides Phi divides Divisor Sum
The number $420$ has the property that:

$\map {\sigma_0} {420} \divides \map \phi {420} \divides \map {\sigma_1} {420}$
where:

$\divides$ denotes divisibility
$\sigma_0$ denotes the divisor count function
$\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ (phi) function
$\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {420}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 24 \, \)





\(\ds \)





$\sigma_0$ of $420$














\(\ds \map \phi {420}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 96 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 4 \times 24\)





$\phi$ of $420$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {420}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 1344 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 14 \times 96\)





$\sigma_1$ of $420$



$\blacksquare$
