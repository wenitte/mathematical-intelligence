Modern Puzzles by Henry Ernest Dudeney: $93$
Sum Equals Product
There are two numbers whose sum equals their product, that is, $2$ and $2$.
What other numbers have that property?


Solution
Any $x$ and $y$ such that $y = \dfrac x {x - 1}$ fits the bill.


Proof













\(\ds x + y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x y\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y \paren {x - 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac x {x - 1}\)









and we note that:














\(\ds x + \dfrac x {x - 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x \paren {x - 1} + x} {x - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x^2} {x - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \dfrac x {x - 1}\)









$\blacksquare$
