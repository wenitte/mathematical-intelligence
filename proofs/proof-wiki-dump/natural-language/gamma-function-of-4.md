Theorem
Let $\Gamma$ denote the Gamma function.
Then:

$\map \Gamma 4 = 6$


Proof













\(\ds \map \Gamma 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Gamma {3 + 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \map \Gamma 3\)





Gamma Difference Equation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 2\)





Gamma Function of 3



$\blacksquare$
