Theorem
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {a + x} {a - x} } } = \dfrac 1 {a^2 - x^2}$
where $\size x < a$.


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \map {\tanh^{-1} } {\frac x a}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \cdot \dfrac 1 2 \map \ln {\dfrac {1 + \frac x a} {1 - \frac x a} }\)





Definition of Real Area Hyperbolic Tangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {a + x} {a - x} }\)





multiplying top and bottom of argument by $a$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 {2 a} \map \ln {\dfrac {a + x} {a - x} } }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\dfrac 1 a \map {\tanh^{-1} } {\frac x a} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 a \cdot \dfrac a {a^2 - x^2}\)





Derivative of Real Area Hyperbolic Tangent of x over a














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {a^2 - x^2}\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
