Theorem
Let $z = a + i b$ be a complex number.
Then its cube is given by:

$z^3 = a^3 - 3 a b^2 + i \paren {3 a^2 b - b^3}$


Proof













\(\ds z^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a + i b}^3\)





by hypothesis














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a + i b}^2 \paren {a + i b}\)





Definition of Cube (Algebra)














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a^2 - b^2 + i \paren {2 a b} } \paren {a + i b}\)





Square of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\paren {a^2 - b^2} a - b \paren {2 a b} } + i \paren {\paren {a^2 - b^2} b + \paren {2 a b} a}\)





Definition of Complex Multiplication














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a^3 - a b^2 - 2 a b^2} + i \paren {a^2 b - b^3 + 2 a^2 b}\)





multiplying out














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^3 - 3 a b^2 + i \paren {3 a^2 b - b^3}\)





gathering like terms



$\blacksquare$
