Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\cos^3 a x} = \frac {\sin a x} {2 a \cos^2 a x} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\cos^3 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sec^3 a x \rd x\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sec a x \tan a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 2 \int \sec a x \rd x\)





Primitive of $\sec^n a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin a x} {2 a \cos^2 a x} + \frac 1 2 \int \sec a x \rd x\)





Secant is $\dfrac 1 \cos$ and Tangent is $\dfrac \sin \cos$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin a x} {2 a \cos^2 a x} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C\)





Primitive of $\sec a x$



$\blacksquare$
