Theorem
Let $f: \R \to \R$ be an odd function.
Let $f$ be defined at the point $x = 0$.

Then:

$\map f 0 = 0$


Proof
By definition of odd function:

$\map f {-x} = -\map f x$
and so:














\(\ds \map f {-0}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map f 0\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map f 0\)









The only real number $a$ for which $a = -a$ is $0$.
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
