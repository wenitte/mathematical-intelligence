Theorem
Let $\struct {F, +, \times}$ be a field whose zero is $0_F$ and whose unity is $1_F$.
Let $a, b, x \in F$ such that $b \ne 0_F$.

Let:

$b \times x = a$
Then:

$x = a b^{-1}$
That is:

$x = \dfrac a b$
where $\dfrac a b$ denotes division.


Proof













\(\ds b \times x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a\)





with $b \ne 0_F$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds b^{-1} \times \paren {b \times x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b^{-1} \times a\)





multiplying both sides by $b^{-1}$, which exists because $b \ne 0$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {b^{-1} \times b} \times x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b^{-1} \times a\)





Field Axiom $\text M1$: Associativity of Product








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 1_F \times x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b^{-1} \times a\)





Field Axiom $\text M4$: Inverses for Product








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b^{-1} \times a\)





Field Axiom $\text M3$: Identity  for Product



$\blacksquare$
