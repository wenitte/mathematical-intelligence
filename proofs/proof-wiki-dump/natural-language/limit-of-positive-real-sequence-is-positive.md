Theorem
Let $\sequence {x_n}$ be a sequence of positive real numbers.
Let $x_n$ converge to $L$.

Then $L \ge 0$.


Proof

Then for any $n \in \N$:














\(\ds \size {x_n - L}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x_n - L\)





$x_n \ge 0 > L$














\(\ds \)

\(\ge\)







\(\ds -L\)





$> 0$



This contradicts Definition of Convergent Real Sequence.

Hence we must have $L \ge 0$.
$\blacksquare$
