Theorem
The magic constant of the smallest prime magic square is $111$.


Proof
The smallest prime magic square (including $1$) is:

$\begin{array}{|c|c|c|}
\hline 67 & 1 & 43 \\
\hline 13 & 37 & 61 \\
\hline 31 & 73 & 7 \\
\hline
\end{array}$

As can be seen by inspection, the sums of the elements in the rows, columns and diagonals are $111$:














\(\ds 67 + 1 + 43\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)




















\(\ds 13 + 37 + 61\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)




















\(\ds 31 + 73 + 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)
























\(\ds 67 + 13 + 31\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)




















\(\ds 1 + 37 + 73\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)




















\(\ds 43 + 61 + 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)
























\(\ds 67 + 37 + 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)




















\(\ds 43 + 37 + 31\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 111\)









$\blacksquare$
