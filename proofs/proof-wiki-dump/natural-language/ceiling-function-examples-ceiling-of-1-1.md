Theorem
$\ceiling {-1 \cdotp 1} = -1$
where $\ceiling x$ denotes the ceiling of $x$.


Proof
We have that:

$-2 < -1 \cdotp 1 \le -1$
Hence $-1$ is the ceiling of $-1 \cdotp 1$ by definition.
$\blacksquare$


Also see
Floor of $1 \cdotp 1$: $\floor {1 \cdotp 1} = 1$
Floor of $-1 \cdotp 1$: $\floor {-1 \cdotp 1} = -2$
