Theorem
The inverse tangent of $i$ is not defined.


Proof
Aiming for a contradiction, suppose $\tan z_0 = i$.














\(\ds \dfrac {\sin z_0} {\cos z_0}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i\)





Definition of Tangent Function








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sin z_0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \cos z_0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sin^2 z_0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cos^2 z_0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sin^2 z_0 + \cos^2 z_0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









This contradicts the theorem Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine:

$\sin^2 z_0 + \cos^2 z_0 = 1$
Hence the result by Proof by Contradiction.
$\blacksquare$
