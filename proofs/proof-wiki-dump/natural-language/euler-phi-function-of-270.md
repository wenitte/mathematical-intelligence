Example of Use of Euler $\phi$ Function
$\map \phi {270} = 72$
where $\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ Function.


Proof
From Euler Phi Function of Integer:

$\ds \map \phi n = n \prod_{p \mathop \divides n} \paren {1 - \frac 1 p}$
where $p \divides n$ denotes the primes which divide $n$.

We have that:

$270 = 3 \times 3^3 \times 5$

Thus:














\(\ds \map \phi {270}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 270 \paren {1 - \dfrac 1 2} \paren {1 - \dfrac 1 3} \paren {1 - \dfrac 1 5}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 270 \times \frac 1 2 \times \frac 2 3 \times \frac 4 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9 \times 1 \times 2 \times 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 72\)









$\blacksquare$
