Theorem
$\sin x = \dfrac {e^{i x} - e^{-i x} } {2 i}$


Proof



\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds e^{i x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos x + i \sin x\)





Euler's Formula




\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds e^{-i x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos x - i \sin x\)





Euler's Formula: Corollary








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds e^{i x} - e^{-i x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\cos x + i \sin x} - \paren {\cos x - i \sin x}\)





$(1) - (2)$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 i \sin x\)





simplifying








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {e^{i x} - e^{-i x} } {2 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin x\)









$\blacksquare$
