Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {56} = 120$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$56 = 2^3 \times 7$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {56}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2^4 - 1} {2 - 1} \times \frac {7^2 - 1} {7 - 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {16 - 1} 1 \times \frac {49 - 1} 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 15 \times 8\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {3 \times 5} \times 2^3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 120\)









$\blacksquare$
