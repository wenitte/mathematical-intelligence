Theorem
Let $\struct {D, +, \circ}$ be an integral domain.
Let $U_D$ be the group of units of $D$.
Let $\ideal x$ be the principal ideal of $D$ generated by $x$.
Let $x, y \in \struct {D, +, \circ}$.

Then:

$x \in U_D \iff \ideal x = D$


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds U_D\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists u \in U_D: \, \)



\(\ds u\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \ideal x\)





Ideal of Unit is Whole Ring








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \ideal x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds D\)





Ideal of Unit is Whole Ring




Conversely:














\(\ds \ideal x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds D\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 1_D\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \ideal x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists t \in D: \, \)



\(\ds 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds t x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds u\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds U_D\)









$\blacksquare$
