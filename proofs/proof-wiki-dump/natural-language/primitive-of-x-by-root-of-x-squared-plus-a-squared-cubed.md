Theorem
$\ds \int x \paren {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }^3 \rd x = \frac {\paren {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }^5} 5 + C$


Proof
Let:














\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^2 + a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x\)





Power Rule for Derivatives








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int x \paren {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }^3 \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {z^{3/2} } 2 \rd z\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \frac {z^{5/2} } {\frac 5 2} + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {z^{5/2} } 5 + C\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }^5} 5 + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
