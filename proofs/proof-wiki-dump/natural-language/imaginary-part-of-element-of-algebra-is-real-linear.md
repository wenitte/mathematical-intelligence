Theorem
Let $\struct {A, \ast}$ be a $\ast$-algebra over $\C$.
Let $x, y \in A$ and $\lambda \in \R$.

Then we have:

$\map \Im {x + \lambda y} = \map \Im x + \lambda \map \Im y$
where $\Im$ denotes real part. 


Proof
We have:














\(\ds \map \Im {x + \lambda y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {x + \lambda y - \paren {x + \lambda y}^\ast} {2 i}\)





Definition of Real Part of Element of *-Algebra














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {x + \lambda y - x^\ast - \lambda y^\ast} {2 i}\)





$(\text C^\ast 2)$ and $(\text C^\ast 4)$ in Definition of Involution on Algebra, Complex Number equals Conjugate iff Wholly Real














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {x - x^\ast} 2 + \lambda \frac {y - y^\ast} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Im x + \lambda \map \Im y\)





Definition of Real Part of Element of *-Algebra



$\blacksquare$
