Theorem
$\map \psi {\dfrac 1 2} = -\gamma - 2 \ln 2$


Proof













\(\ds \sum_{k \mathop = 1}^{n - 1} \map \psi {\frac k n}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {n - 1} \gamma - n \ln n\)





Digamma Additive Formula: Corollary








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sum_{k \mathop = 1}^{2 - 1} \map \psi {\frac k 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {2 - 1} \gamma - 2 \ln 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \psi {\frac 1 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\gamma - 2 \ln 2\)









$\blacksquare$
