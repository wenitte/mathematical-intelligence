Theorem
Let $S$ be a set.
Let $I_S: S \to S$ be the identity mapping on $S$.

Then the inverse of $I_S$ is itself:

$\paren {I_S}^{-1} = I_S$


Proof
From the nature of the identity mapping, we have:

$I_S \circ I_S = I_S$
from which it follows by definition that $I_S$ is the inverse of itself.
$\blacksquare$
