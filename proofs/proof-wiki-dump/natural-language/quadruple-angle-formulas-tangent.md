Theorem
$\tan 4 \theta = \dfrac {4 \tan \theta - 4 \tan^3 \theta} {1 - 6 \tan^2 \theta + \tan^4 \theta}$
where $\tan$ denotes tangent.


Proof













\(\ds \tan 4 \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin 4 \theta} {\cos 4 \theta}\)





Tangent is Sine divided by Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \sin \theta \cos \theta - 8 \sin^3 \theta \cos \theta} {8 \cos^4 \theta - 8 \cos^2 \theta + 1}\)





Quadruple Angle Formula for Sine and Quadruple Angle Formula for Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \frac {\tan \theta} {\cos^2 \theta} - 8 \tan^3 \theta} {8 - \frac 8 {\cos^2 \theta} + \frac 1 {\cos^4 \theta} }\)





dividing top and bottom by $\cos^4 \theta$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \tan \theta \sec^2 \theta - 8 \tan^3 \theta} {8 - 8 \sec^2 \theta + \sec^4 \theta}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \tan \theta \paren {1 + \tan^2 \theta} - 8 \tan^3 \theta} {8 - 8 \paren {1 + \tan^2 \theta} + \paren {1 + \tan^2 \theta}^2}\)





Difference of Squares of Secant and Tangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \tan \theta - 4 \tan^3 \theta} {1 - 6 \tan^2 \theta + \tan^4 \theta}\)





multiplying out and gathering terms



$\blacksquare$
