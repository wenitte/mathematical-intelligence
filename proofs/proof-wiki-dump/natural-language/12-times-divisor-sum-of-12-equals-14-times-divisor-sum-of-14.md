Theorem
$x = 12$ and $y = 14$ are solutions to the indeterminate equation:

$x \, \map {\sigma_1} x = y \, \map {\sigma_1} y$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof













\(\ds 12 \, \map {\sigma_1} {12}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \times 28\)





$\sigma_1$ of $12$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \times 2 \times 14\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 14 \times 24\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 14 \, \map {\sigma_1} {14}\)





$\sigma_1$ of $14$



$\blacksquare$
