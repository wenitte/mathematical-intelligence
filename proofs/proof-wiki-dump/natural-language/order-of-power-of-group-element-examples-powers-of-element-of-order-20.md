Example of Order of Power of Group Element
Let $G$ be a group.
Let $x \in G$ be such that:

$\order x = 20$
where $\order x$ denotes the order of $x$ in $G$.
Then:




\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^4}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^{10} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)










\(\text {(3)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^{12} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)











Proof
From Order of Power of Group Element:

$\order {x^m} = \dfrac {\order x} {\gcd \set {m, \order x} }$

Here we have that $\order x = 20$.
Thus:




\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^4}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {20} {\gcd \set {4, 20} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {20} 4\)





as $\gcd \set {4, 20} = 4$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^{10} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {20} {\gcd \set {10, 20} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {20} {10}\)





as $\gcd \set {10, 20} = 10$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)










\(\text {(3)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \order {x^{12} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {20} 4\)





as $\gcd \set {12, 20} = 4$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)









$\blacksquare$
