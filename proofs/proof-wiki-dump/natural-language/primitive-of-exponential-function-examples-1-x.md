Example of Use of Primitive of Exponential Function
$\ds \int e^{1 - x} \rd x = -e^{1 - x} + C$


Proof
From Primitive of Function of $a x + b$:

$\ds \int \map F {a x + b} \rd x = \frac 1 a \int \map F u \rd u$
where $u = a x + b$.

Hence:














\(\ds \ds \int e^{1 - x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {-1} \ds \int e^{1 - x} \map {\rd} {1 - x}\)





Primitive of Function of $a x + b$, setting $a \gets -1$, $b \gets 1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -e^{1 - x} + C\)





Primitive of Exponential Function



$\blacksquare$


Proof
