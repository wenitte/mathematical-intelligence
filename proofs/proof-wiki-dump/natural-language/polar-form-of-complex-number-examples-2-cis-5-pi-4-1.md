Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $\polar {2, \dfrac {5 \pi} 4}$ can be expressed in Cartesian form as:

$2 \cis \dfrac {5 \pi} 4 = -\sqrt 2 - \sqrt 2 i$
and depicted in the complex plane as:





Proof













\(\ds 2 \cis \dfrac {5 \pi} 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \paren {\cos \dfrac {5 \pi} 4 + i \sin \dfrac {5 \pi} 4}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times \paren {\dfrac {-\sqrt 2} 2 + \dfrac {-\sqrt 2} 2 i}\)





Cosine of $225 \degrees$ and Sine of $225 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sqrt 2 - \sqrt 2 i\)









$\blacksquare$
