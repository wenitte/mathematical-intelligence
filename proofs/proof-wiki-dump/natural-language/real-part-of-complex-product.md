Theorem
Let $z_1$ and $z_2$ be complex numbers.
Then:

$\map \Re {z_1 z_2} = \map \Re {z_1} \map \Re {z_2} - \map \Im {z_1} \map \Im {z_2}$


Proof
Let $z_1 = x_1 + i y_1$ and $z_2 = x_2 + i y_2$.
By definition of complex multiplication:

$z_1 z_2 = x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2 + i \paren {x_1 y_2 + x_2 y_1}$

Then:














\(\ds \map \Re {z_1 z_2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2\)





Definition of Real Part














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {z_1} \map \Re {z_2} - \map \Im {z_1} \map \Im {z_2}\)





Definition of Real Part



$\blacksquare$
