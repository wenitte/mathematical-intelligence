Theorem
$\sec i = \dfrac {2 e} {e^2 + 1}$
where $\sec$ denotes the complex secant function and $i$ is the imaginary unit.


Proof 1













\(\ds \sec i\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos i}\)





Definition of Complex Secant Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac e 2 + \frac 1 {2 e} }\)





Cosine of $i$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 e} {e^2 + 1}\)





multiplying denominator and numerator by $2 e$



$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \sec i\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sech 1\)





Hyperbolic Secant in terms of Secant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 2 {e^1 + e^{-1} }\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Secant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 e} {e^2 + 1}\)





multiplying denominator and numerator by $e$



$\blacksquare$
