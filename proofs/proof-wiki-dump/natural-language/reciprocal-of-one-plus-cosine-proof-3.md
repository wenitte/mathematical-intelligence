Theorem
$\dfrac 1 {1 + \cos x} = \dfrac 1 2 \sec^2 \dfrac x 2$


Proof













\(\ds \frac 1 {1 + \cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {1 + \frac {1- \tan^2 \frac x 2} {1 + \tan^2 \frac x 2} }\)





Tangent Half-Angle Substitution for Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {1 + \tan^2 \frac x 2} 2\)





multiplying through $\frac {1 + \tan^2 \frac x 2} {1 + \tan^2 \frac x 2}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \sec^2 \frac x 2\)





Difference of Squares of Secant and Tangent



$\blacksquare$
