Theorem
Let $a \in \R_{>0}$ be a (strictly) positive real number.

Let $x, y \in \R$ be real numbers.
Let $a^x$ be defined as $a$ to the power of $x$.

Then:

$\paren {a^x}^y = a^{x y}$


Proof













\(\ds a^{x y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp {x y \ln a}\)





Definition of Power to Real Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp {y \, \map \ln {a^x} }\)





Logarithms of Powers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a^x}^y\)





Definition of Power to Real Number



$\blacksquare$
