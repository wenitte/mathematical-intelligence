Theorem
$\map \cos {x + \dfrac \pi 2} = -\sin x$


Proof













\(\ds \map \cos {x + \frac \pi 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos x \cos \frac \pi 2 - \sin x \sin \frac \pi 2\)





Cosine of Sum














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos x \cdot 0 - \sin x \cdot 1\)





Cosine of Right Angle and Sine of Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sin x\)









$\blacksquare$
