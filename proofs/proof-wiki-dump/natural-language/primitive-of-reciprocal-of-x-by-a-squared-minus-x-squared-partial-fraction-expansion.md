Lemma for Primitive of Reciprocal of $x \paren {a^2 - x^2}$
$\dfrac 1 {x \paren {a^2 - x^2} } \equiv \dfrac 1 {a^2 x} + \dfrac x {a^2 \paren {a^2 - x^2} }$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {x \paren {a^2 - x^2} }\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds \dfrac A x + \dfrac {B x + C} {a^2 - x^2}\)










\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)



\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 1\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds A \paren {a^2 - x^2} + B x^2 + C x\)





multiplying through by $x \paren {a^2 - x^2}$




Setting $x = 0$ in $(1)$:














\(\ds A a^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds A\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a^2}\)










Equating coefficients of $x^2$ in $(1)$:














\(\ds 0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -A + B\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds B\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a^2}\)










Equating coefficients of $x$ in $(1)$:














\(\ds 0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds C\)










Summarising:














\(\ds A\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a^2}\)




















\(\ds B\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a^2}\)




















\(\ds C\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
