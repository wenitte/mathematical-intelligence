Theorem
The $9$ integers beginning $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 044$ each has $48$ divisors.


Proof
In the below, $\sigma_0$ denotes the divisor count function.















\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 044}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 044$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 045}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 045$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 046}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 046$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 047}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 047$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 048}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 048$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 049}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 049$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 050}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 050$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 051}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 051$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 052}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 052$




but then:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 043}\)

\(\ds 32\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 043$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 053}\)

\(\ds 8\)





$\sigma_0$ of $17 \, 796 \, 126 \, 877 \, 482 \, 329 \, 126 \, 053$



$\blacksquare$
