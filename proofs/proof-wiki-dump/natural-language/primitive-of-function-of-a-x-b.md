Theorem
$\ds \int \map F {a x + b} \rd x = \frac 1 a \int \map F u \rd u$
where $u = a x + b$.


Proof













\(\ds u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a x + b\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a\)





Derivative of Function of Constant Multiple: Corollary








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \map F {a x + b} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\map F u} a \d u\)





Primitive of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \int \map F u \rd u\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function



$\blacksquare$
