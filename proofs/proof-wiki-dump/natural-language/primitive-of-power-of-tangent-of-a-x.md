Theorem
$\ds \int \tan^n a x \rd x = \frac {\tan^{n - 1} a x} {\paren {n - 1} a} - \int \tan^{n - 2} a x \rd x$
for $n \ne 1$.


Proof













\(\ds \int \tan^n a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \tan^{n - 2} a x \tan^2 a x \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \tan^{n - 2} a x \paren {\sec^2 a x - 1} \rd x\)





Difference of Squares of Secant and Tangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \tan^{n - 2} a x \sec^2 a x \rd x - \int \tan^{n - 2} \rd x\)





Linear Combination of Primitives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\tan^{n - 1} a x} {\paren {n - 1} a} - \int \tan^{n - 2} \rd x\)





Primitive of $\tan^n a x \sec^2 a x$



$\blacksquare$
