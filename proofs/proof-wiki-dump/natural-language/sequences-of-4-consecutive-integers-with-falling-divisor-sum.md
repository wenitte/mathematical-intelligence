Theorem
The following ordered quadruple of consecutive integers have divisor sums which are strictly decreasing:

$44, 45, 46, 47$
$104, 105, 106, 107$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {44}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 84\)





$\sigma_1$ of $44$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {45}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 78\)





$\sigma_1$ of $45$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {46}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 72\)





$\sigma_1$ of $46$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {47}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 48\)





Divisor Sum of Prime Number: $47$ is prime


















\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {104}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 210\)





$\sigma_1$ of $104$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {105}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 192\)





$\sigma_1$ of $105$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {106}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 162\)





$\sigma_1$ of $106$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {107}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 108\)





Divisor Sum of Prime Number: $107$ is prime



$\blacksquare$
