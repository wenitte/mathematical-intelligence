Theorem
The inverse of an inverse relation is the relation itself:

$\paren {\RR^{-1} }^{-1} = \RR$


Proof













\(\ds \tuple {s, t}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {t, s}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR^{-1}\)





Definition of Inverse Relation








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {s, t}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \paren {\RR^{-1} }^{-1}\)





Definition of Inverse Relation



$\blacksquare$
