Example of Lowest Common Multiple of Integers
The lowest common multiple of $7$, $9$, $12$ and $14$ is:

$\lcm \set {7, 9, 12, 14} = 252$


Proof













\(\ds 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7^1\)




















\(\ds 9\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3^2\)




















\(\ds 12\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^2 \times 3\)




















\(\ds 14\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 7\)




















\(\ds \lcm \set {7, 9, 12, 14}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^2 \times 3^2 \times 7\)





LCM from Prime Decomposition














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 252\)









Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
