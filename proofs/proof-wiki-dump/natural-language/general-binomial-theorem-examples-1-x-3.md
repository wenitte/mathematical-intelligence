Example of Use of General Binomial Theorem
$\paren {1 - x}^{-3} = 1 + 3 x + 6 x^2 + 10 x^3 + \cdots$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {1 - x}^{-3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{n \mathop = 0}^\infty \frac {\paren {-3}^{\underline n} } {n!} \paren {-x}^n\)





General Binomial Theorem














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + \paren {-3} \paren {-x} + \dfrac {\paren {-3} \paren {-4} } {2!} \paren {-x}^2 + \dfrac {\paren {-3} \paren {-4} \paren {-5} } {3!} \paren {-x}^3 + \cdots\)





expanding term by term














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + 3 x + \dfrac {12} 2 x^2 + \dfrac {60} 6 x^3 + \cdots\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + 3 x + 6 x^2 + 10 x^3 + \cdots\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
