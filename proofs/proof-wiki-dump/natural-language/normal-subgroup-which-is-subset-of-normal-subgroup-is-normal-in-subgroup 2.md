Theorem
Let $G$ be a group.
Let $H$ and $K$ be normal subgroups of $G$.
Let $H \subseteq K$.
Then $H$ is a normal subgroup of $K$.


Proof









\(\ds \forall g \in G: \, \)



\(\ds g K\)

\(=\)







\(\ds K g\)





Definition of Normal Subgroup










\(\ds \forall g \in G: \, \)



\(\ds g H\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H g\)





Definition of Normal Subgroup








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall g \in K: \, \)



\(\ds g H\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H g\)





as $K \subseteq G$



Hence the result by definition of normal subgroup.
$\blacksquare$
