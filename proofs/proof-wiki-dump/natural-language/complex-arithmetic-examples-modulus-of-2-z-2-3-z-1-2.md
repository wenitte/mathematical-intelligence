Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 1 - i$ and $z_2 = -2 + 4 i$.
Then:

$\cmod {2 z_2 - 3 z_1}^2 = 170$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {2 z_2 - 3 z_1}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {2 \paren {-2 + 4 i} - 3 \paren {1 - i} }^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\paren {-4 + 8 i} - \paren {3 - 3 i} }^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {-7 + 11 i}^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7^2 + 11^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 49 + 121\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 170\)









$\blacksquare$
