Theorem
Let $x_1, p, k \in Z_{\gt 0}$ be any positive integers.
Let $a = x_1^k + p$
Let $\map f X \in \Z \sqbrk X$ be the polynomial:

$X^k - a$
Then:

$\map f {x_1} \equiv 0 \pmod p$


Proof













\(\ds \map f {x_1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x_1^k - \paren {x_1^k + p}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x_1^k - x_1^k} - p\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -p\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 0 \pmod p\)









$\blacksquare$
