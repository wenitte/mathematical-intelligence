Example of Partial Derivative
Let $u^2 + x^2 + y^2 = a^2$ be an implicit function.
Then:














\(\ds \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac x u\)




















\(\ds \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac y u\)











Proof













\(\ds u^2 + x^2 + y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac \partial {\partial x} u^2 + \dfrac \partial {\partial x} x^2 + \dfrac \partial {\partial x} y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \partial {\partial x} a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 u \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial x} + 2 x + 0\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac x u\)









and:














\(\ds u^2 + x^2 + y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac \partial {\partial y} u^2 + \dfrac \partial {\partial y} x^2 + \dfrac \partial {\partial y} y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \partial {\partial y} a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 u \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial y} + 0 + 2 y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac {\partial u} {\partial y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac y u\)









$\blacksquare$
