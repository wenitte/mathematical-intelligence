Theorem
Let $T = \struct {S, \tau}$ be a topological space.
Let $A, B \subseteq S$ such that $A$ and $B$ are separated in $T$.

Then $A$ and $B$ are disjoint:

$A \cap B  = \O$


Proof
Let $A$ and $B$ be separated in $T$.

Then:














\(\ds A^- \cap B\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \O\)





Definition of Separated Sets: $A^-$ is the closure of $A$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {A \cup A'} \cap B\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \O\)





Definition of Set Closure: $A'$ is the derived set of $A$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {A \cap B} \cup \paren {A' \cap B}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \O\)





Intersection Distributes over Union








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds A \cap B\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \O\)





Union is Empty iff Sets are Empty



$\blacksquare$
