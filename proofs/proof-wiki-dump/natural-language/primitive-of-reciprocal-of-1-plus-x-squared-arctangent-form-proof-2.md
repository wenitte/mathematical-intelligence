Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {1 + x^2} = \arctan x + C$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\arctan x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {1 + x^2}\)





Derivative of Arctangent Function








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \dfrac {\d x} {1 + x^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \arctan x + C\)





Definition of Primitive (Calculus)



$\blacksquare$
