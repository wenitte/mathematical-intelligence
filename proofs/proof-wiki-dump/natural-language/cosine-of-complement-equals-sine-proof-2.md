Theorem
$\map \cos {\dfrac \pi 2 - \theta} = \sin \theta$


Proof













\(\ds \map \cos {\frac \pi 2 - \theta}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {\theta - \frac \pi 2}\)





Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \sin {\theta - \frac \pi 2 + \frac \pi 2}\)





Sine of Angle plus Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin \theta\)









$\blacksquare$
