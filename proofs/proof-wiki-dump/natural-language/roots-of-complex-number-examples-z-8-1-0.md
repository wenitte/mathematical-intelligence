Theorem
The roots of the polynomial:

$z^8 + 1 = 0$
are:

$\set {\cos \dfrac {\paren {2 k + 1} \pi} 8 + i \sin \dfrac {\paren {2 k + 1} \pi} 8: k \in \set {0, 1, \ldots, 7} }$


Proof













\(\ds z^8 + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {-1}^{1/8}\)










From Euler's Identity:

$-1 = e^{i \pi}$

Let $b$ be defined as:














\(\ds b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt [8] 1 \paren {\cos \dfrac \pi 8 + i \sin \dfrac \pi 8}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \dfrac \pi 8 + i \sin \dfrac \pi 8\)










From Roots of Complex Number: Corollary:

$\paren {-1}^{1/8} = \cos \paren {\dfrac \pi 8 + \dfrac {2 k \pi} 8} + i \sin \paren {\dfrac \pi 8 + \dfrac {2 k \pi} 8}$
for $k = 0$ to $7$.














\(\ds \paren {-1}^{1/8}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\cos \paren {\dfrac \pi 8 + \dfrac {2 k \pi} 8} + i \sin \paren {\dfrac \pi 8 + \dfrac {2 k \pi} 8}: k \in \set {0, 1, \ldots, 7} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\cos \dfrac {\paren {2 k + 1} \pi} 8 + i \sin \dfrac {\paren {2 k + 1} \pi} 8: k \in \set {0, 1, \ldots, 7} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\exp \dfrac {\paren {2 k + 1} i \pi} 8: k \in \set {0, 1, \ldots, 7} }\)









$\blacksquare$
