Theorem
$\ds \int \map F {e^{a x} } \rd x = \frac 1 a \int \frac {\map F u} u \rd u$
where $u = e^{a x}$.


Proof













\(\ds u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{a x}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a e^{a x}\)





Derivative of Exponential of a x














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a u\)





Definition of $u$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \map F {e^{a x} } \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\map F u} {a u} \rd u\)





Primitive of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \int \frac {\map F u} u \rd u\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function



$\blacksquare$
