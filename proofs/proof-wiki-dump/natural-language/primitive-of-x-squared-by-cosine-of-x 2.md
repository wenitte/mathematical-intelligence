Theorem
$\ds \int x^2 \cos x \rd x = x^2 \sin x + 2 x \cos x + 2 \sin x + C$


Proof
From Primitive of $x^2 \cos a x$:

$\ds \int x^2 \cos a x \rd x = \frac {2 x \cos a x} {a^2} + \paren {\frac {x^2} a - \frac 2 {a^3} } \sin a x + C$

The result follows on setting $a = 1$.
$\blacksquare$
