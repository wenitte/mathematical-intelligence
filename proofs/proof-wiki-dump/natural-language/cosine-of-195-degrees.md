Theorem
$\cos 195 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {13 \pi} {12} = - \dfrac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} 4$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 195 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \paren {360 \degrees - 165 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos 165 \degrees\)





Cosine of Conjugate Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds - \frac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} 4\)





Cosine of $165 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
