Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Then:

$i \tanh z = \map \tan {i z}$
where:

$\tan$ denotes the tangent function
$\tanh$ denotes the hyperbolic tangent
$i$ is the imaginary unit: $i^2 = -1$.


Proof













\(\ds \map \tan {i z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\map \sin {i z} } {\map \cos {i z} }\)





Definition of Complex Tangent Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {i \sinh z} {\map \cos {i z} }\)





Hyperbolic Sine in terms of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {i \sinh z} {\cosh z}\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \tanh z\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Tangent



$\blacksquare$
