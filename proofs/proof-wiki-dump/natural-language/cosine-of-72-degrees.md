Theorem
$\cos 72 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {2 \pi} 5 = \dfrac {\sqrt 5 - 1} 4$
where $\cos$ denotes the cosine function.


Proof 1













\(\ds \cos 72 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {90 \degrees - 18 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin 18 \degrees\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 5 - 1} 4\)





Sine of $18 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \cos 72 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \cos 36 \degrees - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \paren {\dfrac \phi 2}^2 - 1\)





Cosine of $36 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi^2} 2 - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi + 1} 2 - 1\)





Square of Golden Mean equals One plus Golden Mean














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi - 1} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac {1 - \phi} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi^{-1} } 2\)





Reciprocal Form of One Minus Golden Mean














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 5 - 1} 4\)





Definition 2 of Golden Mean, and algebra



$\blacksquare$
