Example of Derivative of Composite Function
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {a^{\sin x} } = \cos x a^{\sin x} \ln a$


Proof
Let $u = \sin x$.
Let $y = a^u$.
Thus we have:

$y = a^{\sin x}$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d u} \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^u \ln a \cdot \cos x\)





Derivative of General Exponential Function, Derivative of Sine Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos x a^{\sin x} \ln a\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
