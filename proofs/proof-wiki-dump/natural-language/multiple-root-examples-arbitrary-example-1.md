Example of Multiple Root
Consider the cubic equation $\EE$:

$\EE: \quad x^3 - 3 x^2 + 4 = 0$
$\EE$ has two solutions: $-1$ and $2$.
$2$ is a multiple root of $\EE$ with multiplicity $2$.


Proof
We have:










\(\ds \EE: \, \)



\(\ds x^3 - 3 x^2 + 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x - 2} \paren {x - 2} \paren {x + 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)





factoring



Hence $2$ appears twice.
Thus $2$ by definition has a multiplicity $2$, that is, $2$ is a double root of $\EE$.
$\blacksquare$
