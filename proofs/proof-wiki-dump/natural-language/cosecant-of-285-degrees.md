Theorem
$\csc 285 \degrees = \csc \dfrac {19 \pi} {12} = -\paren {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2}$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.


Proof













\(\ds \csc 285 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \csc {360 \degrees - 75 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\csc 75 \degrees\)





Cosecant of Conjugate Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2}\)





Cosecant of $75 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
