Theorem
The quadratic form:

$2 a^2 + 3$
yields prime numbers for $a = 0, 1, 2$.


Proof













\(\ds 2 \times 0^2 + 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 + 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 1^2 + 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 2^2 + 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 4 + 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 8 + 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 11\)





which is prime



$\blacksquare$
