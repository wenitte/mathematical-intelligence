Theorem
Let $z \in \C$, and let $k \in \Z$.

Then:

$\map \exp {z + 2 k \pi i} = \map \exp z$


Proof













\(\ds \map \exp {z + 2 k \pi i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z \, \map \exp {2 k \pi i}\)





Exponential of Sum: Complex Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z \times 1\)





Euler's Formula Example: $e^{2 k i \pi}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \exp z\)









$\blacksquare$
