Theorem
Let $u$ be a differentiable real function of $x$ such that $\size u > 1$.
Then:

$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\arcsec u} = \dfrac 1 {\size u \sqrt {u^2 - 1} } \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}$
where $\arcsec$ denotes the arcsecant of $x$.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\frac \d {\d x} } {\arcsec u}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\frac \d {\d u} } {\arcsec u} \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Chain Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\size u \sqrt {u^2 - 1} } \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Arcsecant Function



$\blacksquare$
