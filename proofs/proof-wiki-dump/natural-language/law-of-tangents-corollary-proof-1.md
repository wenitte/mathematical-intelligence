Corollary to Law of Tangents
$\tan \dfrac {A - B} 2 = \dfrac {a - b} {a + b} \cot \dfrac C 2$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {a + b} {a - b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\tan \frac 1 2 \paren {A + B} } {\tan \frac 1 2 \paren {A - B} }\)





Law of Tangents








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tan \frac {A - B} 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - b} {a + b} \tan \frac {A + B} 2\)





algebraic manipulation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - b} {a + b} \tan \frac {180 \degrees - C} 2\)





Sum of Angles of Triangle equals Two Right Angles














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - b} {a + b} \map \tan {90 \degrees - \frac C 2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - b} {a + b} \cot \dfrac C 2\)





Tangent of Complement equals Cotangent



$\blacksquare$
