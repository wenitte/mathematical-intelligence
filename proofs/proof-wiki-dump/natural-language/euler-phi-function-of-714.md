Example of Euler $\phi$ Function of Square-Free Integer
$\map \phi {714} = 192$
where $\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ Function.


Proof
From Euler Phi Function of Square-Free Integer:

$\ds \map \phi n = \prod_{\substack {p \mathop \divides n \\ p \mathop > 2} } \paren {p - 1}$
where $p \divides n$ denotes the primes which divide $n$.

We have that:

$714 = 2 \times 3 \times 7 \times 17$
and so is square-free.

Thus:














\(\ds \map \phi {714}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {3 - 1} \paren {7 - 1} \paren {17 - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 6 \times 16\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times \paren {2 \times 3} \times 2^4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^6 \times 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 192\)









$\blacksquare$
