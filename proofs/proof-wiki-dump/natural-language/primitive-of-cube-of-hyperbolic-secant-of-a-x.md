Theorem
$\ds \int \sech^3 a x \rd x = \frac {\sech a x \tanh a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 {2 a} \map \arctan {\sinh a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \sech^3 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sech a x \tanh a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 2 \int \sech a x \rd x\)





Primitive of $\sech^n a x$ where $n = 3$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sech a x \tanh a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 {2 a} \map \arctan {\sinh a x} + C\)





Primitive of $\sech a x$



$\blacksquare$
