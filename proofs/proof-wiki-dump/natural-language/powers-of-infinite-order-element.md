Theorem
Let $G$ be a group whose identity is $e$.
Let $a \in G$ have infinite order in $G$.

Then:

$\forall m, n \in \Z: m \ne n \implies a^m \ne a^n$


Proof
Let $m, n \in \Z$.
Then:














\(\ds a^m\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^n\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a^m \paren {a^n}^{-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e\)


















\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds a^n \paren {a^m}^{-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a^{m - n}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{n - m} = e\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a^{\size {m - n} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \size {m - n}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)





Definition of Infinite Order Element: there exists no $x \in \Z_{>0}$ such that $a^x = e$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds m\)

\(=\)







\(\ds n\)









The result follows from transposition.
$\blacksquare$
