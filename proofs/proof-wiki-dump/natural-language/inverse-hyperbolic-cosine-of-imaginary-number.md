Theorem
$\cosh^{-1} x = \pm \, i \cos^{-1} x$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh^{-1} x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cosh y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Definition of Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \cosh {\pm \, y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Hyperbolic Cosine Function is Even








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \cos {\pm \, i y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \pm \, i y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos^{-1} x\)





Definition of Inverse Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm \, i \cos^{-1} x\)





multiplying both sides by $\pm \, i$



$\blacksquare$
