Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\paren {a x + b}^2} = -\frac 1 {a \paren {a x + b} } + C$


Proof
Let $u = a x + b$.
Then:














\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\paren {a x + b}^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \int \frac {\d u} {u^2}\)





Primitive of Function of $a x + b$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \frac {-1} u + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 {a \paren {a x + b} } + C\)





substituting for $u$



$\blacksquare$
