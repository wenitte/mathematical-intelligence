Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $-1 + \sqrt 3 i$ can be expressed as a complex number in polar form as $\polar {2, \dfrac {2 \pi} 3}$.


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {-1 + \sqrt 3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\paren {-1}^2 + \paren {\sqrt 3}^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {1 + 3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)










Then:














\(\ds \map \cos {\map \arg {-1 + \sqrt 3 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-1} 2\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-1 + \sqrt 3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {2 \pi} 3 \text { or } \dfrac {4 \pi} 3\)





Cosine of $120 \degrees$, Cosine of $240 \degrees$


















\(\ds \map \sin {\map \arg {-1 + \sqrt 3 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-1 + \sqrt 3 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \pi 3 \text { or } \dfrac {2 \pi} 3\)





Sine of $60 \degrees$, Sine of $120 \degrees$




Hence:

$\map \arg {-1 + \sqrt 3 i} = \dfrac {2 \pi} 3$
and hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
