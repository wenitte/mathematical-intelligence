Theorem
Consider the universal negative categorical statement No $S$ is $P$:

$\map {\mathbf E} {S, P}: \forall x: \map S x \implies \neg \map P x$

Then No $P$ is $S$:

$\map {\mathbf E} {P, S}$


Proof













\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \map {\mathbf E} {S, P}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x: \, \)



\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \map S x \implies \neg \map P x\)





Definition of Universal Negative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x: \, \)



\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \neg \paren {\map S x \land \map P x}\)





Modus Ponendo Tollens








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x: \, \)



\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \neg \paren {\map P x \land \map S x}\)





Conjunction is Commutative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x: \, \)



\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \map P x \implies \neg \map S x\)





Modus Ponendo Tollens








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \map {\mathbf E} {P, S}\)





Definition of Universal Negative



$\blacksquare$
