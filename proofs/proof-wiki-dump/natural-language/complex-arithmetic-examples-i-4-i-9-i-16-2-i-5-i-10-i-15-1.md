Example of Complex Arithmetic
$\dfrac {i^4 + i^9 + i^{16} } {2 - i^5 + i^{10} - i^{15} } = 2 + i$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {i^4 + i^9 + i^{16} } {2 - i^5 + i^{10} - i^{15} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\paren {i^4} + \paren {i^4}^2 \times i + \paren {i^4}^4} {2 - \paren {i^4} \times i + \paren {i^4}^2 \times i^2 - \paren {i^4}^3 \times i^3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {1 + i + 1} {2 - i + i^2 - i^3}\)





$i^4 = 1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {2 + i} {2 - i - 1 + i}\)





$i^2 = -1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {2 + i} 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + i\)









$\blacksquare$
