Theorem
$\cot 60 \degrees = \cot \dfrac \pi 3 = \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 3$
where $\cot$ denotes cotangent.


Proof













\(\ds \cot 60 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cos 60 \degrees} {\sin 60 \degrees}\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac 1 2} {\frac {\sqrt 3} 2}\)





Cosine of $60 \degrees$ and Sine of $60 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sqrt 3} 3\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 \sqrt 3$



$\blacksquare$


Also presented as
Some sources present the cotangent of $60 \degrees$ as:

$\cot 60 \degrees = \cot \dfrac \pi 3 = \dfrac 1 {\sqrt 3}$
