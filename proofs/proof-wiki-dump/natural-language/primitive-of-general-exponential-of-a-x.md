Theorem
$\ds \int b^{a x} \rd x = \frac {b^{a x} } {a \ln b} + C$
where:

$a \ne 0$
$b > 0, b \ne 1$


Proof













\(\ds \int b^x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {b^x} {\ln b} + C\)





Primitive of $b^x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int b^{a x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {\frac {b^x} {\ln b} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {b^{a x} } {a \ln b} + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
