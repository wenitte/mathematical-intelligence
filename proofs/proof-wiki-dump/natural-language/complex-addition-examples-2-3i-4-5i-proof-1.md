Example of Complex Addition
$\paren {2 + 3 i} + \paren {4 - 5 i} = 6 - 2 i$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {2 + 3 i} + \paren {4 - 5 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 + 4} + \paren {3 - 5} i\)





Definition of Complex Addition














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 - 2 i\)









$\blacksquare$
