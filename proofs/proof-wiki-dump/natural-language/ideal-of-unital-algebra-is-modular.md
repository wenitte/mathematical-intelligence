Theorem
Let $R$ be a ring. 
Let $A$ be a unital $R$-algebra.
Let $I$ be an ideal of $A$.

Then $I$ is modular. 


Proof
Let ${\mathbf 1}_A$ be the identity element of $A$.
Let ${\mathbf 0}_A$ be the zero vector of $A$.
Then we have:

$a {\mathbf 1}_A = {\mathbf 1}_A a$
for each $a \in A$. 
Hence we have:

$a - {\mathbf 1}_A a = {\mathbf 0}_A$
and:

$a - a {\mathbf 1}_A = {\mathbf 0}_A$
Since $I$ is a submodule of $A$, we have ${\mathbf 0}_A \in A$.
Hence $a - u a \in I$ and $a - a u \in I$ for $u = {\mathbf 1}_A$.
So $I$ is modular.
$\blacksquare$
