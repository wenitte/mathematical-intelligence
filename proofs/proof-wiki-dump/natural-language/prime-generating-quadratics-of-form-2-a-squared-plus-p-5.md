Theorem
The quadratic form:

$2 a^2 + 5$
yields prime numbers for $a = 0, 1, \ldots, 4$.


Proof













\(\ds 2 \times 0^2 + 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 1^2 + 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 2^2 + 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 4 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 8 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 13\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 3^2 + 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 9 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 18 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 23\)





which is prime














\(\ds 2 \times 4^2 + 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 16 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 32 + 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 37\)





which is prime



$\blacksquare$
