Theorem
$\cosh^2 x = \dfrac {\cosh 2 x + 1} 2$
where $\cosh$ denotes hyperbolic cosine.


Proof 1













\(\ds 2 \cosh^2 x - 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh 2 x\)





Double Angle Formula for Hyperbolic Cosine: Corollary $1$














\(\ds \cosh^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 2 x + 1} 2\)





solving for $\cosh^2 x$



$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds \cosh^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 4 \paren {e^x + e^{-x} }^2\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{2 x} + e^{-2 x} + 2} 4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 2 x + 1} 2\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine



$\blacksquare$


Proof 3













\(\ds \cosh^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos^2 i x\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\map \cos {2 i x} + 1} 2\)





Square of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 2 x + 1} 2\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
