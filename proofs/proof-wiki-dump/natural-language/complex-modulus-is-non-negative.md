Theorem
Let $z = a + i b \in \C$ be a complex number.
Let $\cmod z$ be the modulus of $z$.

Then:

$\cmod z \ge 0$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {a + b i}\)





Definition of $z$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds +\sqrt {a^2 + b^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(\ge\)







\(\ds 0\)





Definition of Positive Square Root



$\blacksquare$
