Theorem
Let $\struct {G, \circ}$ be a group which has an identity element $e \in G$.
Then $e$ is unique.


Proof
Let $e$ and $f$ both be identity elements of a group $\struct {G, \circ}$.
Then:














\(\ds e\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e \circ f\)





$f$ is an identity














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds f\)





$e$ is an identity



So $e = f$ and there is only one identity after all.
$\blacksquare$
