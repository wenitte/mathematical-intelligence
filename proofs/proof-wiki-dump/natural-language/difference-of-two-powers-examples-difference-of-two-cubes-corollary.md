Theorem
$x^3 - 1 = \paren {x - 1} \paren {x^2 + x + 1}$


Proof
From Difference of Two Cubes:

$x^3 - y^3 = \paren {x - y} \paren {x^2 + x y + y^2}$
The result follows directly by setting $y = 1$.
$\blacksquare$
