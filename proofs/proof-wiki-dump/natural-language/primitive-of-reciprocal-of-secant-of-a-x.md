Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sec a x} = \frac {\sin a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sec a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \cos a x \rd x\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\cos a x$



$\blacksquare$
