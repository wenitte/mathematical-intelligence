Theorem
$\ds \lim_{x \mathop \to \infty} \map {\mathrm S} x = \frac 1 2$
where $\mathrm S$ denotes the Fresnel sine integral function.


Proof













\(\ds \lim_{x \mathop \to \infty} \map {\mathrm S} x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\frac 2 \pi} \lim_{x \mathop \to \infty} \int_0^x \sin u^2 \rd u\)





Multiple Rule for Limits of Real Functions, Definition of Fresnel Sine Integral Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\frac 2 \pi} \int_0^\infty \sin u^2 \rd u\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\frac 2 \pi} \times \frac 1 2 \sqrt {\frac \pi 2}\)





Definite Integral to Infinity of $\map \sin {a x^2}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2\)









$\blacksquare$
