Theorem
$\dfrac {\partial^2} {\partial r^2} \map \ln {r^2 + s} = \dfrac {2 \paren {s - r^2} } {\paren {r^2 + s}^2}$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {\partial^2} {\partial r^2} \map \ln {r^2 + s}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \partial {\partial r} } {\dfrac \partial {\partial r} \map \ln {r^2 + s} }\)





Definition of Second Partial Derivative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \partial {\partial r} \frac 1 {r^2 + s} 2 r\)





Derivative of Natural Logarithm, Chain Rule for Derivatives, treating $s$ as a constant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 \paren {r^2 + s} - 2 r \paren {2 r} } {\paren {r^2 + s}^2}\)





Quotient Rule for Derivatives, again treating $s$ as a constant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2 \paren {s - r^2} } {\paren {r^2 + s}^2}\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
