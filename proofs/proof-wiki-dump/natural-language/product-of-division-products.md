Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a commutative ring with unity.
Let $\struct {U_R, \circ}$ be the group of units of $\struct {R, +, \circ}$.

Let $a, b \in R, c, d \in U_R$.
Then:

$\dfrac a c \circ \dfrac b d = \dfrac {a \circ b} {c \circ d}$
where $\dfrac x z$ is defined as $x \circ \paren {z^{-1} }$, that is, $x$ divided by $z$.


Proof













\(\ds \frac a c \circ \frac b d\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \circ c^{-1} \circ b \circ d^{-1}\)





Definition of Division Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a \circ b} \circ \paren {d^{-1} \circ c^{-1} }\)





Definition of Commutative Operation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a \circ b} \circ \paren {c \circ d}^{-1}\)





Inverse of Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {a \circ b} {c \circ d}\)





Definition of Division Product



$\blacksquare$
