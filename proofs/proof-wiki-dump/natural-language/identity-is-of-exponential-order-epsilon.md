Theorem
Let $I_\R: t \mapsto t$ be the identity mapping on $\R_{\ge 0}$.

Then $I_\R$ is of exponential order $\epsilon$ for any  $\epsilon > 0$ arbitrarily small in magnitude.

Proof













\(\ds e^{\epsilon t}\)

\(\ge\)







\(\ds 1 + \epsilon t\)





Exponential of $t$ not less than $1 + t$














\(\ds \)

\(>\)







\(\ds \epsilon t\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds K e^{\epsilon t}\)

\(>\)







\(\ds t\)





$K = \dfrac 1 \epsilon$



$\blacksquare$
