Example of Use of GCD from Prime Decomposition
The greatest common divisor of $121$ and $66$ is:

$\gcd \set {121, 66} = 11$


Proof













\(\ds 121\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 11^2\)




















\(\ds 66\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 3 \times 11\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 121\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^0 \times 11^2\)




















\(\ds 66\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^1 \times 3^1 \times 11^1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \gcd \set {121, 66}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^0 \times 11^1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 11\)









$\blacksquare$
