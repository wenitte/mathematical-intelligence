Theorem
Let $G$ be a group whose identity is $e$ and whose order is $n$.

Then:

$\forall g \in G: g^n = e$


Proof
Let $G$ be a group such that $\order G = n$.
Let $g \in G$ and let $\order g = k$.
From Order of Element Divides Order of Finite Group:

$k \divides n$
So:

$\exists m \in \Z_{>0}: k m = n$
Thus:














\(\ds g^n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {g^k}^m\)





Powers of Group Elements: Product of Indices














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^m\)





Definition of Order of Group Element: $g^k = e$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e\)





Power of Identity is Identity



$\blacksquare$
