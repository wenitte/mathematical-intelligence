Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\csc a x} = \frac {-\cos a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\csc a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sin a x \rd x\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\sin a x$



$\blacksquare$
