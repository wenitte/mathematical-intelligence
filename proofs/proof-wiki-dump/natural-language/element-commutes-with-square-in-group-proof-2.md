Theorem
Let $\left({G, \circ}\right)$ be a group.
Let $x \in G$.

Then $x$ commutes with $x \circ x$.


Proof
By definition, a group is also a semigroup.
Thus the result Element Commutes with Square in Semigroup can be applied.
$\blacksquare$
