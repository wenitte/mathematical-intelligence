Examples of Numbers such that Divisor Count divides Phi divides Divisor Sum
The number $570$ has the property that:

$\map {\sigma_0} {570} \divides \map \phi {570} \divides \map {\sigma_1} {570}$
where:

$\divides$ denotes divisibility
$\sigma_0$ denotes the divisor count function
$\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ (phi) function
$\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {570}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 16 \, \)





\(\ds \)





$\sigma_0$ of $570$














\(\ds \map \phi {570}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 144 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 9 \times 16\)





$\phi$ of $570$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {570}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 1440 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 10 \times 144\)





$\sigma_1$ of $570$



$\blacksquare$
