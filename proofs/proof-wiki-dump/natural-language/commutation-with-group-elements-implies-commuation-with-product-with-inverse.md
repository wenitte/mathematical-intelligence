Theorem
Let $G$ be a group.
Let $a, b, c \in G$ such that $a$ commutes with both $b$ and $c$.

Then $a$ commutes with $b c^{-1}$.


Proof













\(\ds a b c^{-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b a c^{-1}\)





as $a$ commutes with $b$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds b c^{-1} a\)





Commutation with Inverse in Monoid



$\blacksquare$
