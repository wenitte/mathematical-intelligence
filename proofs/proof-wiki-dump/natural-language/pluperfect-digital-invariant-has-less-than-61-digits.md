Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{>0}$ be a pluperfect digital invariant.
Then $n$ has less than $61$ digits.


Proof
We have that:

$n \times 9^n < 10^\paren {n - 1}$
when $n > 60$.
So an $n$-digit integer, for $n > 60$, is always greater than the sum of the $n$th powers of its digits.
$\blacksquare$
