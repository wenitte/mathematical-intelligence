Theorem
Let $k \in \R$ be a real number.
The first order ODE:

$y \, \dfrac {\d y} {\d x} = k$
has the general solution:

$y^2 = 2 k x + C$


Proof













\(\ds y \, \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int y \rd y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int k \rd x\)





Solution to Separable Differential Equation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {y^2} 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds k x + \frac C 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 k x + C\)









$\blacksquare$
