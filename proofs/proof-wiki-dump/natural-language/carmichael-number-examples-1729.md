Example of Carmichael Number
$1729$ is a Carmichael number:

$\forall a \in \Z: a \perp 1729: a^{1729} \equiv a \pmod {1729}$
while $1729$ is composite.


Proof
We have that:

$1729 = 7 \times 13 \times 19$
and so:














\(\ds 7^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 1729\)




















\(\ds 13^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 1729\)




















\(\ds 19^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 1729\)










We also have that:














\(\ds 1728\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 288 \times 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 144 \times 12\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 96 \times 18\)









The result follows by Korselt's Theorem.
$\blacksquare$
