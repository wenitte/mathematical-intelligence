Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {36} = 91$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$36 = 2^2 \times 3^2$
Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {36}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2^3 - 1} {2 - 1} \times \frac {3^3 - 1} {3 - 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {8 - 1} 1 \times \frac {27 - 1} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7 \times 13\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 91\)









$\blacksquare$
