Example of Use of GCD from Prime Decomposition
The greatest common divisor of $p^2 q$ and $p q r$, where $p$, $q$ and $r$ are all primes, is:

$\gcd \set {p^2 q, p q r} = p q$


Proof













\(\ds p^2 q\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^2 q^1 r^0\)




















\(\ds p q r\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^1 q^1 r^1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \gcd \set {p^2 q, p q r}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^1 q^1 r^0\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds p q\)









$\blacksquare$
