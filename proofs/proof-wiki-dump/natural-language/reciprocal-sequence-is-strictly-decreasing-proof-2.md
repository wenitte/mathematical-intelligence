Theorem
The reciprocal sequence:

$\sequence {\operatorname {recip} }: \N_{>0} \to \R$: $n \mapsto \dfrac 1 n$
is strictly decreasing.


Proof
Let $n \in \N_{>0}$.














\(\ds \frac 1 n - \frac 1 {n + 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {n + 1} - n} {n \paren {n + 1} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {n^2 + n}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(>\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac 1 n\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {n + 1}\)









Hence the result, as $n$ was arbitrary.
$\blacksquare$
