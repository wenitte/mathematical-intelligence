Example of Carmichael Number
$561$ is a Carmichael number:

$\forall a \in \Z: a \perp 561: a^{561} \equiv a \pmod {561}$
while $561$ is composite.


Proof
We have that:

$561 = 3 \times 11 \times 17$
and so:














\(\ds 3^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 561\)




















\(\ds 11^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 561\)




















\(\ds 17^2\)

\(\nmid\)







\(\ds 561\)










We also have that:














\(\ds 560\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 280 \times 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 56 \times 10\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 35 \times 16\)









The result follows by Korselt's Theorem.
$\blacksquare$
