Theorem
$\laptrans {\dfrac 1 {\sqrt t} } = \sqrt {\dfrac \pi s}$
where $\laptrans f$ denotes the Laplace transform of the real function $f$.


Proof













\(\ds \laptrans {\dfrac 1 {\sqrt t} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \laptrans {t^{-1 / 2} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\map \Gamma {1 / 2} } {s^{1 / 2} }\)





Laplace Transform of Real Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt \pi} {\sqrt s}\)





Gamma Function of One Half














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\dfrac \pi s}\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
