Corollary to Definite Integral of Constant
$\ds \int_a^b \rd x = b - a$


Proof
From Definite Integral of Constant:

$\ds \int_a^b c \rd x = c \paren {b - a}$
where $c$ is a constant.

Setting $c = 1$:

$\ds \int_a^b \rd x = b - a$
$\blacksquare$
