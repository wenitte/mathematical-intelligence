Examples of Complex Natural Logarithm
$\map \ln {-1} = \paren {2 k + 1} \pi i$
for all $k \in \Z$.


Proof













\(\ds -1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{i \pi}\)





Euler's Identity








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \ln {-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \ln {e^{i \pi + 2 k \pi i} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln 1 + i \pi + 2 k \pi i\)





Definition of Complex Natural Logarithm














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 + \paren {2 k + 1} \pi i\)









$\blacksquare$
