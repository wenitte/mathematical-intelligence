Theorem
Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets. 
Let $fÂ : X \times Y \to \overline \R$ be a function.
Let $y \in Y$. 

Then: 

$\paren {f^y}^+ = \paren {f^+}^y$
where:

$f^y$ denotes the $y$-horizontal function of $f$
$f^+$ denotes the positive part of $f$.


Proof
Fix $y \in Y$.
Then, we have, for each $x \in X$:














\(\ds \map {\paren {f^+}^y} x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {f^+} {x, y}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \max \set {0, \map f {x, y} }\)





Definition of Positive Part














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \max \set {0, \map {f^y} x}\)





Definition of Horizontal Section of Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\paren {f^y}^+} x\)





Definition of Positive Part



$\blacksquare$
