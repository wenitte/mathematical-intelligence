Theorem
Let $\map \delta t$ denote the Dirac delta function.

The Laplace transform of $\map \delta {t - a}$ is given by:

$\laptrans {\map \delta {t - a} } = e^{-a s}$


Proof 1













\(\ds \laptrans {\map \delta {t - a} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_0^{\to +\infty} e^{-s t} \map \delta {t - a} \rd t\)





Definition of Laplace Transform














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{-s \times a}\)





Integral to Infinity of Shifted Dirac Delta Function by Continuous Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^{-a s}\)









$\blacksquare$
