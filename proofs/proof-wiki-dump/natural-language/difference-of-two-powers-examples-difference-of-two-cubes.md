Theorem
$x^3 - y^3 = \paren {x - y} \paren {x^2 + x y + y^2}$


Corollary
$x^3 - 1 = \paren {x - 1} \paren {x^2 + x + 1}$


Proof
From Difference of Two Powers:

$\ds a^n - b^n = \paren {a - b} \sum_{j \mathop = 0}^{n - 1} a^{n - j - 1} b^j$
The result follows directly by setting $n = 3$.
$\blacksquare$
