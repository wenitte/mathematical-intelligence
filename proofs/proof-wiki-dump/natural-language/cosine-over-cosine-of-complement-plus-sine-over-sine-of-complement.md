Theorem
$\dfrac {\cos x} {\map \cos {90 \degrees - x} } + \dfrac {\sin x} {\map \sin {90 \degrees - x} } = \csc x \sec x$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {\cos x} {\map \cos {90 \degrees - x} } + \dfrac {\sin x} {\map \sin {90 \degrees - x} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\cos x} {\sin x} + \dfrac {\sin x} {\cos x}\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine, Sine of Complement equals Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cot x + \tan x\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine, Tangent is Sine divided by Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \csc x \sec x\)





Sum of Tangent and Cotangent



$\blacksquare$
