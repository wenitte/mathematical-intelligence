Examples of Polynomial Divisors Modulo $m$
Let $\map f x$ be the polynomial:

$\map f x = 2 x^4 - 4 x - 3$
Then $\map f x$ has the following (polynomial) divisors modulo $7$:

$2 x^2 + 3 x + 3$
$x - 2$
$x + 4$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {2 x^2 + 3 x + 3} \paren {x - 2} \paren {x + 4}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x^4 + 7 x^3 - 7 x^2 - 18 x - 24\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x^4 + 7 x^3 - 7 x^2 - \paren {2 \times 7 + 4} x - \paren {3 \times 7 + 3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 2 x^4 + 0 x^3 - 0 x^2 - 4 x - 3\)

\(\ds \pmod 7\)


















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 2 x^4 - 4 x - 3\)

\(\ds \pmod 7\)







$\blacksquare$
