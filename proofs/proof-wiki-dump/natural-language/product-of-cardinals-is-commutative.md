Theorem
Let $\mathbf a$ and $\mathbf b$ be cardinals.

Then:

$\mathbf a \mathbf b = \mathbf b \mathbf a$
where $\mathbf a \mathbf b$ denotes the product of $\mathbf a$ and $\mathbf b$.


Proof
Let $\mathbf a = \card A$ and $\mathbf b = \card B$ for some sets $A$ and $B$.

Then:














\(\ds \mathbf a \mathbf b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \card {A \times B}\)





Definition of Product of Cardinals














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \card {B \times A}\)





Cardinality of Cartesian Product of Finite Sets/Corollary














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \mathbf b \mathbf a\)





Definition of Product of Cardinals



$\blacksquare$
