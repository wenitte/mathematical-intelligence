Theorem
$\ds \pi \map \csch {\pi z} = \frac 1 z + 2 \sum_{n \mathop = 1}^\infty \paren {-1}^n \frac z {z^2 + n^2}$
where:

$z \in \C$ is not an integer multiple of $i$
$\csch$ is the hyperbolic cosecant function.


Proof













\(\ds \pi \map \csch {\pi z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \pi \map \csc {i \pi z}\)





Hyperbolic Cosecant in terms of Cosecant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds i \paren {\dfrac 1 {i z} + 2 i \sum _{n \mathop = 1}^\infty \paren {-1}^n \dfrac z {\paren {i z}^2 - n^2} }\)





Mittag-Leffler Expansion for Cosecant Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 z - 2 \sum_{n \mathop = 1}^\infty \paren {-1}^n \dfrac z {-z^2 - n^2}\)





$i^2=-1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 z + 2 \sum_{n \mathop = 1}^\infty \paren {-1}^n \dfrac z {z^2 + n^2}\)









$\blacksquare$
