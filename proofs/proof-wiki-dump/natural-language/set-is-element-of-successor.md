Theorem
Let $x$ be a set.
Let $x^+$ be the successor of $x$.

Then $x \in x^+$.


Proof
By the definition of successor set:

$x^+ = x \cup \{x\}$.
By the definition of singleton, $x \in \{x\}$.
Thus by the definition of union, $x \in x^+$.
$\blacksquare$
