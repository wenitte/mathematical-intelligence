Theorem
$\ds \int \sech^{-1} \frac x a \rd x = -x \paren {-\sech^{-1} \dfrac x a} - a \arcsin \dfrac x a + C$
where $-\sech^{-1}$ denotes the negative branch of the real inverse hyperbolic secant multifunction.


Proof













\(\ds -\sech^{-1} \frac x a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\arsech \frac x a\)





Definition of Real Inverse Hyperbolic Secant








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int -\sech^{-1} \frac x a \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\int \arsech \frac x a \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {x \arsech \dfrac x a + a \arcsin \dfrac x a + C}\)





Primitive of $\arsech \dfrac x a$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -x \paren {-\sech^{-1} \dfrac x a} - a \arcsin \dfrac x a + C\)





Definition of Real Inverse Hyperbolic Secant



$\blacksquare$
