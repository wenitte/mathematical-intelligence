Theorem
Equality is transitive:

$\forall a, b, c: \paren {a = b} \land \paren {b = c} \implies a = c$


Proof













\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b\)














\(\ds \vdash \ \ \)





\(\ds \map P a\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds \map P b\)





Leibniz's law














\(\ds b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds c\)














\(\ds \vdash \ \ \)





\(\ds \map P b\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds \map P c\)





Leibniz's law








\(\ds \vdash \ \ \)





\(\ds \map P a\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds \map P c\)





Biconditional is Transitive








\(\ds \vdash \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds c\)





Leibniz's law



$\blacksquare$
