Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Then:

$\cmod {z - 1} < \cmod {z + 1} \iff \map \Re z > 0$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {z - 1}\)

\(<\)







\(\ds \cmod {z + 1}\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \cmod {z + 1}\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \cmod {z - 1}\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x + 1}^2 + y^2\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \paren {x - 1}^2 + y^2\)





Definition of Complex Modulus








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x + 1}^2\)

\(>\)







\(\ds \paren {x - 1}^2\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x^2 + 2 x + 1\)

\(>\)







\(\ds x^2 - 2 x + 1\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds 4 x\)

\(>\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(>\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \Re z\)

\(>\)







\(\ds 0\)





Definition of Real Part



$\blacksquare$
