Theorem
Let $\struct {A, \ast}$ be a $\ast$-algebra over $\C$.
Let $A_{\mathbf{SA} }$ be the set of Hermitian elements of $A$.

Then $A_{\mathbf{SA} }$ is a $\R$-vector subspace of $A$.


Proof
We use the One-Step Vector Subspace Test.
From Zero Vector in *-Algebra is Hermitian, we have ${\mathbf 0}_A \in A_{\mathbf{SA} }$.
In particular $A_{\mathbf{SA} } \ne \O$.
Let $a, b \in A_{\mathbf{SA} }$ and $\lambda \in \R$.
We have, from $(\text C^\ast 2)$ and $(\text C^\ast 4)$ in the definition of an involution:

$\paren {a + \lambda b}^\ast = a^\ast + \lambda b^\ast = a + \lambda b$
Hence $a + \lambda b$ is Hermitian and $a + \lambda b \in A_{\mathbf{SA} }$.
So by the One-Step Vector Subspace Test, we have that $A_{\mathbf{SA} }$ is a $\R$-vector subspace of $A$.
$\blacksquare$
