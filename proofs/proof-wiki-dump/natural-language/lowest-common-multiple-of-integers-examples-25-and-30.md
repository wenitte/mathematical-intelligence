Example of Lowest Common Multiple of Integers
The lowest common multiple of $25$ and $30$ is:

$\lcm \set {25, 30} = 150$


Proof
We find the greatest common divisor of $25$ and $30$ using the Euclidean Algorithm:




\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 30\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 25 + 5\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 25\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5 \times 5\)









Thus $\gcd \set {25, 30} = 5$.

Then:














\(\ds \lcm \set {25, 30}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {25 \times 30} {\gcd \set {25, 30} }\)





Product of GCD and LCM














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {5^2 \times 5 \times 6} 5\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 5^2 \times 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 150\)









$\blacksquare$
