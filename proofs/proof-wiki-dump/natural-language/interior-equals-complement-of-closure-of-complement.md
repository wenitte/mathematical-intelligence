Theorem
Let $T$ be a topological space.
Let $H \subseteq T$.
Let $H^-$ denote the closure of $H$ and $H^\circ$ denote the interior of $H$.
Let $H^\prime$ denote the complement of $H$ in $T$:

$H^\prime = T \setminus H$

Then:

$H^\circ = H^{\prime \, - \, \prime}$


Proof













\(\ds H^{\circ \, \prime}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H^{\prime \, -}\)





Complement of Interior equals Closure of Complement








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {H^{\circ \, \prime} }^\prime\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {H^{\prime \, -} }^\prime\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds H^{\circ \, \prime \, \prime}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H^{\prime \, - \, \prime}\)





Composition of Mappings is Associative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds H^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H^{\prime \, - \, \prime}\)





Relative Complement of Relative Complement



$\blacksquare$
