Example of Divisor Sum of Prime Number
$\map {\sigma_1} 2 = 3$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum.


Proof
From Divisor Sum of Prime Number:

$\map {\sigma_1} p = p + 1$
where $p$ is a prime number.

We have that $2$ is a prime number.
Hence:

$\map {\sigma_1} 2 = 2 + 1 = 3$
$\blacksquare$
