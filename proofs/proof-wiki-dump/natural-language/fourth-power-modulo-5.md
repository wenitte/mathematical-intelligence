Theorem
Let $n \in \Z$ be an integer.
Then:

$n^4 \equiv m \pmod 5$
where $m \in \set {0, 1}$.


Proof
By Congruence of Powers:

$a \equiv b \pmod 5 \iff a^4 \equiv b^4 \pmod 5$
so it is sufficient to demonstrate the result for $n \in \set {0, 1, 2, 3, 4}$.

Thus:














\(\ds 0^4\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 0 \, \)

\(\, \ds \equiv \, \)



\(\ds 0\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)


















\(\ds 1^4\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 1 \, \)

\(\, \ds \equiv \, \)



\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)


















\(\ds 2^4\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 16 \, \)

\(\, \ds \equiv \, \)



\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)


















\(\ds 3^4\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 81 \, \)

\(\, \ds \equiv \, \)



\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)


















\(\ds 4^4\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 256 \, \)

\(\, \ds \equiv \, \)



\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)







The result follows.
$\blacksquare$
