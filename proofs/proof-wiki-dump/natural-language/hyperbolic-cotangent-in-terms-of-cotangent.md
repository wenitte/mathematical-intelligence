Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Then:

$\coth z = -\cot \paren {i z}$
where:

$\cot$ denotes the cotangent function
$\coth$ denotes the hyperbolic cotangent
$i$ is the imaginary unit: $i^2 = -1$.


Proof














\(\ds i \coth z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {i \cosh z} {\sinh z}\)





Definition 2 of Hyperbolic Cotangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cosh z} {i \sinh z}\)





$i^2 = -1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos \paren {i z} } {i \sinh z}\)





Hyperbolic Cosine in terms of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos \paren {i z} } {\sin \paren {i z} }\)





Hyperbolic Sine in terms of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cot \paren {i z}\)





Definition of Complex Cotangent Function



$\blacksquare$
