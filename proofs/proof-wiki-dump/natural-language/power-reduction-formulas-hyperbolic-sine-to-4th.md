Theorem
$\sinh^4 x = \dfrac {3 - 4 \cosh 2 x + \cosh 4 x} 8$
where $\sinh$ and $\cosh$ denote hyperbolic sine and hyperbolic cosine respectively.


Proof













\(\ds \sinh^4 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \left({\sinh^2 x}\right)^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \left({\frac {\cosh 2 x - 1} 2}\right)^2\)





Square of Hyperbolic Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh^2 2 x - 2 \cosh 2 x + 1} 4\)





multiplying out














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac {\cosh 4 x + 1} 2 - 2 \cosh 2 x + 1} 4\)





Square of Hyperbolic Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cosh 4 x + 1 - 4 \cosh 2 x + 2} 8\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 - 4 \cosh 2 x + \cosh 4 x} 8\)





rearrangement



$\blacksquare$
