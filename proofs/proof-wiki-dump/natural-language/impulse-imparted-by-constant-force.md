Theorem
Let a constant force $\mathbf F$ be applied to a particle $P$ from time $t_1$ to time $t_2$.
Then the impulse $\mathbf J$ imparted to $P$ is:

$\mathbf J = \mathbf F \paren {t_2 - t_1}$


Proof













\(\ds \mathbf J\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \mathbf F \rd t\)





Definition of Impulse














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \bigintlimits {\mathbf F t} {t \mathop = t_1} {t \mathop = t_2}\)





Definite Integral of Constant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \mathbf F \paren {t_2 - t_1}\)









$\blacksquare$
