Theorem
Let $a \in \R_{>0}$ be a positive real number.
Let $x, y \in \R$ be real numbers.
Let $a^x$ be defined as $a$ to the power of $x$.

Then:

$\dfrac{a^x} {a^y} = a^{x - y}$


Proof













\(\ds \frac {a^x} {a^y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^x \paren {\frac 1 {a^y} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a^x} \paren {a^{-y} }\)





Exponent Combination Laws: Negative Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^{x - y}\)





Product of Powers



$\blacksquare$
