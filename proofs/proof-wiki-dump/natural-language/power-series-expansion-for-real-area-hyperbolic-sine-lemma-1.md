Lemma for Power Series Expansion for Real Area Hyperbolic Sine













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {x^2 + 1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \frac 1 2 x^2 + \frac {1 \times 3} {2 \times 4} x^4 - \frac {1 \times 3 \times 5} {2 \times 4 \times 6} x^6 + \cdots\)










\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{n \mathop = 0}^\infty \frac {\paren {-1}^n \paren {2 n}!} {2^{2 n} \paren {n!}^2} x^{2 n}\)









for $x \in \R: -1 < x < 1$.


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {x + 1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \frac 1 2 x + \frac {1 \times 3} {2 \times 4} x^2 - \frac {1 \times 3 \times 5} {2 \times 4 \times 6} x^3 + \cdots\)





Power Series Expansion for $\dfrac 1 {\sqrt {1 + x} }$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\sqrt {x^2 + 1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \frac 1 2 x^2 + \frac {1 \times 3} {2 \times 4} x^4 - \frac {1 \times 3 \times 5} {2 \times 4 \times 6} x^6 + \cdots\)





setting $x \gets x^2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{n \mathop = 0}^\infty \frac {\paren {-1}^n \paren {2 n}!} {2^{2 n} \paren {n!}^2} x^{2 n}\)









for $-1 < x < 1$.
$\blacksquare$
