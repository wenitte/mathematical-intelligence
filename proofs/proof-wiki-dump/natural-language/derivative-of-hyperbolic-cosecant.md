Theorem
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\csch x} = -\csch x \coth x$
where:

$\coth x$ denotes the hyperbolic cotangent and $\csch z$ denotes the hyperbolic cosecant.
$x \in \R_{\ne 0}$


Proof
It is noted that at $x = 0$, $\csch x$ is undefined.
Hence the restriction of the domain.














\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\csch x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\frac 1 {\sinh x} }\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosecant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\paren {\sinh z}^{-1} }\)





Exponent Laws














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {\sinh x}^{-2} \cosh x\)





Derivative of Hyperbolic Cosine, Power Rule for Derivatives, Chain Rule for Derivatives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} {\sinh x} \ \frac {\cosh x} {\sinh x}\)





Exponent Combination Laws














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\csch x \coth x\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosecant and Definition of Hyperbolic Cotangent



$\blacksquare$
