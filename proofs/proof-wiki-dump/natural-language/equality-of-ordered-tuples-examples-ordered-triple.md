Example of Equality of Ordered Tuples
Let:

$\tuple {a_1, a_2, a_3}$ and $\tuple {b_1, b_2, b_3}$
be ordered triples.

Then:

$\tuple {a_1, a_2, a_3} = \tuple {b_1, b_2, b_3}$
if and only if:

$\forall i \in \set {1, 2, 3}: a_i = b_i$


Proof 1
A special case of Equality of Ordered Tuples for $m = n = 3$.
$\blacksquare$


Proof 2













\(\ds A\)

\(=\)







\(\ds B\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {a_1, a_2, a_3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \tuple {b_1, b_2, b_3}\)





Definition of $A$ and $B$








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {a_1, \tuple {a_2, a_3} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \tuple {b_1, \tuple {b_2, b_3} }\)





Definition of Ordered Triple








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b_1\)





Equality of Ordered Pairs












\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds \tuple {a_2, a_3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \tuple {b_2, b_3}\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b_1\)





Equality of Ordered Pairs












\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds a_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b_2\)


















\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds a_3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b_3\)









$\blacksquare$
