Theorem
$\ds \int \sec a x \rd x = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C$
where $\map \tan {\dfrac \pi 4 + \dfrac {a x} 2} \ne 0$.


Proof













\(\ds \int \sec x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2} }\)





Primitive of $\sec x$: Tangent plus Angle Form








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \sec a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
