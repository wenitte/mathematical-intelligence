Theorem
$h g = e \implies h = g^{-1}$ and $g = h^{-1}$


Proof
From the Division Laws for Groups:

$h g = e \implies g = e h^{-1} = h^{-1}$
Also by the Division Laws for Groups:

$h g = e \implies h = g^{-1} e = g^{-1}$
$\blacksquare$
