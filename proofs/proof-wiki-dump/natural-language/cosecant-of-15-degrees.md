Theorem
$\csc 15 \degrees = \csc \dfrac \pi {12} = \sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.


Proof













\(\ds \csc 15 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\sin 15 \degrees}\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\frac {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} 4}\)





Sine of $15 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 4 {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2}\)





multiplying top and bottom by $4$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \paren {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} } {\paren {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} \paren {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \paren {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} } {6 - 2}\)





Difference of Two Squares














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
