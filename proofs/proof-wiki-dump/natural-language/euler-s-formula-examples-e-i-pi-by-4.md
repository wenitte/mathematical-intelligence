Example of Use of Euler's Formula
$e^{i \pi / 4} = \dfrac {1 + i} {\sqrt 2}$


Proof













\(\ds e^{i \pi / 4}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \frac \pi 4 + i \sin \frac \pi 4\)





Euler's Formula














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 2} 2 + i \dfrac {\sqrt 2} 2\)





Cosine of $\dfrac \pi 4$, Sine of $\dfrac \pi 4$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {1 + i} {\sqrt 2}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
