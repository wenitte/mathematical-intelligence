Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^3 a x} = \frac {-\cos a x} {2 a \sin^2 a x} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\tan \frac {a x} 2} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^3 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \csc^3 a x \rd x\)





Cosecant is $\dfrac 1 \sin$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac{-\csc a x \cot a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 2 \int \csc a x \rd x\)





Primitive of $\csc^n a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos a x} {2 a \sin^2 a x} + \frac 1 2 \int \csc a x \rd x\)





Cosecant is $\dfrac 1 \sin$ and Cotangent is $\dfrac \cos \sin$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cos a x} {2 a \sin^2 a x} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\tan \frac {a x} 2} + C\)





Primitive of $\csc a x$



$\blacksquare$
