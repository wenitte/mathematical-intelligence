Theorem
Let $\mathbb A$ be one of the standard number systems $\N,\Z,\Q,\R,\C$.
Let $a,b$ be integers.
Let $\closedint a b$ denote the integer interval between $a$ and $b$.
Let $f, gÂ : \closedint a b \to \mathbb A$ be mappings.
Let $\lambda, \mu \in \mathbb A$.
Let $\lambda \cdot f + \mu \cdot g$ be the sum of the product of $f$ with $\lambda$ and the product of $g$ with $\mu$.

Then we have the equality of indexed summations:

$\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \paren {\lambda \cdot \map f i + \mu \cdot \map g i} = \lambda \cdot \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \map f i + \mu \cdot \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \map g i$


Proof
We have:














\(\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \paren {\lambda \cdot \map f i + \mu \cdot \map g i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \paren {\lambda \cdot \map f i} + \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \paren {\mu \cdot \map g i}\)





Indexed Summation of Sum of Mappings














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \lambda \cdot \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \map f i + \mu \cdot \sum_{i \mathop = a}^b \map g i\)





Indexed Summation of Multiple of Mapping



$\blacksquare$
