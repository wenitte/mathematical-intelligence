Theorem
$x^6 - y^6 = \paren {x - y} \paren {x + y} \paren {x^2 + x y + y^2} \paren {x^2 - x y + y^2}$


Proof













\(\ds x^6 - y^6\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x^3}^2 - \paren {y^3}^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x^3 - y^3} \paren {x^3 + y^3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x - y} \paren {x^2 + x y + y^2} \paren {x^3 + y^3}\)





Difference of Two Cubes














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {x - y} \paren {x^2 + x y + y^2} \paren {x + y} \paren {x^2 - x y + y^2}\)





Sum of Two Cubes



$\blacksquare$
