Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a division ring with zero $0_R$ and unity $1_R$.
Let $\norm {\, \cdot \,}$ be a norm on $R$.
Let $x \in R$

Then:

$x \ne 0_R \implies \norm {x^{-1} } = \dfrac 1 {\norm x}$


Proof
Let $x \ne 0_R$.
By Norm Axiom $\text N 1$: Positive Definiteness:

$\norm x \ne 0$
So:














\(\ds \norm x \norm {x^{-1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {x \circ x^{-1} }\)





Norm Axiom $\text N 2$: Multiplicativity














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {1_R}\)





Definition of Product Inverse














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Norm of Unity of Division Ring








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \norm {x^{-1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {\norm x}\)









$\blacksquare$
