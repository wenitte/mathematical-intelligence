Theorem
Let $G$ be a group.
Let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$.
Let $x, y \in G$.

Let $y H$ denote the left coset of $H$ by $y$.

Then:

$x \in y H \iff x^{-1} y \in H$


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds y H\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists h \in H: \, \)



\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y h\)





Definition of Left Coset








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists h \in H: \, \)



\(\ds x^{-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds h^{-1} y^{-1}\)





Inverse of Group Product








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists h \in H: \, \)



\(\ds x^{-1} y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds h^{-1}\)





Product with $y$ on the right








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x^{-1} y\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds H\)





$H$ is a subgroup



$\blacksquare$
