Theorem
Let $\struct {F, +, \times}$ be a field whose zero is $0_F$.
Let $a, b \in F$.

Then:

$-\paren {a \times b} = \paren {-a} \times \paren {-b} = a \times b$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {-a} \times \paren {-b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {a \times \paren {-b} }\)





Product with Field Negative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\paren {-\paren {a \times b} }\)





Product with Field Negative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \times b\)





Negative of Field Negative



$\blacksquare$
