Theorem
Let $\struct {D, +, \times}$ be an integral domain.
Let $a \in R$.

Then the negative $-a$ of $a$ is unique.


Proof
From the definition of an integral domain, $\struct {D, +, \times}$ is a ring.
The result follows from Ring Negative is Unique.
$\blacksquare$
