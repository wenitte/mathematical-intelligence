Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 2 + i$ and $z_2 = 3 - 2 i$.
Then:

$\cmod {3 z_1 - 4 z_2} = \sqrt {157}$


Proof













\(\ds 3 z_1 - 4 z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \paren {2 + i} - 4 \paren {3 - 2 i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {6 + 3 i} - \paren {12 - 8 i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -6 + 11 i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cmod {3 z_1 - 4 z_2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\paren {-6}^2 + \paren {11}^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {36 + 121}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {157}\)









$\blacksquare$
