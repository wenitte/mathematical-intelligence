Theorem
Let $z_1$ and $z_2$ be complex numbers expressed as vectors such taht $z_1$ is not parallel to $z_2$.
Let $a, b \in \R$ be real numbers such that:

$a z_1 + b z_2 = 0$
Then $a = 0$ and $b = 0$.


Proof
Suppose it is not the case that $a = b = 0$.
Then:














\(\ds a z_1 + b z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a \paren {x_1 + i y_1} + b \paren {x_2 + i y_2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a x_1 + b x_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)




















\(\ds a y_1 + b y_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a x_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -b x_2\)




















\(\ds a y_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -b y_2\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac {y_1} {x_1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {y_2} {x_2}\)









and $z_1$ and $z_2$ are parallel.
$\blacksquare$
