Theorem
$\sec^{-1} x = \pm i \sech^{-1} x$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sec^{-1} x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \sec y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Definition of Inverse Secant








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \sec {\pm \, y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Secant Function is Even








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \sech {\pm \, i y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Secant in terms of Hyperbolic Secant








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \pm \, i y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sech^{-1} x\)





Definition of Inverse Hyperbolic Secant








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm \, i \sech^{-1} x\)





multiplying both sides by $\pm \, i$



$\blacksquare$
