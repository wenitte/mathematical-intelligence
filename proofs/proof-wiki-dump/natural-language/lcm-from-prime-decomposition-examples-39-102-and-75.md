Example of Use of LCM from Prime Decomposition
The lowest common multiple of $39$, $102$ and $75$ is:

$\lcm \set {39, 102, 75} = 33 \, 150$


Proof













\(\ds 39\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 13\)




















\(\ds 102\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 3 \times 17\)




















\(\ds 75\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 5^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 39\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^1 \times 5^0 \times 13^1 \times 17^0\)




















\(\ds 102\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^1 \times 3^1 \times 5^0 \times 13^0 \times 17^1\)




















\(\ds 75\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^1 \times 5^2 \times 13^0 \times 17^0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \lcm \set {39, 102, 75}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^1 \times 3^1 \times 5^2 \times 13^1 \times 17^1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 33 \, 150\)









$\blacksquare$
