Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\csch^2 a x \rd x} {\coth a x} = \frac {-1} a \ln \size {\coth a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \frac \d {\d x} \coth a x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a \csch^2 a x\)





Derivative of $\coth a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {-a \csch^2 a x \rd x} {\coth a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\coth a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function under its Derivative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\csch^2 a x \rd x} {\coth a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} a \ln \size {\coth a x} + C\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function



$\blacksquare$
