Theorem
$\cos 72 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {2 \pi} 5 = \dfrac {\sqrt 5 - 1} 4$


Proof













\(\ds \cos 72 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \cos 36 \degrees - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \paren {\dfrac \phi 2}^2 - 1\)





Cosine of $36 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi^2} 2 - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi + 1} 2 - 1\)





Square of Golden Mean equals One plus Golden Mean














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi - 1} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac {1 - \phi} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\phi^{-1} } 2\)





Reciprocal Form of One Minus Golden Mean














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 5 - 1} 4\)
