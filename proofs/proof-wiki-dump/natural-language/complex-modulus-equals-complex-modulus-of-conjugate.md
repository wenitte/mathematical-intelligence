Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Let $\overline z$ denote the complex conjugate of $z$.
Let $\cmod z$ denote the modulus of $z$.

Then:

$\cmod z = \cmod {\overline z}$


Proof
Let $z = a + b i$.
Then:














\(\ds \cmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {a + b i}\)





Definition of $z$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {a^2 + b^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus



and:














\(\ds \cmod {\overline z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {\overline {a + b i} }\)





Definition of $z$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {a - b i}\)





Definition of Complex Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {a + \paren {- b} i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {a^2 + \paren {- b}^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {a^2 + b^2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod z\)









$\blacksquare$
