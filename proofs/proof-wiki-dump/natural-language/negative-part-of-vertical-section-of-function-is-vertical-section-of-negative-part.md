Theorem
Let $X$ and $Y$ be sets. 
Let $fÂ : X \times Y \to \overline \R$ be a function.
Let $x \in X$. 

Then: 

$\paren {f_x}^- = \paren {f^-}_x$
where:

$f_x$ denotes the $x$-vertical function of $f$
$f^-$ denotes the negative part of $f$.


Proof
Fix $x \in X$.
Then, we have:














\(\ds \map {\paren {f^-}_x} y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {f^-} {x, y}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\min \set {0, \map f {x, y} }\)





Definition of Negative Part














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\min \set {0, \map {f_x} y}\)





Definition of Vertical Section of Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\paren {f_x}^-} y\)





Definition of Negative Part



$\blacksquare$
