Theorem
$\ds \int e^{a x} \rd x = \frac {e^{a x} } a + C$


Proof for Real Numbers
Let $x \in \R$ be a real variable.














\(\ds \int e^x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^x + C\)





Primitive of $e^x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int e^{a x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {e^{a x} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{a x} } a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
