Corollary to Primitive of Reciprocal
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} x = \ln x + C$
for $x > 0$.


Proof
From Primitive of Reciprocal:

$\ds \int \frac {\d x} x = \ln \size x + C$
for $x \ne 0$.
By definition of absolute value:

$\forall x \in \R_{>0}: \size x = x$
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
