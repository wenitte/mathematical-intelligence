Examples of Use of Euclidean Algorithm
The GCD of $143$ and $227$ is:

$\gcd \set {143, 227} = 1$


Proof



\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 227\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 143 + 84\)










\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 143\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 84 + 59\)










\(\text {(3)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 84\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 59 + 25\)










\(\text {(4)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 59\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 25 + 9\)










\(\text {(5)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 25\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 9 + 7\)










\(\text {(6)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 9\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 \times 7 + 2\)










\(\text {(7)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times 2 + 1\)










\(\text {(8)}: \quad\)









\(\ds 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \times 1\)









Thus:

$\gcd \set {143, 227} = 1$
$\blacksquare$
