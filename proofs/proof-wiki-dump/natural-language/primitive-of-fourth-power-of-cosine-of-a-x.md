Theorem
$\ds \int \cos^4 a x \rd x = \frac {3 x} 8 + \frac {\sin 2 a x} {4 a} + \frac {\sin 4 a x} {32 a} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \cos^4 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \paren {\frac {3 + 4 \cos 2 x + \cos 4 x} 8} \rd x\)





Power Reduction Formula for $\cos^4$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 3 8 \int \d x + \frac 1 2 \int \cos 2 a x \rd x + \frac 1 8 \int \cos 4 a x \rd x\)





Linear Combination of Primitives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 x} 8 + \frac 1 2 \int \cos 2 a x \rd x + \frac 1 8 \int \cos 4 a x \rd x + C\)





Primitive of Constant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 x} 8 + \frac 1 2 \paren {\frac {\sin 2 a x} {2 a} } + \frac 1 8 \paren {\frac {\sin 4 a x} {4 a} } + C\)





Primitive of $\cos a x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 x} 8 + \frac {\sin 2 a x} {4 a} + \frac {\sin 4 a x} {32 a} + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
