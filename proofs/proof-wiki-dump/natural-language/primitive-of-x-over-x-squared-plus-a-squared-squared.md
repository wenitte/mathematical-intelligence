Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {\paren {x^2 + a^2}^2} = -\frac 1 {2 \paren {x^2 + a^2} } + C$


Proof
Let:














\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^2 + a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x\)





Derivative of Power








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {\paren {x^2 + a^2}^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\d z} {2 z^2}\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 {2 z} + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 {2 \paren {x^2 + a^2} } + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
