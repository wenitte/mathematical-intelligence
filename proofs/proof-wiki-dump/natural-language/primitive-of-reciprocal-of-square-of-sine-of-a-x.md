Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 a x} = \frac {-\cot a x} a + C$


Corollary
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 x} = -\cot x + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \csc^2 a x \rd x\)





Definition of Cosecant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\cot a x} a + C\)





Primitive of $\csc^2 a x$



$\blacksquare$
