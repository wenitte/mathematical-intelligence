Theorem
Let $\struct {G, \circ, \preccurlyeq}$ be an ordered group with identity $e$.
Let $x \in G$.

Then the following equivalences hold:










\(\ds \forall x \in G: \, \)



\(\ds x \preccurlyeq e\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds e \preccurlyeq x^{-1}\)




















\(\ds e \preccurlyeq x\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x^{-1} \preccurlyeq e\)




















\(\ds x \prec e\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds e \prec x^{-1}\)




















\(\ds e \prec x\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x^{-1} \prec e\)











Proof
By Inversion Mapping Reverses Ordering in Ordered Group:










\(\ds \forall x \in G: \, \)



\(\ds x \preccurlyeq e\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds e^{-1} \preccurlyeq x^{-1}\)




















\(\ds e \preccurlyeq x\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x^{-1} \preccurlyeq e^{-1}\)




















\(\ds x \prec e\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds e^{-1} \prec x^{-1}\)




















\(\ds e \prec x\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x^{-1} \prec e^{-1}\)









Since $e^{-1} = e$, the theorem holds.
$\blacksquare$
