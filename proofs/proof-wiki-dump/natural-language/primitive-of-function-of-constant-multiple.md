Theorem
Let $f$ be a real function which is integrable.
Let $c$ be a constant.
Then:

$\ds \int \map f {c x} \rd x = \frac 1 c \int \map f u \d u$
where $u = c x$.


Proof
Let $u = c x$.
By Derivative of Identity Function: Corollary:

$\dfrac {\d u} {\d x} = c$

Thus:














\(\ds \int \map f {c x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\map f u} c \rd u\)





Primitive of Composite Function‎














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 c \int \map f u \rd u\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function



$\blacksquare$
