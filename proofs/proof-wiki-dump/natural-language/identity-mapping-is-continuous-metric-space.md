Theorem
Let $M = \struct {A, d}$ be a metric space.

The identity mapping $I_A: A \to A$ defined as:

$\forall x \in A: \map {I_A} x = x$
is a continuous mapping.


Proof
Let $a \in A$.
Let $\epsilon \in \R_{>0}$.
Let $\delta  = \epsilon$.
Then:














\(\ds \map d {x, a}\)

\(<\)







\(\ds \delta\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map d {\map {I_A} x, \map {I_A} a}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map d {x, a}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(<\)







\(\ds \delta\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \epsilon\)









$\blacksquare$
