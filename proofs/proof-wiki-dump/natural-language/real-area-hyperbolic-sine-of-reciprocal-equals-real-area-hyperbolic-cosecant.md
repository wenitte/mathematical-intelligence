Theorem
Everywhere that the function is defined:

$\map \arsinh {\dfrac 1 x} = \arcsch x$
where $\arsinh$ and $\arcsch$ denote real area hyperbolic sine and real area hyperbolic cosecant respectively.


Proof













\(\ds \map \arsinh {\dfrac 1 x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac 1 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sinh y\)





Definition of Real Area Hyperbolic Sine








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \csch y\)





Definition 2 of Hyperbolic Cosecant








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \arcsch x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)





Definition of Real Area Hyperbolic Cosecant



$\blacksquare$
