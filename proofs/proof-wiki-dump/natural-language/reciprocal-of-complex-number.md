Theorem
Let $z = a + i b$ be a complex number.
The reciprocal of $z$ is:

$\dfrac 1 z = \dfrac {a - i b} {a^2 + b^2}$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 {a + i b}\)





Definition of $z$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - i b} {\paren {a + i b} \paren {a - i b} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $a - i b$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {a - i b} {a^2 + b^2}\)





Product of Complex Number with Conjugate



$\blacksquare$
