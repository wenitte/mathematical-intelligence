Example of Derivative of Composite Function
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\map \ln {1 + x^3} } = \dfrac {3 x^2} {1 + x^3}$


Proof
Let $u = 1 + x^3$.
Let $y = \ln u$.
Thus we have:

$y = \map \ln {1 + x^3}$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d u} \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 1 u \cdot 3 x^2\)





Power Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Natural Logarithm














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3 x^2} {1 + x^3}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
