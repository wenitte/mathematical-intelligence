Theorem
Let $\struct {S, \circ, \preceq}$ be a naturally ordered semigroup.
Then $\forall m, n \in S$: 

$m \preceq n \iff \exists p \in S: m \circ p = n$


Proof
Necessary Condition
From Naturally Ordered Semigroup Axiom $\text {NO} 3$: Existence of Product, we have:

$\forall m, n \in S: m \preceq n \implies \exists p \in S: m \circ p = n$
$\Box$


Sufficient Condition
Suppose that $m \circ p = n$.














\(\ds 0\)

\(\preceq\)







\(\ds p\)





Definition of Zero of Naturally Ordered Semigroup








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds m \circ 0\)

\(\preceq\)







\(\ds m \circ p\)





$\preceq$ is compatible with $\circ$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds m\)

\(\preceq\)







\(\ds n\)





Zero is Identity in Naturally Ordered Semigroup



$\Box$

So $\forall m, n \in S$: 

$m \preceq n \iff \exists p \in S: m \circ p = n$
$\blacksquare$
