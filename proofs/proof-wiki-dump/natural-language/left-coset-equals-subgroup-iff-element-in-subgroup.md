Theorem
Let $G$ be a group whose identity is $e$.
Let $H$ be a subgroup of $G$.
Let $x \in G$.
Let $x H$ denote the left coset of $H$ by $x$.

Then:

$x H = H \iff x \in H$


Proof













\(\ds x H\)

\(=\)







\(\ds H\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x H\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e H\)





Left Coset by Identity: $e H = H$








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x e^{-1}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds H\)





Left Cosets are Equal iff Product with Inverse in Subgroup








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds H\)





Group Properties



$\blacksquare$
