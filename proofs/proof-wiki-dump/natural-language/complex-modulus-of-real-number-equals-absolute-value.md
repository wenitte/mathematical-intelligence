Theorem
Let $x \in \R$ be a real number.

Then the complex modulus of $x$ equals the absolute value of $x$.


Proof
Let $x = x + 0 i \in \R$.
Then:














\(\ds \cmod {x + 0 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {x^2 + 0^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {x^2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \size x\)





Definition 2 of Absolute Value



$\blacksquare$
