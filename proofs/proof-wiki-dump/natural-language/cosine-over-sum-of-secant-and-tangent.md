Theorem
$\dfrac {\cos x} {\sec x + \tan x} = 1 - \sin x$


Proof













\(\ds \frac {\cos x} {\sec x + \tan x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cos^2 x} {1 + \sin x}\)





Sum of Secant and Tangent














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {1 - \sin^2 x} {1 + \sin x}\)





Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {1 + \sin x} \paren {1 - \sin x} } {1 + \sin x}\)





Difference of Two Squares














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - \sin x\)









$\blacksquare$
