Theorem
Let $R$ be a ring.
Let $A, B$ be $R$-algebras.
Let $\phiÂ : A \to B$ be an $R$-algebra homomorphism.

Then $\phi \sqbrk A$ is a subalgebra of $B$. 


Proof
From Image of Submodule under Linear Transformation is Submodule, $\phi \sqbrk A$ is a submodule of $B$. 
Let $a, b \in A$. 
We then have: 

$\map \phi a \map \phi b = \map \phi {a b} \in \phi \sqbrk A$
since $\phi$ is a $R$-algebra homomorphism.
Hence $\phi \sqbrk A$ is a subalgebra of $B$.
$\blacksquare$
