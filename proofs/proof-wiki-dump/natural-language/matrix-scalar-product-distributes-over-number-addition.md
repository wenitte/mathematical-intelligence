Theorem
Let $\GF$ denote one of the standard number systems.
Let $\map \MM {m, n}$ be a $m \times n$ matrix space over $\GF$.
For $\mathbf A \in \map \MM {m, n}$ and $\lambda \in \GF$, let $\lambda \mathbf A$ be defined as the matrix scalar product of $\lambda$ and $\mathbf A$.

The matrix scalar product is distributive on $\map \MM {m, n}$, in the following sense:
For all $\mathbf A$ in $\map \MM {m, n}$ and $\lambda, \mu \in \GF$:

$\paren {\lambda + \mu} \mathbf A = \lambda \mathbf A + \mu \mathbf A$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {\lambda + \mu} \mathbf A\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\lambda + \mu} \sqbrk a_{m n}\)





Definition of $\mathbf A$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqbrk {\paren {\lambda + \mu} a}_{m n}\)





Definition of Matrix Scalar Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqbrk {\lambda a + \mu a}_{m n}\)





Distributive Property














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqbrk {\lambda a}_{m n} + \sqbrk {\mu a}_{m n}\)





Definition of Matrix Entrywise Addition














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \lambda \mathbf A + \mu \mathbf A\)





Definition of $\mathbf A$



$\blacksquare$
