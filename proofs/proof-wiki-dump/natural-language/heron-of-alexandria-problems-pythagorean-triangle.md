Problem
In a Pythagorean triangle, the sum of the area and the perimeter is $280$.
Find the sides and the area.


Solution
The Pythagorean triangle has sides $20$, $21$ and $29$.
Its area is $210$.


Proof
There is no simple way to solve this except by trial and error.
First we note that:

the area of the $20-21-29$ Pythagorean triangle is $\dfrac {20 \times 21} 2 = 210$
and we have that:

$210 + 20 + 21 + 29 = 280$

Then from Sequence of Solutions of Pythagorean Equation:
The sequence of solutions of the Pythagorean equation can be tabulated as follows:

$\begin{array} {r r | r r | r r r | c}
m & n & m^2 & n^2 & 2 m n & m^2 - n^2 & m^2 + n^2 \\
\hline
2 & 1 & 4 & 1 & 4 & 3 & 5 & \text{Primitive} \\
\hline
3 & 1 & 9 & 1 & 6 & 8 & 10 \\
3 & 2 & 9 & 4 & 12 & 5 & 13 & \text{Primitive} \\
\hline
4 & 1 & 16 & 1 & 8 & 15 & 17 & \text{Primitive} \\

4 & 3 & 16 & 9 & 24 & 7 & 25 & \text{Primitive} \\
\hline
5 & 1 & 25 & 1 & 10 & 24 & 26 \\

5 & 3 & 25 & 9 & 30 & 16 & 34 \\

6 & 1 & 36 & 1 & 12 & 35 & 37 & \text{Primitive} \\

6 & 3 & 36 & 9 & 36 & 27 & 45 \\

from which it can soon be seen that the $20-21-29$ triangle is the one required.
$\blacksquare$
