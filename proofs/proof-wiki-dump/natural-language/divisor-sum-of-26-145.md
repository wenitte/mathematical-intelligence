Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {26 \, 145} = 52 \, 416$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$26 \, 145 = 3^2 \times 5 \times 7 \times 83$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {26 \, 145}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3^3 - 1} {3 - 1} \times \paren {5 + 1} \times \paren {7 + 1} \times \paren {83 + 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {26} 2 \times 6 \times 8 \times 84\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 13 \times 6 \times 8 \times 84\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 13 \times \paren {2 \times 3} \times 2^3 \times \paren {2^2 \times 3 \times 7}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^6 \times 3^2 \times 7 \times 13\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 52 \, 416\)









$\blacksquare$
