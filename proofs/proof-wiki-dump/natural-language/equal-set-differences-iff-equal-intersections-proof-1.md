Theorem
$R \setminus S = R \setminus T \iff R \cap S = R \cap T$


Proof













\(\ds R \setminus S\)

\(=\)







\(\ds R \setminus T\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \set {x \in R: x \notin S}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {x \in R: x \notin T}\)





Definition of Set Difference








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x \in R: \, \)



\(\ds x \notin S\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x \notin T\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x \in R: \, \)



\(\ds x \in S\)

\(\iff\)







\(\ds x \in T\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \set {\paren {x \in R} \land \paren {x \in S} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \set {\paren {x \in R} \land \paren {x \in T} }\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds R \cap S\)

\(=\)







\(\ds R \cap T\)





Definition of Set Intersection



$\blacksquare$
