Theorem
Let $S$ be a set.
Let $\RR, \QQ$ be relations on $S$ such that

$\RR \subseteq \QQ$
Let $x \in S$.

Then

$x^\RR \subseteq x^\QQ$
where $x^\RR$ denotes the $\RR$-segment of $x$.


Proof
Let $y \in x^\RR$.
By definition of $\RR$-segment:

$\tuple {y, x} \in \RR$
By definition of subset:

$\tuple {y, x} \in \QQ$
Thus by definition of $\QQ$-segment:

$y \in x^\QQ$
$\blacksquare$
