Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 a x \rd x} {\tan a x} = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \frac {\d} {\d x} \tan x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sec^2 x\)





Derivative of Tangent Function








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 x \rd x} {\tan x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\tan a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function under its Derivative








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 a x \rd x} {\tan a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
