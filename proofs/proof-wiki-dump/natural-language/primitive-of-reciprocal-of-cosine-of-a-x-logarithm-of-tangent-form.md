Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\cos a x} = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sec x \rd x\)





Definition of Real Secant Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac x 2} } + C\)





Primitive of $\sec x$: Tangent plus Angle Form








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\cos a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {\map \tan {\frac \pi 4 + \frac {a x} 2} } + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
