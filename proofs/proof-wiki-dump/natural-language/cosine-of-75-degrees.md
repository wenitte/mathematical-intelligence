Theorem
$\cos 75^\circ = \cos \dfrac {5 \pi}{12} = \dfrac {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} 4$
where $\cos$ denotes the cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 75^\circ\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos \left({90^\circ - 15^\circ}\right)\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin 15^\circ\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 6 - \sqrt 2} 4\)





Sine of $15^\circ$



$\blacksquare$
