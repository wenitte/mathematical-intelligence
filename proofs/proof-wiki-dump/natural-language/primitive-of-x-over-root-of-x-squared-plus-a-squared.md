Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} } = \sqrt {x^2 + a^2} + C$


Proof
Let:














\(\ds z^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^2 + a^2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 z \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x\)





Chain Rule for Derivatives, Power Rule for Derivatives








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {x \rd x} {\sqrt {x^2 + a^2} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {z \rd z} {z}\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \rd z\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds z + C\)





Primitive of Constant














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {x^2 + a^2} + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
