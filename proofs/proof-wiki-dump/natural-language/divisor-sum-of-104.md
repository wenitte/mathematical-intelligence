Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {104} = 210$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$104 = 2^3 \times 13$
Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {104}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2^4 - 1} {2 - 1} \times \frac {13^2 - 1} {13 - 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {16 - 1} 1 \times \frac {169 - 1} {12}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 15 \times 14\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 210\)









$\blacksquare$
