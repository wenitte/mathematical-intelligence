Theorem
$\sec \left({x + \dfrac \pi 2}\right) = -\csc x$


Proof













\(\ds \sec \left({x + \frac \pi 2}\right)\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos \left({x + \frac \pi 2}\right)}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {- \sin x}\)





Cosine of Angle plus Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\csc x\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine



$\blacksquare$
