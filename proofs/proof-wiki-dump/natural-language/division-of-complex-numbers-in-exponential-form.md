Theorem
Let $z_1 := r_1 e^{i \theta_1}$ and $z_2 := r_2 e^{i \theta_2}$ be complex numbers expressed in exponential form.

Then:

$\dfrac {z_1} {z_2} = \dfrac {r_1} {r_2} e^{i \paren {\theta_1 - \theta_2} }$


Proof













\(\ds \frac {z_1} {z_2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {r_1 e^{i \theta_1} } {r_2 e^{i \theta_2} }\)





Definition of Exponential Form of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {r_1 \paren {\cos \theta_1 + i \sin \theta_1 } } {r_2 \paren {\cos \theta_2 + i \sin \theta_2} }\)





Euler's Formula














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {r_1} {r_2} \paren {\map \cos {\theta_1 - \theta_2} + i \map \sin {\theta_1 - \theta_2} }\)





Division of Complex Numbers in Polar Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {r_1} {r_2} e^{i \paren {\theta_1 - \theta_2} }\)





Euler's Formula



$\blacksquare$
