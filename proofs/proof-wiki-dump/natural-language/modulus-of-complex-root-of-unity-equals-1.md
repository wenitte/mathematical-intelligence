Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{>0}$ be a (strictly) positive integer such that $n$ is even.
Let $U_n = \set {z \in \C: z^n = 1}$ be the set of complex $n$th roots of unity.
Let $z \in U_n$.

Then:

$\cmod z = 1$
where $\cmod z$ denotes the modulus of $z$.


Proof













\(\ds z^n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cmod {z^n}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cmod z^n\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Power of Complex Modulus equals Complex Modulus of Power








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





Positive Real Complex Root of Unity



$\blacksquare$
