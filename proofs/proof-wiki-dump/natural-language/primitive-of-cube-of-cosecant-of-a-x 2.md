Theorem
$\ds \int \csc^3 a x \rd x = \frac {-\csc a x \cot a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\tan \dfrac {a x} 2} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \csc^3 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\csc a x \cot a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 2 \int \csc a x \rd x\)





Primitive of $\csc^n a x$ where $n = 3$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\csc a x \cot a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 2 \paren {\frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan \dfrac {a x} 2} }\)





Primitive of $\csc a x$: Tangent Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-\csc a x \cot a x} {2 a} + \frac 1 {2 a} \ln \size {\tan \dfrac {a x} 2} + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
