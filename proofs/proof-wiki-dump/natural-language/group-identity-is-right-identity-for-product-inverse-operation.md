Theorem
Let $\struct {G, \circ}$ be a group whose identity is $e$.
Let $\oplus: G \times G \to G$ be the product inverse of $\circ$ on $G$.
Then $e$ is also the right identity of $\struct {G, \oplus}$, in the sense that:

$\forall x \in G: x \oplus e = x$


Proof









\(\ds \forall x \in G: \, \)



\(\ds x \oplus e\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \circ e^{-1}\)





Definition of Product Inverse Operation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \circ e\)





Inverse of Identity Element is Itself














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Group Axiom $\text G 2$: Existence of Identity Element



$\blacksquare$
