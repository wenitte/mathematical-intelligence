Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {x \ln x} = \ln \size {\ln x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 x\)





Derivative of Natural Logarithm








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {x \ln x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\ln x} + C\)





Primitive of Function under its Derivative



$\blacksquare$
