Example of Roots of Complex Number: Corollary
The squares of the complex cube roots of $i$ are given by:

$\paren i^{2/3} = \set {\map \cis {60 + 120 k} \degrees}$
for $k = 0, 1, 2$.

That is:








\(\ds k = 0: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cis 60 \degrees = \dfrac 1 2 + \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i\)














\(\ds k = 1: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cis 180 \degrees = -1\)














\(\ds k = 2: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cis 300 \degrees = \dfrac 1 2 - \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i\)











Proof


Let $z^3 = i$.
We have that:

$z^3 = \map \cis {90 + 2 k \pi}$

Let $z = r \cis \theta$.
Then:














\(\ds z^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^3 \cis 3 \theta\)





De Moivre's Theorem














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cis {\dfrac \pi 2 + 2 k \pi}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds r^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)




















\(\ds 3 \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \pi 2 + 2 k \pi\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds r\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)




















\(\ds \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \pi 6 + \dfrac {2 k \pi} 3\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 30 \degrees + k \times 120 \degrees\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2$




Hence:














\(\ds z^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^2 \cis 2 \theta\)





De Moivre's Theorem








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds r\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)




















\(\ds \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {2 \pi} 6 + \dfrac {4 k \pi} 3\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 60 \degrees + k \times 240 \degrees\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2$



$\blacksquare$
