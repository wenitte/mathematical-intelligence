Example of Lowest Common Multiple of Integers
The lowest common multiple of $3$, $9$ and $11$ is:

$\lcm \set {3, 9, 11} = 99$


Proof













\(\ds \lcm \set {3, 9}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)





as $3 \divides 9$














\(\ds \lcm \set {9, 11}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 99\)





as $9 \perp 11$



Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
