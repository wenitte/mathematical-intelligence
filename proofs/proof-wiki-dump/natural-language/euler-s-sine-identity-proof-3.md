Theorem
$\sin z = \dfrac {e^{i z} - e^{-i z} } {2 i}$


Proof



\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds e^{i z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos z + i \sin z\)





Euler's Formula




\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds e^{-i z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos z - i \sin z\)





Euler's Formula: Corollary








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds e^{i z} - e^{-i z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {\cos z + i \sin z} - \paren {\cos z - i \sin z}\)





$(1) - (2)$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 i \sin z\)





simplifying








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {e^{i z} - e^{-i z} } {2 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin z\)









$\blacksquare$
