Theorem
The quadratic function:

$x^2 - 79 x + 1601$
gives prime values for integer $x$ such that $0 \le x \le 79$.
The primes generated are repeated once each.


Proof
Let $x = z + 40$.
Then:














\(\ds \)

\(\)







\(\ds \left({z + 40}\right)^2 - 79 \left({z + 40}\right) + 1601\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds z^2 + 2 \times 40 z + 40^2 - 79 z - 79 \times 40 + 1601\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds z^2 + 80 z + 1600 - 79 z - 3160 + 1601\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds z^2 + z + 41\)









Thus it can be seen that this is an application of Euler Lucky Number $41$.
$\blacksquare$
