Theorem
  
Let $\ln x$ be the natural logarithm of $x$ where $x \in \R_{>0}$.
Then $\ln$ satisfies the compound inequality:

$1 - \dfrac 1 x \le \ln x \le x - 1$


Proof
From Upper Bound of Natural Logarithm:

$\ln x \le x - 1$
From Lower Bound of Natural Logarithm:

$1 - \dfrac 1 x \le \ln x$
$\blacksquare$
