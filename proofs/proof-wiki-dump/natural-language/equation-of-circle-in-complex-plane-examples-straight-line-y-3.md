Example of Use of Equation of Circle in Complex Plane
The equation:

$\overline z = z + 6 i$
describes the straight line $y = -3$ embedded in the complex plane 


Proof













\(\ds \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z + 6 i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds z - \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -6 i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds i z - i \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -6 i^2\)





multiplying both sides by $i$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds i z - \overline i \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6\)









$\blacksquare$

This is an instance of the Equation of Circle in Complex Plane: Formulation 2:

$\alpha z \overline z + \beta z + \overline \beta \overline z + \gamma = 0$
where $\alpha = 0$.
This is a straight line if and only if $\alpha = 0$ and $\beta \ne 0$.

Hence:















\(\ds i z - \overline i \overline z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds i \paren {x + i y} - i \paren {x - i y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds i x + i^2 y - i x + i^2 y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -6\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -3\)









$\blacksquare$
