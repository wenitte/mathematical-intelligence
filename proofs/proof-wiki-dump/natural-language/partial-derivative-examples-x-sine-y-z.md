Example of Partial Derivative
Let $\map f {x, y, z} = x \map \sin {y z}$ be a real function of $3$ variables.
Then:

$\map {f_3} {a, 1, \pi} = -a$


Proof
By definition, the partial derivative with respect to the $3$rd variable $z$ is obtained by holding $1$st and $2$nd ones constant.














\(\ds \map {f_3} {x, y, z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map {\dfrac \partial {\partial z} } {x \map \sin {y z} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x y \map \cos {y z}\)





Derivative of $\sin a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map {f_3} {a, 1, \pi}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \times 1 \times \cos \pi\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \times \paren {-1}\)





Cosine of Multiple of Pi














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a\)









$\blacksquare$
