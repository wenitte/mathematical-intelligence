Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {115} = 144$


Proof
From Divisor Sum of Integer:

$\ds \map {\sigma_1} n = \prod_{1 \mathop \le i \mathop \le r} \frac {p_i^{k_i + 1} - 1} {p_i - 1}$
where $n = \ds \prod_{1 \mathop \le i \mathop \le r} p_i^{k_i}$ denotes the prime decomposition of $n$.

We have that:

$115 = 5 \times 23$
Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {115}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {5^2 - 1} {5 - 1} \times \frac {23^2 - 1} {23 - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {4 \times 6} 4 \times \frac {22 \times 24} {22}\)





Difference of Two Squares














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 \times 24\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2 \times 3} \times \paren {2^3 \times 3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^4 \times 3^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {2^2 \times 3}^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12^2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 144\)









$\blacksquare$
