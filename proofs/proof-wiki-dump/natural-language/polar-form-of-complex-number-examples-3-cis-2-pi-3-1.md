Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $\polar {3, -\dfrac {2 \pi} 3}$ can be expressed in Cartesian form as:

$3 \, \map \cis {-\dfrac {2 \pi} 3} = -\dfrac 3 2 - \dfrac {3 \sqrt 3} 2 i$
and depicted in the complex plane as:





Proof













\(\ds 3 \, \map \cis {-\dfrac {2 \pi} 3}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \paren {\map \cos {\dfrac {4 \pi} 3} + i \, \map \sin {\dfrac {4 \pi} 3} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 \times \paren {-\dfrac 1 2 + \dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 i}\)





Cosine of $240 \degrees$ and Sine of $240 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 3 2 - \dfrac {3 \sqrt 3} 2 i\)









$\blacksquare$
