Lemma
Let $n \in Z_{>0}$ be a positive composite number
Let $a b \equiv 0 \pmod n$.
Then it is not necessarily the case that either $a \equiv 0 \pmod n$ or $b \equiv 0 \pmod n$.


Proof
Let $n = 6$.
We have that:

$6 \equiv 0 \pmod 6$
but:

$2 \equiv 2 \pmod 6$
$3 \equiv 3 \pmod 6$
Hence the result by Proof by Counterexample.
$\blacksquare$
