Theorem
$\cos 135 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {3 \pi} 4 = -\dfrac {\sqrt 2} 2$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 135 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {90 \degrees + 45 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sin 45 \degrees\)





Cosine of Angle plus Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\sqrt 2} 2\)





Sine of $45 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
