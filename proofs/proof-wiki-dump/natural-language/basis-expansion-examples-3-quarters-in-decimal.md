Example of Basis Expansion
The fraction $\dfrac 3 4$ has a basis expansion in decimal (base $10$) notation as:

$0 \cdotp 75$


Proof













\(\ds 0 \cdotp 75\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7 \times 10^{-1} + 5 \times \times 10^{-2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {70 + 5} {10^2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {75} {100}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3 \times 25} {4 \times 25}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 3 4\)









$\blacksquare$
