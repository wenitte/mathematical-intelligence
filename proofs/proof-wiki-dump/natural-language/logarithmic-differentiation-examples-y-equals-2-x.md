Example of Logarithmic Differentiation
Let $y = 2^x$.
Then:

$\dfrac {\d y} {\d x} = 2^x \ln x$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \ln y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln 2^x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \ln 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac 1 y \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln 2\)





Chain Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Natural Logarithm, Derivative of Power








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y \ln 2\)





simplifying














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^x \ln 2\)





by hypothesis



$\blacksquare$
