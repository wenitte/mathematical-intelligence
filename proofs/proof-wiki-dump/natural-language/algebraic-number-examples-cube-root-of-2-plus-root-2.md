Example of Algebraic Number
$\sqrt [3] {2 + \sqrt 2}$ is an algebraic number.


Proof
Let $x = \sqrt [3] {2 + \sqrt 2}$.
We have that:














\(\ds x^3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + \sqrt 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^3 - 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^3 - 2}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^6 - 4 x^3 + 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^6 - 4 x^3 + 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









Thus $\sqrt [3] {2 + \sqrt 2}$ is a root of $x^6 - 4 x^3 + 2 = 0$.
Hence the result by definition of algebraic number.
$\blacksquare$
