Theorem
Let $z_1, z_2 \in \C$ be complex numbers such that $z_2 \ne 0$.
Let $\cmod z$ denote the modulus of $z$.

Then:

$\cmod {\dfrac {z_1} {z_2} } = \dfrac {\cmod {z_1} } {\cmod {z_2} }$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {\dfrac {z_1} {z_2} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {z_1 \times z_2^{-1} }\)





Definition of Complex Division














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {z_1} \times \cmod {z_2^{-1} }\)





Complex Modulus of Product of Complex Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {z_1} \times \cmod {z_2}^{-1}\)





Complex Modulus of Reciprocal of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\cmod {z_1} } {\cmod {z_2} }\)





Definition of Real Division



$\blacksquare$


Examples
Complex Modulus of $\dfrac {\paren {1 + 2 i}^{12} } {\paren {1 - 2 i}^9}$
$\cmod {\dfrac {\paren {1 + 2 i}^{12} } {\paren {1 - 2 i}^9} } = 5 \sqrt 5$
