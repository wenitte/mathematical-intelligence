Theorem
$100 \bmod 7 = 2$
where $\bmod$ denotes the modulo operation.


Proof
By definition of modulo operation:

$x \bmod y := x - y \floor {\dfrac x y}$
for $y \ne 0$.

We have:

$\dfrac {100} 7 = 14 + \dfrac 2 7$
and so:

$\floor {\dfrac {100} 7} = 14$

Thus:














\(\ds 100 \bmod 7\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 100 - 7 \times \floor {\dfrac {100} 7}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 100 - 7 \times 14\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2\)









$\blacksquare$
