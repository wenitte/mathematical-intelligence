Theorem
Let $\RR$ be a relation on a set $S$.

If $\RR$ is antireflexive, then so is $\RR^{-1}$.


Proof













\(\ds x\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds S\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {x, x}\)

\(\notin\)







\(\ds \RR\)





Definition of Antireflexive Relation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {x, x}\)

\(\notin\)







\(\ds \RR^{-1}\)





Definition of Inverse Relation



Hence the result by definition of antireflexive relation.
$\blacksquare$
