Theorem
$\ds \int \csch x \coth x \rd x = -\csch x + C$
where $C$ is an arbitrary constant.


Proof
From Derivative of Hyperbolic Cosecant:

$\dfrac \d {\d x} \csch x = -\csch x \coth x$
The result follows from the definition of primitive.
$\blacksquare$
