Theorem
Let $\RR$ be a relation on a set $S$.

If $\RR$ is asymmetric, then so is $\RR^{-1}$.


Proof
Let $\RR$ be asymmetric.
Let $\tuple {x, y} \in \RR^{-1}$.
Then:














\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR^{-1}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {y, x}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR\)





Inverse of Inverse Relation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\notin\)







\(\ds \RR\)





Definition of Asymmetric Relation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \tuple {y, x}\)

\(\notin\)







\(\ds \RR^{-1}\)





Definition of Inverse Relation



Thus it follows that $\RR^{-1}$ is also asymmetric.
$\blacksquare$
