Example of Lucas-Carmichael Number
$2915$ is a Lucas-Carmichael number:

$p \divides 2915 \implies \paren {p + 1} \divides 2916$


Proof
We have that:

$2915 = 5 \times 11 \times 53$
Then:














\(\ds 2916\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 \times 486\)





so $\paren {5 + 1} \divides \paren {2915 + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \times 243\)





so $\paren {13 + 1} \divides \paren {2915  + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 54 \times 54\)





so $\paren {31 + 1} \divides \paren {2915 + 1}$



$\blacksquare$
