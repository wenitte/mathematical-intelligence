Example of Complex Arithmetic
Let $z_1 = 1 - i$.
Then:

${z_1}^2 + 2 z_1 - 3 = -1 - 4 i$


Proof













\(\ds {z_1}^2 + 2 z_1 - 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {1 - i}^2 + 2 \paren {1 - i} - 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {1 - 2 i + i^2} + \paren {2 - 2 i} - 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2 i + 2 - 2 i - 3\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1 - 4 i\)









$\blacksquare$
