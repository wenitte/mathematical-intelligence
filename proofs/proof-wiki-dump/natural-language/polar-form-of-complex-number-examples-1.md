Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The real number $-1$ can be expressed as a complex number in polar form as $\polar {1, \pi}$.


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\paren {-1}^2 + 0^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)










Then:














\(\ds \map \cos {\map \arg {-1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-1} 1\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pi\)





Cosine of Multiple of Pi


















\(\ds \map \sin {\map \arg {-1} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 0 1\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 \text { or } \pi\)





Sine of Multiple of Pi




Hence:

$\map \arg {-1} = \pi$
and hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
