Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets.
Let $f: S \to T$ be a mapping such that its inverse $f^{-1}: T \to S$ is also a mapping.

Then:

$\forall x \in S: \map {f^{-1} } {\map f x} = x$


Proof









\(\ds \forall x \in S, y \in T: \, \)



\(\ds \map f x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds y\)


















\(\, \ds \iff \, \)

\(\ds \map {f^{-1} } y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Image of Element under Inverse Mapping








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \forall x \in S: \, \)



\(\ds \map {f^{-1} } {\map f x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





substituting $\map f x$ for $y$



$\blacksquare$
