Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin x \cos x} = \ln \size {\tan x} + C$


Corollary
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin a x \cos a x} = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\tan a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin x \cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sec x \rd x} {\sin x}\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 x \rd x} {\sin x \sec x}\)





multiplying top and bottom by $\sec x$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 x \rd x} {\frac {\sin x} {\cos x} }\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sec^2 x \rd x} {\tan x}\)





Tangent is Sine divided by Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\tan x} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac {\sec^2 a x} {\tan a x}$



$\blacksquare$
