Lemma
Let $K$ be a field. 
Let $X$ be a vector space over $K$.
Let $N$ be a linear subspace of $X$.
Let $z \in N$.

Then: 

$z + N = N$


Proof
Since $N$ is a linear subspace, we have: 

$z + N \subseteq N$
Conversely, let $x \in N$.
Then since $z \in N$ and $N$ is a linear subspace, we have $x - z \in N$. 
Then: 

$x = z + \paren {x - z} \in z + N$
So that: 

$N \subseteq z + N$
giving:

$N = z + N$
$\blacksquare$
