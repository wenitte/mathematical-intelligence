Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2$ can be expressed as a complex number in polar form as $\polar {\sqrt 3, \dfrac {5 \pi} 3}$.


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\paren {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2}^2 + \paren {\dfrac 3 2}^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\dfrac 3 4 + \dfrac 9 4}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {\dfrac {12} 4}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3\)










Then:














\(\ds \map \cos {\map \arg {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sqrt 3 / 2} {\sqrt 3}\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \pi 3 \text { or } \dfrac {5 \pi} 3\)





Cosine of $60 \degrees$, Cosine of $300 \degrees$


















\(\ds \map \sin {\map \arg {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-3 / 2} {\sqrt 3}\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {4 \pi} 3 \text { or } \dfrac {5 \pi} 3\)





Sine of $240 \degrees$, Sine of $300 \degrees$




Hence:

$\map \arg {\dfrac {\sqrt 3} 2 - \dfrac {3 i} 2} = \dfrac {5 \pi} 3$
and hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
