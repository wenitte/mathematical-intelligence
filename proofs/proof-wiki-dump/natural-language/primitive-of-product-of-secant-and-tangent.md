Theorem
$\ds \int \sec x \tan x \rd x = \sec x + C$
where $C$ is an arbitrary constant.


Proof
From Derivative of Secant Function:

$\dfrac \d {\d x} \sec x = \sec x \tan x$
The result follows from the definition of primitive.
$\blacksquare$
