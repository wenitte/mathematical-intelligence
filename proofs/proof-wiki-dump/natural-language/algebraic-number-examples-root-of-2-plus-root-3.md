Example of Algebraic Number
$\sqrt {2 + \sqrt 3}$ is an algebraic number.


Proof
Let $x = \sqrt {2 + \sqrt 3}$.
We have that:














\(\ds x^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 + \sqrt 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {x^2 - 2}^2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^4 - 4 x^2 + 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x^4 - 4 x^2 + 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)









Thus $\sqrt {2 + \sqrt 3}$ is a root of $x^4 - 4 x^2 + 1 = 0$.
Hence the result by definition of algebraic number.
$\blacksquare$
