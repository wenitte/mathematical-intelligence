Theorem
$\map \phi {25 \, 930} = \map \phi {25 \, 935} = \map \phi {25 \, 940} = \map \phi {25 \, 942} = 10 \, 368 = 2^7 \times 3^4$
where $\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ function.


Proof













\(\ds \map \phi {25 \, 930}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 \, 368\)





$\phi$ of $25 \, 930$














\(\ds \map \phi {25 \, 935}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 \, 368\)





$\phi$ of $25 \, 935$














\(\ds \map \phi {25 \, 940}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 \, 368\)





$\phi$ of $25 \, 940$














\(\ds \map \phi {25 \, 942}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 10 \, 368\)





$\phi$ of $25 \, 942$



$\blacksquare$

The significance of this result escapes the author of this page.
