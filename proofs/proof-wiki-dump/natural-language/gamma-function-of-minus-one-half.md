Theorem
$\map \Gamma {-\dfrac 1 2} = -2 \sqrt \pi$
where $\Gamma$ denotes the Gamma function.


Proof













\(\ds \map \Gamma {-\dfrac 1 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\map \Gamma {\frac 1 2} } {-1/2}\)





Gamma Difference Equation














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2 \, \map \Gamma {\frac 1 2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2 \sqrt \pi\)





Gamma Function of One Half



$\blacksquare$
