Example of Use of LCM from Prime Decomposition
The lowest common multiple of $p^2 q$ and $p q r$, where $p$, $q$ and $r$ are all primes, is:

$\lcm \set {p^2 q, p q r} = p^2 q r$


Proof













\(\ds p^2 q\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^2 q^1 r^0\)




















\(\ds p q r\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^1 q^1 r^1\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \lcm \set {p^2 q, p q r}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^2 q^1 r^1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds p^2 q r\)









$\blacksquare$
