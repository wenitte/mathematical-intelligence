Example of Derivative of Composite Function
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {e^{a x^2} } = 2 a x e^{a x^2}$


Proof
Let $u = a x^2$.
Let $y = e^u$.
Thus we have:

$y = e^{a x^2}$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d u} \dfrac {\d u} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds e^u \cdot 2 a x\)





Power Rule for Derivatives, Derivative of Exponential Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 a x e^{a x^2}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
