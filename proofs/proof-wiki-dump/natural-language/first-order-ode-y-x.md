Theorem
The first order ODE:

$\dfrac {\d y} {\d x} = x$
has the general solution:

$y = \dfrac {x^2} 2 + C$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \rd y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int x \rd x\)





Solution to Separable Differential Equation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {x^2} 2 + C\)





Primitive of Constant, Primitive of Power



$\blacksquare$
