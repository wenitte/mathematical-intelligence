Theorem
$0.999 \ldots = 1$


Proof
Let  $c = 0.999 \ldots$
Then:














\(\ds c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0.999 \ldots\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 10 c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {9.999 \ldots}\)





multiplying $c$ by $10$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 10 c - c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {9.999 \ldots} - \paren {0.999 \ldots}\)





subtracting $c$ from each side








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 9 c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 9\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









It follows that:

$0.999 \ldots = 1$
$\blacksquare$
