Theorem
$\map \csc {2 \pi - \theta} = -\csc \theta$
where $\csc$ denotes cosecant.

That is, the cosecant of an angle is the negative of its conjugate.


Proof













\(\ds \map \csc {2 \pi - \theta}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\map \sin {2 \pi - \theta} }\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {-\sin \theta}\)





Cosine of Conjugate Angle and Sine of Conjugate Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\csc \theta\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine



$\blacksquare$
