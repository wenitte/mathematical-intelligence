Theorem
The prime number $17$ is a long period prime:

$\dfrac 1 {17} = 0 \cdotp \dot 05882 \, 35294 \, 11764 \, \dot 7$


Proof
From Reciprocal of $17$:

$\dfrac 1 {17} = 0 \cdotp \dot 05882 \, 35294 \, 11764 \, \dot 7$
Counting the digits, it is seen that this has a period of recurrence of $16$.
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
