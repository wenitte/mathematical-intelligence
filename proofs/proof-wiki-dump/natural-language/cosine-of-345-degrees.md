Theorem
$\cos 345 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {23 \pi} {12} = \dfrac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} 4$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 345 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {360 \degrees - 15 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos 15 \degrees\)





Cosine of Conjugate Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sqrt 6 + \sqrt 2} 4\)





Cosine of $15 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
