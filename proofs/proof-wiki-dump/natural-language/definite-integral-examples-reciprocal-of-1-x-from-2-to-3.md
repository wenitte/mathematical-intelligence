Example of Definite Integral
$\ds \int_2^3 \dfrac {\d x} {1 - x} = \ln \dfrac 1 2$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - x\)





with a view to making a substitution








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \d z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\rd x\)









Then we re-evaluate the limits:














\(\ds 1 - 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -1\)




















\(\ds 1 - 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -2\)










Hence:














\(\ds \int_2^3 \dfrac {\d x} {1 - x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_{-1}^{-2} \dfrac {-\rd z} z\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int_{-2}^{-1} \dfrac {\d z} z\)





Reversal of Limits of Definite Integral














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \bigintlimits {\ln \size z} {-2} {-1}\)





Primitive of Reciprocal














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln 1 - \ln 2\)





Definition of Definite Integral














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0 - \ln 2\)





Natural Logarithm of 1 is 0














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \dfrac 1 2\)





Logarithm of Reciprocal



$\blacksquare$
