Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 a x \cos^2 a x} = \frac {-2 \cot 2 a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 a x \cos^2 a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\left({\sin a x \cos a x}\right)^2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\left({\frac {\sin 2 a x} 2}\right)^2}\)





Double Angle Formula for Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^2 2 a x}\)





Primitive of Constant Multiple of Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \int \csc^2 2 a x \rd x\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 4 \frac {-\cot 2 a x} {2 a} + C\)





Primitive of Square of Cosecant of a x














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-2 \cot 2 a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
