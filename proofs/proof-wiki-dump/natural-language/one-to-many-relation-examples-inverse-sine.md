Examples of One-to-Many Relations
Let $\RR \subseteq \R \times \R$ be the relation on $\R$ defined as:

$\forall \tuple {x, y} \in \R \times \R: \tuple {x, y} \in \RR \iff x = \sin y$
Then $\RR$ is a one-to-many relation.


Proof
Let:

$x_1, x_2 \in \R: \tuple {x_1, y} \in \RR \land \tuple {x_2, y} \in \RR$
Then:

$x_1 = \sin y = x_2$
and so:

$x_1 = x_2$
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
