Theorem
The empty set is unique.


Proof
Let $A$ and $B$ both be empty sets.
Thus:

$\forall x: \neg \paren {x \in A}$
and:

$\forall x: \neg \paren {x \in B}$
Hence:

$x \notin A \iff x \notin B$
and so:

$x \in A \iff x \in B$
vacuously.

From the Axiom of Extension:

$\forall x: \paren {x \in A \iff x \in B} \iff A = B$

Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
