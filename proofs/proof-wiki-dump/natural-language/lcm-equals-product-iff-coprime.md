Theorem
Let $a, b \in \Z_{>0}$ be strictly positive integers.

Then:

$\lcm \set {a, b} = a b$
if and only if:

$a$ and $b$ are coprime
where $\lcm$ denotes the lowest common multiple.


Proof
Necessary Condition
Let $a$ and $b$ be coprime.
Then:














\(\ds \lcm \set {a, b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {a b} {\gcd \set {a, b} }\)





Product of GCD and LCM














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {a b} 1\)





Definition of Coprime Integers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a b\)









$\Box$


Sufficient Condition
Let $\lcm \set {a, b} = a b$.
Then:














\(\ds \lcm \set {a, b} \gcd \set {a, b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a b\)





Product of GCD and LCM








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \gcd \set {a, b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {a b} {\lcm \set {a, b} }\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \gcd \set {a, b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {a b} {a b}\)





by hypothesis














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









$\blacksquare$
