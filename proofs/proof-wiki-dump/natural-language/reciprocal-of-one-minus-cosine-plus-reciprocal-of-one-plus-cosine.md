Theorem
$\dfrac 1 {1 - \cos x} + \dfrac 1 {1 + \cos x} = 2 \cosec^2 x$


Proof













\(\ds \dfrac 1 {1 - \cos x} + \dfrac 1 {1 + \cos x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\paren {1 + \cos x} + \paren {1 - \cos x} } {\paren {1 - \cos x} \paren {1 + \cos x} }\)





common denominator














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {1 - \cos^2 x}\)





Difference of Two Squares and simplification














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {\sin^2 x}\)





Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \cosec^2 x\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine



$\blacksquare$
