Theorem
$\cos 120 \degrees = \cos \dfrac {2 \pi} 3 = -\dfrac 1 2$
where $\cos$ denotes cosine.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 120 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {90 \degrees + 30 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\sin 30 \degrees\)





Cosine of Angle plus Right Angle














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac 1 2\)





Sine of $30 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
