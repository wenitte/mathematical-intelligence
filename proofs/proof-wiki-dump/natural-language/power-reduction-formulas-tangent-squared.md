Theorem
$\tan^2x = \dfrac {1 - \cos2x} {1 + \cos2x}$
where $\cos$ and $\tan$ denote cosine and tangent respectively.


Proof













\(\ds \tan^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin^2 x} {\cos^2 x}\)





Tangent is Sine divided by Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac {1 - \cos 2 x} 2} {\frac {\cos 2 x + 1} 2}\)





Square of Sine and Square of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {1 - \cos 2 x} {1 + \cos 2 x}\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2$



$\blacksquare$
