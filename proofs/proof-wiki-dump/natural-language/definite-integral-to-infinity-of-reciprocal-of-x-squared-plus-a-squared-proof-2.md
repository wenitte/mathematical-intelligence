Theorem
$\ds \int_0^\infty \dfrac {\d x} {x^2 + a^2} = \frac \pi {2 a}$
for $a \ne 0$. 


Proof













\(\ds \int_0^\infty \dfrac {\d x} {x^2 + a^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac \pi { 2 a^{2 - 1} } \csc \left({\frac \pi 2}\right)\)





Definite Integral to Infinity of $\dfrac 1 {1 + x^n}$: Corollary














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac \pi {2 a}\)





Sine of Right Angle




$\blacksquare$
