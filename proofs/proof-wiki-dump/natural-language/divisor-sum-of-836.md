Example of Divisor Sum of Integer
$\map {\sigma_1} {836} = 1680$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
We have that:

$836 = 2^2 \times 11 \times 19$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {836}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2^3 - 1} {2 - 1} \times \paren {11 + 1} \times \paren {19 + 1}\)





Divisor Sum of Integer














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 7 1 \times 12 \times 20\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 7 \times \paren {2^2 \times 3} \times \paren {2^2 \times 5}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^4 \times 3 \times 5 \times 7\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1680\)









$\blacksquare$
