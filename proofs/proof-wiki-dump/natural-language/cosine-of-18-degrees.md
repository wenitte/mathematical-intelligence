Theorem
$\cos 18 \degrees = \cos \dfrac \pi {10} = \dfrac {\sqrt {10 + 2 \sqrt 5} } 4$
where $\cos$ denotes the cosine function.


Proof













\(\ds \cos 18 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \cos {90 \degrees - 72 \degrees}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin 72 \degrees\)





Cosine of Complement equals Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sqrt {10 + 2 \sqrt 5} } 4\)





Sine of $72 \degrees$



$\blacksquare$
