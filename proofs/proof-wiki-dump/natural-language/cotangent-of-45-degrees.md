Theorem
$\cot 45 \degrees = \cot \dfrac \pi 4 = 1$
where $\cot$ denotes cotangent.


Proof













\(\ds \cot 45 \degrees\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\cos 45 \degrees} {\sin 45 \degrees}\)





Cotangent is Cosine divided by Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\frac {\sqrt 2} 2} {\frac {\sqrt 2} 2}\)





Cosine of $45 \degrees$ and Sine of $45 \degrees$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)





dividing top and bottom by $\dfrac {\sqrt 2} 2$



$\blacksquare$
