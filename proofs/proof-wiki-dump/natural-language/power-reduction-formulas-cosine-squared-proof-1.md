Theorem
$\cos^2 x = \dfrac {1 + \cos 2 x} 2$


Proof













\(\ds 2 \cos^2 x - 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos 2 x\)





Double Angle Formula for Cosine: Corollary $1$














\(\ds \cos^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {1 + \cos 2 x} 2\)





solving for $\cos^2 x$



$\blacksquare$
