Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{> 0}$ be a strictly positive integer.
Then:

$16^n \equiv 16 \pmod {20}$


Proof













\(\ds 16\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 16\)

\(\ds \pmod {20}\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 16\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 0\)

\(\ds \pmod 4\)
















\(\, \ds \text {and} \, \)

\(\ds 16\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 16^n\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 0\)

\(\ds \pmod 4\)
















\(\, \ds \text {and} \, \)

\(\ds 16^n\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 5\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 16^n\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 16\)

\(\ds \pmod {20}\)



Chinese Remainder Theorem



$\blacksquare$
