Theorem
Let $z = a \mathbf 1 + b \mathbf i + c \mathbf j + d \mathbf k$ be a quaternion.
Let $\overline z$ be the conjugate of $z$.
Let $\cmod z$ be the quaternion modulus of $z$.

Then:

$\cmod {\overline z} = \cmod z$


Proof













\(\ds \cmod z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2\)





Definition of Quaternion Modulus














\(\ds \cmod {\overline z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cmod {a \mathbf 1 - b \mathbf i - c \mathbf j - d \mathbf k}\)





Definition of Quaternion Conjugate














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2 + \paren {-b}^2 + \paren {-c}^2 + \paren {-d}^2\)





Definition of Quaternion Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2\)









$\blacksquare$
