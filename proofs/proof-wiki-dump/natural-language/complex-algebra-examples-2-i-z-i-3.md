Example of Complex Algebra
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number such that:

$\paren {2 + i} z + i = 3$
Then:

$z = 1 - i$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {2 + i} z + i\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {2 + i} z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 - i\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3 - i} {2 + i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\paren {3 - i} \paren {2 - i} } {\paren {2 + i} \paren {2 - i} }\)





multiplying top and bottom by $2 - i$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {6 + i^2 - 3 i - 2 i} {2^2 + 1^2}\)





simplification














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {5 - 5 i} 5\)





simplification














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 - i\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
