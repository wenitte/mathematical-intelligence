Theorem
Let $\mathbb U$ be a non-empty Grothendieck universe.

Then $\O \in \mathbb U$.


Proof
Let $A \in \mathbb U$ be an arbitrary element.














\(\ds \O\)

\(\subseteq\)







\(\ds A\)





Empty Set is Subset of All Sets








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \O\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \mathbb U\)





Grothendieck Universe is Closed under Subset



$\blacksquare$
