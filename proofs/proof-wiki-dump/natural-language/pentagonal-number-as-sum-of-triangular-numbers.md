Theorem
Let $P_n$ be the $n$th pentagonal number.
Then:

$P_n = T_n + 2 T_{n - 1}$
where $T_n$ is the $n$th triangular number.


Proof













\(\ds T_n + 2 T_{n - 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {n \paren {n + 1} } 2 + 2 \frac {\paren {n - 1} n} 2\)





Closed Form for Triangular Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {n^2 + n + 2 \paren {n^2 - n} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 n^2 - n} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 n \paren {n - 1} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds P_n\)





Closed Form for Pentagonal Numbers



$\blacksquare$
