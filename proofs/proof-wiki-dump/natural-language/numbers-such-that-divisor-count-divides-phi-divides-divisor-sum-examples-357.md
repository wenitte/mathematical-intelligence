Examples of Numbers such that Divisor Count divides Phi divides Divisor Sum
The number $357$ has the property that:

$\map {\sigma_0} {357} \divides \map \phi {357} \divides \map {\sigma_1} {357}$
where:

$\divides$ denotes divisibility
$\sigma_0$ denotes the divisor count function
$\phi$ denotes the Euler $\phi$ (phi) function
$\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof













\(\ds \map {\sigma_0} {357}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 8 \, \)





\(\ds \)





$\sigma_0$ of $357$














\(\ds \map \phi {357}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 192 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 24 \times 8\)





$\phi$ of $357$














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {357}\)

\(=\)

\(\, \ds 576 \, \)

\(\, \ds = \, \)



\(\ds 3 \times 192\)





$\sigma_1$ of $357$



$\blacksquare$
