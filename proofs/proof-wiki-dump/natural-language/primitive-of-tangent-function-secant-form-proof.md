Theorem
$\ds \int \tan x \rd x = \ln \size {\sec x} + C$
where $\sec x$ is defined.


Proof













\(\ds \int \tan x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\ln \size {\cos x} + C\)





Primitive of $\tan x$: Cosine Form














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\frac 1 {\cos x} } + C\)





Logarithm of Reciprocal














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\sec x} + C\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
