Theorem
Let $b \in \R_{>0}$ be a strictly positive real number such that $b \ne 1$.
Let $\log_b$ denote the logarithm to base $b$.

Then:

$\log_b 1 = 0$


Proof
By definition of logarithm:














\(\ds \log_b 1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\log_e 1} {\log_e b}\)





Change of Base of Logarithm














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 0 {\log_e b}\)





Natural Logarithm of 1 is 0














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)





whatever $\log_e b$ happens to be



$\blacksquare$
