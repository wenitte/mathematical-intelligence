Example of Giuga Number
$30$ is a Giuga number (the smallest):

$\dfrac 1 2 + \dfrac 1 3 +  \dfrac 1 5 - \dfrac 1 {30} = 1$


Proof
We have that:

$30 =  2 \times 3 \times 5$
Then:














\(\ds \dfrac 1 2 + \dfrac 1 3 +  \dfrac 1 5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3 \times 5 + 2 \times 5 + 2 \times 3} {2 \times 3 \times 5}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {15 + 10 + 6} {30}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {31} {30}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1 + \frac 1 {30}\)









Hence the result by definition of Giuga number.
$\blacksquare$
