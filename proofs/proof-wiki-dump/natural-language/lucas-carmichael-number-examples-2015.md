Example of Lucas-Carmichael Number
$2015$ is a Lucas-Carmichael number:

$p \divides 2015 \implies \paren {p + 1} \divides 2016$


Proof
We have that:

$2015 = 5 \times 13 \times 31$
Then:














\(\ds 2016\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 \times 336\)





so $\paren {5 + 1} \divides \paren {2015 + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 14 \times 144\)





so $\paren {13 + 1} \divides \paren {2015 + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 32 \times 63\)





so $\paren {31 + 1} \divides \paren {2015 + 1}$



$\blacksquare$
