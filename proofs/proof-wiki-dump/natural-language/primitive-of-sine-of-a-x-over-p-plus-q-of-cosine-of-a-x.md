Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\sin a x \rd x} {p + q \cos a x} = \frac {-1} {a q} \ln \size {p + q \cos a x} + C$


Proof













\(\ds \map {\frac \d {\d x} } {p + q \cos a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -a q \sin a x\)





Derivative of $\cos a x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\sin a x \rd x} {p + q \cos a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} {a q} \ln \size {p + q \cos a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function under its Derivative



$\blacksquare$
