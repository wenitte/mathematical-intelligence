Example of Consecutive Integers with Same Divisor Sum
Let $\sigma_1: \Z_{>0} \to \Z_{>0}$ denote the divisor sum function.
Then:

$\map {\sigma_1} {14} = \map {\sigma_1} {15} = 24$


Proof
From $\sigma_1$ of $14$:

$\map {\sigma_1} {14} = 24$
From $\sigma_1$ of $15$:

$\map {\sigma_1} {15} = 24$
Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
