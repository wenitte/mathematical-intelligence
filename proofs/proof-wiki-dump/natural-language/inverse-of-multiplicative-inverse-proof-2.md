Theorem
Let $\struct {F, +, \times}$ be a field whose zero is $0_F$.
Let $a \in F$ such that $a \ne 0_F$.
Let $a^{-1}$ be the multiplicative inverse of $a$.

Then $\paren {a^{-1} }^{-1} = a$.


Proof













\(\ds \paren {a^{-1} } \times a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a \times \paren {a^{-1} }\)





Field Axiom $\text M2$: Commutativity of Product














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1_F\)





Field Axiom $\text M4$: Inverses for Product








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a^{-1} }^{-1}\)





Definition of Multiplicative Inverse in Field



$\blacksquare$
