Theorem
Let $a, b$ be integers.
Let:

$a \divides b$
where $\divides$ denotes divisibility.

Then:

$\forall c \in \Z: a \divides b c$


Proof













\(\ds a\)

\(\divides\)







\(\ds b\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists x \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x a\)





Definition of Divisor of Integer








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds b c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x c a\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists z \in \Z: \, \)



\(\ds b c\)

\(=\)







\(\ds z a\)





where $z = x c$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(\divides\)







\(\ds b c\)





Definition of Divisor of Integer



$\blacksquare$
