Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets.
Let $\RR_1 \subseteq S \times T$ be a relation in $S \times T$.
Let $\RR_2 \subseteq \RR_1$.
Let $A \subseteq S$.

Then:

$\RR_2 \sqbrk A \subseteq \RR_1 \sqbrk A$
where $\RR_1 \sqbrk A$ denotes the image of $A$ under $\RR_1$.


Corollary
Let $x \in S$.

Then:

$\map {\RR_2} x \subseteq \map {\RR_1} x$
where $\map {\RR_1} x$ denotes the image of $x$ under $\RR_1$.

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2 \sqbrk A\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists x \in A: \, \)



\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_2\)

\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)

\(\ds \exists x \in A: \, \)



\(\ds \tuple {x, y}\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1\)





Definition of Subset








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \RR_1 \sqbrk A\)





Definition of Image of Subset under Relation



$\blacksquare$
