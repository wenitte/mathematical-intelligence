Example of Divisor Sum of Power of Prime
$\map {\sigma_1} {81} = 121$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
From Divisor Sum of Power of Prime:

$\map {\sigma_1} {p^k} = \dfrac {p^{k + 1} - 1} {p_i - 1}$

We have that:

$81 = 3^4$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {81}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {3^5 - 1} {3 - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {242} 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 121\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 11^2\)









$\blacksquare$
