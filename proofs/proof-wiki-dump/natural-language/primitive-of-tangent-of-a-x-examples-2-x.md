Example of Use of Primitive of $\tan a x$
$\ds \int \tan 3 x \rd x = \frac 1 2 \ln \size {\sec 2 x} + C$


Proof
From Primitive of $\tan a x$: Secant Form:

$\ds \int \tan a x \rd x = \frac {\ln \size {\sec a x} } a + C$

The result follows by setting $a = 2$.
$\blacksquare$
