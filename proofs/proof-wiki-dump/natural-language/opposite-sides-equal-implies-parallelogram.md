Theorem
Let $ABCD$ be a convex quadrilateral with $AB = CD$ and $BC = AD$.
Then $ABCD$ is a parallelogram.


Proof
Join $AC$.




\(\text {(1)}: \quad\)









\(\ds AB\)

\(=\)







\(\ds CD\)





by hypothesis




\(\text {(2)}: \quad\)









\(\ds BC\)

\(=\)







\(\ds DA\)





by hypothesis




\(\text {(3)}: \quad\)









\(\ds AC\)

\(=\)







\(\ds CA\)





Equality is Reflexive




\(\text {(4)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \Delta ABC\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \Delta CDA\)





SSS from $(1)$, $(2)$, and $(3)$




\(\text {(5)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \angle BCA\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \angle DAC\)





from $(4)$




\(\text {(6)}: \quad\)









\(\ds \angle BAC\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \angle DCA\)





from $(4)$




\(\text {(7)}: \quad\)









\(\ds BC\)

\(\parallel\)







\(\ds DA\)





Equal Alternate Angles implies Parallel Lines from $(5)$




\(\text {(8)}: \quad\)









\(\ds BA\)

\(\parallel\)







\(\ds DC\)





Equal Alternate Angles implies Parallel Lines from $(6)$



From $(7)$ and $(8)$, it follows by definition that $ABCD$ is a parallelogram.
$\blacksquare$
