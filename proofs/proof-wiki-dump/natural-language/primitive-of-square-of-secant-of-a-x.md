Theorem
$\ds \int \sec^2 a x \rd x = \frac {\tan a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \sec^2 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \tan x + C\)





Primitive of $\sec^2 x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \sec^2 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {\tan a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\tan a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
