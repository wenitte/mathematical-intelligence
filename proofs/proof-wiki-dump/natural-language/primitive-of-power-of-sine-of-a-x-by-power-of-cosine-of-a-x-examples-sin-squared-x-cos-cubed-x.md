Example of Use of Primitive of $\sin^m x \cos^n x$
$\ds \int \sin^2 x \cos^3 x \rd x = \dfrac {\sin^3 x} 3 - \dfrac {\sin^5 x} 5 + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \sin^2 x \cos^3 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sin^2 x \cos^2 x \cos x \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sin^2 x \paren {1 - \sin^2 x} \sin^2 x \cos x \rd x\)





Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \paren {\sin^2 x - \sin^4 x} \cos x \rd x\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \sin^2 x \cos x \rd x - \int \sin^4 x \cos x \rd x\)





Linear Combination of Primitives














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\sin^3 x} 3 - \dfrac {\sin^5 x} 5 + C\)





Primitive of Power of $\sin a x$ by $\cos a x$



$\blacksquare$
