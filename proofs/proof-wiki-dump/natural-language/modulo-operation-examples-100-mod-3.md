Theorem
$100 \bmod 3 = 1$
where $\bmod$ denotes the modulo operation.


Proof
By definition of modulo operation:

$x \bmod y := x - y \floor {\dfrac x y}$
for $y \ne 0$.

We have:

$\dfrac {100} 3 = 33 + \dfrac 1 3$
and so:

$\floor {\dfrac {100} 3} = 33$

Thus:














\(\ds 100 \bmod 3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 100 - 3 \times \floor {\dfrac {100} 3}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 100 - 3 \times 33\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









$\blacksquare$
