Theorem
Let $y = a e^{b t}$ be an exponential growth function.
Then the rate of growth of $y$ is proportional to the value of $y$ such that:

$\dfrac {\d y} {\d t} = b y$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a e^{b t}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \dfrac {\d y} {\d t}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds a b e^{b t}\)





Derivative of Exponential Function: Corollary $1$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds b y\)





Definition of $y$



$\blacksquare$
