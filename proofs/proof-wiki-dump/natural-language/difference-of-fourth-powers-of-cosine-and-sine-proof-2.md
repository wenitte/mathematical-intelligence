Theorem
$\sin^4 x - \cos^4 x = \sin^2 x - \cos^2 x$


Proof













\(\ds \sin^2 x - \cos^2 x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \left({\sin^2 x - \cos^2 x}\right) \left({\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x}\right)\)





Sum of Squares of Sine and Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin^4 x - \cos^4 x\)





Difference of Two Squares



$\blacksquare$
