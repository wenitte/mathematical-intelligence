Theorem
$\ds \int \sin x \rd x = -\cos x + C$


Proof
From Derivative of Cosine Function:

$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {-\cos x} = \sin x$
The result follows from the definition of primitive.
$\blacksquare$
