Lemma for Primitive of Reciprocal of p by Sine of a x plus q by Cosine of a x
$\ds \frac 1 2 \map \arctan {\dfrac {-p} q} + \frac \pi 4 = \frac {\arctan \dfrac q p} 2$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 2 \arctan \dfrac {-p} q + \frac \pi 4\)





making a definition








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 y - \frac \pi 2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \arctan \dfrac {-p} q\)





rearranging








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \tan {2 y - \frac \pi 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-p} q\)





Definition of Real Arctangent








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \tan {2 y + \frac {3 \pi} 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-p} q\)





Tangent Function is Periodic on Reals








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds - \map \cot {2 y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-p} q\)





Tangent of Angle plus Three Right Angles








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \tan {2 y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac q p\)





Cotangent is Reciprocal of Tangent








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\arctan \dfrac q p} 2\)





Definition of Real Arctangent



$\blacksquare$
