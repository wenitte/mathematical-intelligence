Theorem
Let $\RR \subseteq S \times T$ be a relation.

Then the preimage of $\RR$ is a subset of its domain:

$\Preimg \RR \subseteq S$


Proof
The preimage of $\RR$ is defined as:

$\Preimg \RR = \set {s \in \Dom \RR: \exists t \in \Rng \RR: \tuple {s, t} \in \RR}$

Hence:














\(\ds s\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \Preimg \RR\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds s\)

\(\in\)







\(\ds \Dom \RR\)





Definition of Preimage of Relation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \Preimg \RR\)

\(\subseteq\)







\(\ds \Dom \RR\)





Subset of Set with Propositional Function



$\blacksquare$
