Theorem
The positive integer $256 \, 103 \, 393$ can be expressed as the sum of $3$ fourth powers in $2$ different ways:














\(\ds 256 \, 103 \, 393\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 22^4 + 93^4 + 116^4\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 29^4 + 66^4 + 124^4\)










Also note that:














\(\ds 237 \, 336\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 22 \times 93 \times 116\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 29 \times 66 \times 124\)











Proof
We have that:














\(\ds 256 \, 103 \, 393\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 234 \, 256 + 74 \, 805 \, 201 + 181 \, 063 \, 936\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 22^4 + 93^4 + 116^4\)
























\(\ds 256 \, 103 \, 393\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 707 \, 281 + 18 \, 974 \, 736 + 236 \, 421 \, 376\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 29^4 + 66^4 + 124^4\)










Then we have:














\(\ds 22 \times 93 \times 116\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \left({2 \times 11}\right) \times \left({3 \times 31}\right) \times \left({2^2 \times 29}\right)\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^3 \times 3 \times 11 \times 29 \times 31\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 237 \, 336\)
























\(\ds 29 \times 66 \times 124\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 29 \times \left({2 \times 3 \times 11}\right) \times \left({2^2 \times 31}\right)\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^3 \times 3 \times 11 \times 29 \times 31\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 237 \, 336\)









$\blacksquare$


Historical Note
This result is reported by David Wells in his Curious and Interesting Numbers, 2nd ed. of $1997$ as the work of Stephane Vandemergel, but details are lacking.
