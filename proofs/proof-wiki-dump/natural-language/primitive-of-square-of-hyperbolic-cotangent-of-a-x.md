Theorem
$\ds \int \coth^2 a x \rd x = x - \frac {\coth a x} a + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \coth^2 x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x - \coth x + C\)





Primitive of $\coth^2 x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \coth^2 a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {a x - \coth a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x - \frac {\coth a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
