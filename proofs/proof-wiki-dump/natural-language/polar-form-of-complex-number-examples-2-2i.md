Example of Polar Form of Complex Number
The complex number $2 - 2 i$ can be expressed as a complex number in polar form as $\polar {2 \sqrt 2, \dfrac {7 \pi} 4}$.


Proof













\(\ds \cmod {2 - 2 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {2^2 + \paren {-2}^2}\)





Definition of Complex Modulus














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt {2 \times 4}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \sqrt 2\)










Then:














\(\ds \map \cos {\map \arg {2 - 2 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac 2 {2 \sqrt 2}\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sqrt 2} 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {2 - 2 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\pi} 4 \text { or } \dfrac {7 \pi} 4\)





Cosine of $45 \degrees$, Cosine of $315 \degrees$


















\(\ds \map \sin {\map \arg {2 - 2 i} }\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-2} {2 \sqrt 2}\)





Definition of Argument of Complex Number














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {\sqrt 2} 2\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \arg {2 - 2 i}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {5 \pi} 4 \text { or } \dfrac {7 \pi} 4\)





Sine of $225 \degrees$, Sine of $315 \degrees$




Hence:

$\map \arg {2 - 2 i} = \dfrac {7 \pi} 4$
and hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
