Theorem
Let $\struct {G, \circ}$ be a group.
Let $g \in G$.
Let $m, n \in \N_{>0}$.
Then:

$\forall m, n \in \N_{>0}: g^n \circ g^m = g^m \circ g^n$


Proof
By definition, a group is also a semigroup.
The result follows as a special case of Powers of Semigroup Element Commute
$\blacksquare$
