Theorem
Let $L_1 = \struct{S_1, \preceq_1}, L_2 = \struct{S_2, \preceq_2}$ be frames.
Let $f : L_1 \to L_2$ be a frame homomorphism.

Then there exists a Galois connection $g = \tuple {\upperadjoint g, \loweradjoint g}$:

the lower adjoint $\loweradjoint g = f$
Proof
By definition of frame homomorphism:

$f$ is arbitrary join preserving
From All Suprema Preserving Mapping is Lower Adjoint of Galois Connection:

there exists a Galois connection $g = \tuple {\upperadjoint g, \loweradjoint g} : \loweradjoint g = f$
$\blacksquare$
