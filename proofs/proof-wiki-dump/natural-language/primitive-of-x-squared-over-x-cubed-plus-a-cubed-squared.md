Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {x^2 \rd x} {\paren {x^3 + a^3}^2} = \frac {-1} {3 \paren {x^3 + a^3} } + C$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^3 + a^3\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3 x^2\)





Derivative of Power








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {x^2 \rd x} {\paren {x^3 + a^3}^2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\d z} {3 z^2}\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} {3 z} + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {-1} {3 \paren {x^3 + a^3} } + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
