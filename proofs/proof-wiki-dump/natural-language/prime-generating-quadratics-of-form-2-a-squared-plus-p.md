Theorem
The quadratic form:

$2 a^2 + p$
yields prime numbers for $a = 0, 1, \ldots, p - 1$ for values of $p$:

$3, 5, 11, 29$

When $x = p$ we have:














\(\ds 2 p^2 + p\)

\(=\)







\(\ds p \left({2 p + 1}\right)\)









which has divisors $p$ and $2 p + 1$ and so is not prime.
