Corollary to Primitive of Cosine Function
$\ds \int \cos a x \rd x = \frac {\sin a x} a + C$
where $a$ is a non-zero constant.


Proof













\(\ds \int \cos x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sin x + C\)





Primitive of $\cos x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \cos a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {\sin a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin a x} a + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
