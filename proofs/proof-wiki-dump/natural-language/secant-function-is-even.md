Theorem
Let $x \in \R$ be a real number.
Let $\sec x$ be the secant of $x$.

Then, whenever $\sec x$ is defined:

$\map \sec {-x} = \sec x$
That is, the secant function is even.


Proof













\(\ds \map \sec {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\map \cos {-x} }\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\cos x}\)





Cosine Function is Even














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sec x\)





Secant is Reciprocal of Cosine



$\blacksquare$
