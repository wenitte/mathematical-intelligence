Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^m a x \cos^n a x} = \frac 1 {a \paren {n - 1} \sin^{m - 1} a x \cos^{n - 1} a x} + \frac {m + n - 2} {n - 1} \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^m a x \cos^{n - 2} a x}$


Proof













\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^m a x \cos^n a x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\sin^{-m} a x \rd x} {\cos^n a x}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\sin^{-m + 1} a x} {a \paren {n - 1} \cos^{n - 1} a x} - \frac {-m - n + 2} {n - 1} \int \frac {\sin^{-m} a x} {\cos^{n - 2} a x} \rd x + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac {\sin^m a x} {\cos^n a x}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {a \paren {n - 1} \sin^{m - 1} a x \cos^{n - 1} a x} + \frac {m + n - 2} {n - 1} \int \frac {\d x} {\sin^m a x \cos^{n - 2} a x} + C\)









$\blacksquare$
