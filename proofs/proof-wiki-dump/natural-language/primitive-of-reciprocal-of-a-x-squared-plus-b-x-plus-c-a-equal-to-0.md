Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\d x} {a x^2 + b x + c} = \frac 1 b \ln \size {b x + c} + C$
when $a = 0$.


Proof













\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {a x^2 + b x + c}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \frac {\d x} {b x + c}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 b \ln \size {b x + c} + C\)





Primitive of $\dfrac 1 {a x + b}$



$\blacksquare$
