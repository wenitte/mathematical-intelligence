Theorem
$\cos^{-1} x = \pm \, i \cosh^{-1} x$


Proof













\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos^{-1} x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \cos y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Definition of Inverse Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \cos {\pm \, y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Cosine Function is Even








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \cosh {\pm \, i y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x\)





Cosine in terms of Hyperbolic Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \pm \, i y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cosh^{-1} x\)





Definition of Inverse Hyperbolic Cosine








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds y\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm \, i \cosh^{-1} x\)





multiplying both sides by $\pm \, i$



$\blacksquare$
