Theorem
Let $r, s \in \Z$ be integers.
Let $a, b \in \Z$ such that $a$ is congruent modulo $r s$ to $b$, that is:

$a \equiv b \pmod {r s}$

Then:

$a \equiv b \pmod r$
and:

$a \equiv b \pmod s$


Proof













\(\ds a\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds b\)

\(\ds \pmod {r s}\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a - b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds q r s\)





Definition of Congruence Modulo Integer








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds a - b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {q r} s\)


















\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds a - b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {q s} r\)




















\(\ds a\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds b\)

\(\ds \pmod r\)



Definition of Congruence Modulo Integer: $q s$ is an integer












\(\, \ds \land \, \)

\(\ds a\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds b\)

\(\ds \pmod s\)



Definition of Congruence Modulo Integer: $q r$ is an integer



$\blacksquare$
