Example of Derivative of Composite Function
$\map {\dfrac \d {\d x} } {\map \sin {x^2} } = 2 x \map \cos {x^2}$


Proof
Let $y = x^2$.
Let $z = \sin y$.
Then we have:

$z = \map \sin {x^2}$
and so:














\(\ds \dfrac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\d z} {\d y} \dfrac {\d y} {\d x}\)





Derivative of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos y \cdot 2 x\)





Derivative of Sine Function, Derivative of Square Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 x \map \cos {x^2}\)





simplification



$\blacksquare$
