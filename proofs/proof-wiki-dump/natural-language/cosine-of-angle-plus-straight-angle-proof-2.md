Theorem
$\map \cos {x + \pi} = -\cos x$


Proof













\(\ds \map \cos {x + \pi}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {\map \cos {x + \pi} + i \, \map \sin {x + \pi} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {e^{i \paren {x + \pi} } }\)





Euler's Formula














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {e^{i x + i \pi} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {e^{i x} e^{i \pi} }\)





Exponential of Sum: Complex Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \map \Re {-e^{i x} }\)





Euler's Identity














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map \Re {e^{i x} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map \Re {\cos x + i \cos x}\)





Euler's Formula














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\cos x\)









$\blacksquare$
