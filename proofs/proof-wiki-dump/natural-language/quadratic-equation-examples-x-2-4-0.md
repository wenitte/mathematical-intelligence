Example of Quadratic Equation
The quadratic equation:

$x^2 + 4 = 0$
has the wholly imaginary roots:

$x = \pm 2 i$
where $i = \sqrt {-1}$ is the imaginary unit.


Proof
From the Quadratic Formula:














\(\ds x^2 + 4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 0\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {-0 \pm \sqrt {0^2 - 4 \times 1 \times 4} } {2 \times 1}\)





Quadratic Formula














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {\pm \sqrt {-4} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \pm 2 i\)





Definition of Imaginary Unit



$\blacksquare$
