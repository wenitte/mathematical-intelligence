Theorem
$\ds \int \sec a x \rd x = \frac 1 a \ln \size {\sec a x + \tan a x} + C$
where $\sec a x + \tan a x \ne 0$.


Proof













\(\ds \int \sec x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln \size {\sec x + \tan x}\)





Primitive of $\sec x$: Secant plus Tangent Form








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \sec a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \ln \size {\sec a x + \tan a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple



$\blacksquare$
