Theorem
Let $a, b \in \Z$ be integers.
The greatest common divisor of $a$ and $b$ is at least $1$:

$\forall a, b \in \Z_{\ne 0}: \gcd \set {a, b} \ge 1$


Proof
From One Divides all Integers:

$\forall a, b \in \Z: 1 \divides a \land 1 \divides b$
and so:

$1 \le \gcd \set {a, b}$
as required.
$\blacksquare$
