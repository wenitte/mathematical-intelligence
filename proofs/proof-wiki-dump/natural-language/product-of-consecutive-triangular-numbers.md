Theorem
$2 T_n T_{n - 1} = T_{n^2 - 1}$
where $T_n$ denotes the $n$th triangular number.


Proof













\(\ds 2 T_n T_{n - 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2 \paren {\frac {n \paren {n + 1} } 2} \paren {\frac {\paren {n - 1} n} 2}\)





Closed Form for Triangular Numbers














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {n^2 + n} \paren {n^2 - n} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {n^4 - n^2} 2\)





Difference of Two Squares














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {n^2 \paren {n^2 - 1} } 2\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds T_{n^2 - 1}\)





Closed Form for Triangular Numbers



$\blacksquare$
