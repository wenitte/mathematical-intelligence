Theorem
$\ds \int \frac {\ln^n x} x \rd x = \frac {\ln^{n + 1} x} {n + 1} + C$


Proof













\(\ds z\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d z} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 x\)





Derivative of Natural Logarithm








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \frac {\ln^n x} x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int z^n \rd z\)





Integration by Substitution














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {z^{n + 1} } {n + 1} + C\)





Primitive of Power














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\ln^{n + 1} x} {n + 1} + C\)





substituting for $z$



$\blacksquare$
