Example of Roots of Complex Number
The complex $6$th roots of $-27 i$ are given by:

$\paren {-27 i}^{1/6} = \set {\sqrt 3 \, \map \cis {45 + 60 k} \degrees}$
for $k = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$.

That is:








\(\ds k = 0: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_1\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 45 \degrees\)














\(\ds k = 1: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_2\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 105 \degrees\)














\(\ds k = 2: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_3\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 165 \degrees\)














\(\ds k = 3: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 225 \degrees\)














\(\ds k = 4: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_5\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 285 \degrees\)














\(\ds k = 5: \ \ \)





\(\ds z = z_6\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3 \cis 345 \degrees\)











Proof


Let $z^6 = -27 i$.
We have that:

$z^6 = 27 \, \map \cis {\dfrac {3 \pi} 2  + 2 k \pi}$

Let $z = r \cis \theta$.
Then:














\(\ds z^6\)

\(=\)







\(\ds r^6 \cis 6 \theta\)





De Moivre's Theorem














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 27 \, \map \cis {\dfrac {3 \pi} 2 + 2 k \pi}\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds r^6\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 27\)




















\(\ds 6 \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac {3 \pi} 2 + 2 k \pi\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds r\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 27^{1/6}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \sqrt 3\)




















\(\ds \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \dfrac \pi 4 + \dfrac {k \pi} 3\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$














\(\ds \theta\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 45 \degrees + k \times 60 \degrees\)





for $k = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$



$\blacksquare$
