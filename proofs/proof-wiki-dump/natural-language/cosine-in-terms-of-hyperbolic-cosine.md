Theorem
Let $z \in \C$ be a complex number.
Then:

$\cos z = \map \cosh {i z}$
where:

$\cos$ denotes the complex cosine
$\cosh$ denotes the hyperbolic cosine
$i$ is the imaginary unit: $i^2 = -1$.


Proof













\(\ds \map \cosh {i z}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {e^{i z} + e^{-i z} } 2\)





Definition of Hyperbolic Cosine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \cos z\)





Euler's Cosine Identity



$\blacksquare$
