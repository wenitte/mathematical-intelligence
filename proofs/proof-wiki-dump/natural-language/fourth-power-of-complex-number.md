Theorem
Let $z = a + i b$ be a complex number.
Then its fourth power is given by:

$z^4 = a^4 - 6 a^2 b^2 + b^4 + i \paren {4 a^3 b - 4 a b^3}$


Proof













\(\ds z^4\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \paren {a + i b}^4\)





by hypothesis














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4 + 4 a^3 \paren {i b} + 6 a^2 \paren {i b}^2 + 4 a \paren {i b}^3 + \paren {i b}^4\)





Fourth Power of Sum














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4 + 4 i a^3 b + 6 i^2 a^2 b^2 + 4 i^3 a b^3 + i^4 b^4\)





Complex Multiplication is Commutative














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4 + 4 i a^3 b - 6 a^2 b^2 - 4 i a b^3 + b^4\)





Definition of Imaginary Unit














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds a^4 - 6 a^2 b^2 + b^4 + i \paren {4 a^3 b - 4 a b^3}\)





gathering like terms



$\blacksquare$
