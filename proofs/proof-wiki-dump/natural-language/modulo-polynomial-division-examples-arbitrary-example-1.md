Example of Modulo Polynomial Division
Let:














\(\ds \map f x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^3 - x^2 - 1\)




















\(\ds \map g x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x + 1\)










Then $\map f x$ divided by $\map g x$ modulo $3$ is:

$x^2 + x - 1$


Proof













\(\ds \paren {x + 1} \paren {x^2 + x - 1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x^3 + 2 x^2 - 1\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds x^3 - x^2 - 1\)

\(\ds \pmod 3\)







$\blacksquare$
