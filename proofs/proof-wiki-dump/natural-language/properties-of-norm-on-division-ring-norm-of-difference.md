Theorem
Let $\struct {R, +, \circ}$ be a division ring.
Let $\norm {\,\cdot\,}$ be a norm on $R$.
Let $x, y \in R$.

Then:

$\norm {x - y} \le \norm x + \norm y$


Proof
Then:














\(\ds \norm {x - y}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm {x + \paren {-y} }\)




















\(\ds \)

\(\le\)







\(\ds \norm x + \norm {-y}\)





Norm Axiom $\text N 3$: Triangle Inequality














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \norm x + \norm y\)





Norm of Ring Negative



as desired.
$\blacksquare$
