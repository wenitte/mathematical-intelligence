Theorem
$\ds \int \map F {\ln x} \rd x = \int \map F u e^u \rd u$
where $u = \ln x$.


Proof













\(\ds u\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \ln x\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \frac {\d u} {\d x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 x\)





Derivative of Natural Logarithm Function








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \map F {\ln x} \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \map F u x \rd u\)





Primitive of Composite Function














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \int \map F u e^u \rd u\)





Definition of Exponential Function



$\blacksquare$
