Theorem
$\ds \int \ln a x \rd x = x \ln a x - x + C$


Proof













\(\ds \int \ln x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \ln x - x + C\)





Primitive of $\ln x$








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \int \ln a x \rd x\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 a \paren {a x \ln a x - a x} + C\)





Primitive of Function of Constant Multiple














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds x \ln a x - x + C\)





simplifying



$\blacksquare$
