Example of Lucas-Carmichael Number
$935$ is a Lucas-Carmichael number:

$p \divides 935 \implies \paren {p + 1} \divides 936$


Proof
We have that:

$935 = 5 \times 11 \times 17$
Then:














\(\ds 936\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 6 \times 156\)





so $\paren {5 + 1} \divides \paren {935 + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 12 \times 78\)





so $\paren {11 + 1} \divides \paren {935 + 1}$














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 18 \times 52\)





so $\paren {17 + 1} \divides \paren {935 + 1}$



$\blacksquare$
