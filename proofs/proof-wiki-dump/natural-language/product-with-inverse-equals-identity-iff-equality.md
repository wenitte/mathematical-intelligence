Theorem
Let $\struct {G, \circ}$ be a group whose identity element is $e$.
Then:

$\forall a, b \in G: a \circ b^{-1} = e \iff a = b$


Proof
Using various properties of groups:















\(\ds a \circ b^{-1}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e\)














\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds \paren {a \circ b^{-1} } \circ b\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e \circ b\)





Cancellation Laws








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a \circ \paren {b^{-1} \circ b}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds e \circ b\)





Group Axiom $\text G 1$: Associativity








\(\ds \leadstoandfrom \ \ \)





\(\ds a\)

\(=\)







\(\ds b\)





Group Axiom $\text G 2$: Existence of Identity Element and Group Axiom $\text G 3$: Existence of Inverse Element



$\blacksquare$
