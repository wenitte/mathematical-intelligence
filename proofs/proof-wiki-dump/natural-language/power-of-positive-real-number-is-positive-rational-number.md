Theorem
Let $x \in \R_{>0}$ be a (strictly) positive real number.
Let $q \in \Q$ be a rational number.

Then:

$x^q > 0$
where $x^q$ denotes the $x$ to the power of $q$.


Proof
Let $q = \dfrac r s$, where $r \in \Z$, $s \in \Z \setminus \set 0$.
Then:














\(\ds x > 0\)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds x^r > 0\)





Power of Positive Real Number is Positive: Integer














\(\ds \)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds \sqrt [s] {\paren {x^r} } > 0\)





Existence of Positive Root of Positive Real Number














\(\ds \)

\(\leadsto\)







\(\ds x^{r / s}\)





Definition of Rational Power



Hence the result.
$\blacksquare$
