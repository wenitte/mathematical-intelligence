Theorem
Let $S$ and $T$ be sets.
Let $f: S \to T$ be a bijection.
Let $f^{-1}: T \to S$ be the inverse of $f$.

Then the domain of $f^{-1}$ equals the codomain of $f$.


Proof
Follows directly from the definition of domain and codomain:

$\Dom f = S$ and $\Cdm f = T$
$\Dom {f^{-1} } = T$ and $\Cdm {f^{-1} } = S$
That is:

$\Dom {f^{-1} } = T = \Cdm f$
$\blacksquare$
