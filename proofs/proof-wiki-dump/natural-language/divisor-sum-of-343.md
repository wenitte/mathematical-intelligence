Example of Divisor Sum of Power of Prime
$\map {\sigma_1} {343} = 400$
where $\sigma_1$ denotes the divisor sum function.


Proof
From Divisor Sum of Power of Prime:

$\map {\sigma_1} {p^k} = \dfrac {p^{k + 1} - 1} {p_i - 1}$

We have that:

$343 = 7^3$

Hence:














\(\ds \map {\sigma_1} {343}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {7^4 - 1} {7 - 1}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {2400} 6\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 400\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 20^2\)










Thus we have that:

$7^0 + 7^2 + 7^2 + 7^3 = 20^2$
$\blacksquare$
