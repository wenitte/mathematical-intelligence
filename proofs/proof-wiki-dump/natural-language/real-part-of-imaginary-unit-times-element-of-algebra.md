Theorem
Let $\struct {A, \ast}$ be a $\ast$-algebra over $\C$.
Let $x \in A$.

Then:

$\map \Re {i x} = -\map \Im x$
where $\Re$ and $\Im$ denote the real and imaginary parts of $A$.


Proof
We have:














\(\ds \map \Re {i x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {\paren {i x} + \paren {i x}^\ast} 2\)





Definition of Real Part of Element of *-Algebra














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac {i x - i x^\ast} 2\)





$(\text C^\ast 4)$ from Definition of Involution on Algebra














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds i^2 \frac {x - x^\ast} {2 i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\frac {x - x^\ast} {2 i}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\map \Im x\)





Definition of Imaginary Part of Element of *-Algebra



$\blacksquare$
