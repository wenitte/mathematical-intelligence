Example of Common Factor Cancelling in Congruence
We have that:

$6 \equiv 12 \pmod 2$
but:

$3 \not \equiv 6 \pmod 2$


Proof













\(\ds 6\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 12\)

\(\ds \pmod 2\)












\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 2 \times 3\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 2 \times 6\)

\(\ds \pmod 2\)












\(\ds \not \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 3\)

\(\equiv\)







\(\ds 6\)

\(\ds \pmod 2\)



as $\gcd \set {2, 6} \ne 1$



$\blacksquare$
