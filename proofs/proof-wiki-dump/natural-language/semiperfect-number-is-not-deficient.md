Theorem
Let $n \in \Z_{>0}$ be a semiperfect number.

Then $n$ is not deficient.


Proof
Let $n$ be semiperfect.
Then by definition, the sum of the aliquot parts of $n$ is not less than $n$.
The result follows by definition of deficient.
$\blacksquare$
