Example of Use of GCD from Prime Decomposition
The greatest common divisor of $64$ and $81$ is:

$\gcd \set {64, 81} = 1$


Proof













\(\ds 64\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^6\)




















\(\ds 81\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 3^4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds 64\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^6 \times 3^0\)




















\(\ds 81\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^4\)














\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \gcd \set {64, 81}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds 2^0 \times 3^0\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds 1\)









$\blacksquare$
