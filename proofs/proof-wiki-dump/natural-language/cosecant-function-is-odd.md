Theorem
Let $x \in \R$ be a real number.
Let $\csc x$ be the cosecant of $x$.

Then, whenever $\csc x$ is defined:

$\map \csc {-x} = -\csc x$
That is, the cosecant function is odd.


Proof













\(\ds \map \csc {-x}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {\map \sin {-x} }\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds \frac 1 {-\sin x}\)





Sine Function is Odd














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\csc x\)





Cosecant is Reciprocal of Sine



$\blacksquare$
