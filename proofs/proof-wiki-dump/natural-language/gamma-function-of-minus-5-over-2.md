Theorem
$\map \Gamma {-\dfrac 5 2} = -\dfrac {8 \sqrt \pi} {15}$
where $\Gamma$ denotes the Gamma function.


Proof













\(\ds \map \Gamma {-\dfrac 3 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 5 2 \map \Gamma {-\dfrac 5 2}\)





Gamma Difference Equation








\(\ds \leadsto \ \ \)





\(\ds \map \Gamma {-\dfrac 5 2}\)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 2 5 \map \Gamma {-\dfrac 3 2}\)




















\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac 2 5 \paren {\dfrac {4 \sqrt \pi} 3}\)





Gamma Function of Minus 3 over 2














\(\ds \)

\(=\)







\(\ds -\dfrac {8 \sqrt \pi} {15}\)









$\blacksquare$
